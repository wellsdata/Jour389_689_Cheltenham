CHELTENHAM ISSUE
PUT UP TO O'CONOR
Attorney General Asked To
Decide Legality Of Salaries
For Board Members
Also Must Prepare Answer
To Show-Cause Order
On Employes' Pay
The next steps in the controversy
over Cheltenham School for Boys,
Negro reformatory, were put up to
Herbert R. O'Conor, Attorney General,
yesterday.
He was asked by Harry C. Jones.
State Employment Commissioner, to
rule on whether or not it is legal for
four members of the board of man-
agers to draw salaries from the State
for services performed for the insti-
tution.

Court Order Pending
Also, he was handed an order of the
Circuit Court of Prince George's;
County asking the State Employment):
Commissioner to show cause why he
should not approve pay for twenty-
four employes of the institution the
commissioner has ruled are unfit for
the State service.
As counsel for all State departments
Mr. O'Conor must give Mr. Jones an
opinion on the first point and prepare
a court answer to the mandamus pro-
ceedings brought on behalf of the
employes by the institution's board of
managers.
Four Salaried Members
The four members of the board
drawing salaries are:
Joseph H. Blandford, president, $600
annually; William H. Gibbons, part-
time physician, $600 annually; John E.
McCormack, financial secretary, $1,200
plus maintenance, and Frederick
Douglas, Negro parole officer, $900 an-
nually.
