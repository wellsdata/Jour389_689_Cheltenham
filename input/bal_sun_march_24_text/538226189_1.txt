The attention of the people of Baltimore
should be directed to the subject dealing
with colored children. The colored people
as a rule go from their homes each day
to work and thousands of children are
left during the day to run the streets un-
-cared for. The result of this condition
will be an ever-increasing number of
criminals and undesirable citizens. There
is urgent need for an industrial school for
colored children on the order of St. Mary's
Industrial School. The institution for col-
ored boys at Cheltenham is said to be well
conducted, but another nearer the city is
needed. Liberal provision for preventing
the increase of the number of criminals
is true economy.
RESCUE OF COLORED CHILDREN.
One of the most beneficent of the chari-
.table institutions in this city is St. Eliza-
beth's Home for Colored Children, on St.
Paul street. It is under the charge of
The Franciscan Sisters, and is at once
an infant asylum and industrial, school
orphan and abandoned colored chil-
dren. It is probably the only institution
of its kind in the United States. Here
in Baltimore it does a splendid charity
among a class of persons by whom it is
especially needed. No abandoned colored
infant or child or indigent orphan child is
ever denied admission if room for it can
be found. Mother Mary Mildred, the
president and head of the home, indeed,
has a heart too big for the building.. She
