CHELTENHAM
BOARD HEAD
QUITS POST
Rev. F. R. Isaac Feels His
Efforts Frustrated; T.B.
Sprague Named
| The Rev. E. Reid Isaac has re-
signed from the board of managers
of the Cheltenham School for Boys,
it was learned yesterday. partly
because he felt "frustrated" in his
efforts as president of the board
and believed he was "getting no-
where."
He said he asked Governor
O'Conor to relieve him of his duties
in connection with the school for
delinquent Negro youths so that he
might devote full time to his posi
tion as general superintendent of
the Board of Child Care of the
Methodist Church.
Concerned Over New Site
Explaining that he had found
work in the Cheltenham board
time-consuming and that it had
placed him under considerable
strain, he said:
"I seemed to be working all the
time and getting nowhere."
The Cheltenham board recently
has been particularly concerned
with seeking a site for the proposed
new school and with coping with
the immediate need for closer su-
pervision of chronic runaways at
the school.
Thomas B. Sprague Named
One of the school's cottages
currently is being converted for
use as a security unit.
Thomas B. Sprague, who served
on the January (1945) term of
the Baltimore grand jury, was
elected president of the board
Monday. He had been appointed
to the board last Thursday, when
the Rev. Mr. Isaac's resignation
became effective.
