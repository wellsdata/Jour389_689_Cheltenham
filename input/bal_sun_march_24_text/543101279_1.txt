Seeks To Fix Blame
For Beating Of Boys
Employment Commissioner Places
Charges Against Cottage Mas-
ter At Cheltenham
An effort to determine who is re-
sponsible for the alleged beating of
inmates at the Cheltenham School for
Colored Boys will be made by Harry
C. Jones, State Employment Commis-
sioner,
Mr. Jones yesterday placed charges
against Andrew Beckwith, a' cottage
master at the school, for allegedly
assaulting two Negro boys, about 14
years old, when they tusseled over a
bed in the cell to which they were
confined. Beckwith has five days to
answer the charges.
Mr. Jones explained he wanted to
find out whether such beatings were
wanton acts of individual employes at
the institution, or on orders from the
superintendent, B. B. Sunderland. If
the beating of inmates is Sunderland's
policy, then Beckwith cannot be
blamed, Mr. Jones said.
