CHELTENHAM
FUNDAPPROVED
$128,300 Provided For Con-
struction Plan At School
Annapolis, Oct. 7 (AP)
The
Maryland Board of Public Works
today approved an emergency con-
struction plan for Cheltenham
School for Negro Boys to cost ap-
proximately $128,300.
The board provided $75,000 from
money turned back to the State by
the State Planning Commission
and authorized the expenditure of
other funds available to the, institu-
tion:
In other action the board:.
1. Adopted a standard of archi-
tects' fees for work performed for
the State.
Rosewood Request Rejected
2. Rejected a request from Rose-
wood Training School for Girls for
approximately $8,000 to provide a
bus and station wagon and $9,132
for annual operation and mainte-
nance to accommodate their per-
sonnel.
3. 'Gave the go-ahead to com-
plete negotiations for the Solomons
Island amphibious base in Calvert
county for use of the Board of
Natural Resources.
4. Approved spending up to
$21,000 to eliminate the backlog of
work in the Department of Motor
Vehicles,
The action on Cheltenham was
taken after consideration of a re-
port, submitted by John B. Funk,
State engineer. The report was
drawn up after visits of a various
State officials to the institution.
Improvement Noted
The emergency program for the
school-which was chosen instead
of a long-range relocation plan-
was adopted after Governor Lane
read a report showing there was
some improvement in the operation
of the institution since Calvin L.
Kiah, 36-year-old war veteran, had
taken charge.
Mr. Funk's report stated: "It is
suggested that, no matter what re-
pairs are affected in the institution,
and regardless of what new build-
ings and accommodations are pro-
vided, the value of such improve-
ments will be worthless if staff and
adminstration officials of the insti-
tution do not organize Cheltenham
and run it in such manner as to
keep it clean and habitable."
The report recommended that
the present dining hall, kitchen and
food-handling facilities be aban-
doned in the present' administra-
tion building..
New Use Of Structure
The structure would be con-
verted in part to living space for
the staff; the dining room would be
made into office space; the laundry
retained but adequately lighted and
provided with steam exhaust, and
fumigation and rodent control
would be carried out.
Cottages would be generally re-
paired, and changes made in the
heating system..
A new dining room and kitchen
would be constructed of concrete
blocks and of a type that could be
improved later, when more money
was available. The basement of this
building would be used also as a
pasteurization plant and a food
storehouse.
Architects' Fees Set
The architects' fee standard was
set after proposals were submitted
by the Maryland Chapter of the
American Institute of Architects.
Hereafter the pay will range from
6 to 3 per cent contract fees on
simpler types of building; 10 to
61½ per cent on specialized work,
and 7 to 6 per cent on the type of
plans most frequently drawn.
On all types of building plans,
the State will pay the highest rate
for the first unit of any group and
3 per cent on any identical plan.
Under the permit and sale of the
Solomon's base as proposed by the
Navy; the State would be required
to maintain certain buildings on
the property and could buy others
for a price up to $5,000.
If the State failed to comply
with the Navy's conditions, the
Federal Government could take
_ over the property.
State Labor To Be Used
Mr. Funk said state labor would
be used to dismantle the property
of over 100 bulldings in order that
the materials may be used in other
state repairs or construction.
The Department of Motor Vehi-
cles will have a clean-up of its
backlog of work by January 1 un-
der the $21,000 program approved
by the board.
W. Lee Elgin, motor vehicles
commissioner had asked the board
for $51,736 from the emergency
fund to take care of office work
which needs to be done in the
bureau.
Aftera study of the bureau,
Griffenhagen and Associates rec-
ommended new procedures be in-
stituted and that the back work be
accomplished speedily.
Under the plan approved $9,000
would go to the International Busi-
ness Machine Company, $6,000 to a
"task force" of workers and $6,000
to Griffenhagen, whose officials
would supervise the job and pre-
pare procedures for the entire de-
partment to use in the future.
