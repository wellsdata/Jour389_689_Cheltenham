Members of the board of man-
agers of the Cheltenham School for
Boys and the State Department of
Welfare, acting jointly, have been
instructed by the Board of Public
Works to find a new site for an in-
stitution for delinquent Negro
juveniles, Governor O'Conor dis-
closed yesterday.
Construction of a new institu-
tion, however, normally could not
start for more than a year, since
an appropriation through a bond
issue must be made by the Legisla-
ture, which does not convene until
next January, and the new fiscal
year in which the bonds normally
would be issued will not begin until
June 30, 1945. The Legislature
could provide for the beginning of
work next spring if it made the
bond issue an emergency measure.
To Be Fitted For Defectives
Meanwhile, the Governor re-
vealed, the present institutional
buildings at Cheltenham in Prince
George's county would be
modeled, to some extent, and be
turned over to the Board of Mental
Hygiene for the housing of Negro
defective delinquents.
re-
The Board of Public Works sev-
eral days ago conferred with repre-
sentatives of the architectural firm
of Ross and Walton, of Hyattsville,
which was engaged several months
ago to plan repairs at the present
institution.
Nothing has been done, architec-
turally, with respect to the pro-
posed new institution for non-
defective delinquents, the Gov-.
ernor said.
Authorized By Legislature
Construction of the new correc-
tional institution and the utiliza-
tion of the present building group
for defective juveniles was pro-
vided for in the revision of the
laws with respect to handling juve-
nile delinquency which were adopt-
ed at the 1943 session of the Legis-
lature.
The plans submitted by the
Hyattsville architects for repairs
at Cheltenham include a rearrange-
ment and reconstruction of dining
halls and sanitary facilities. The
Board of Public Works instructed
the architects to lay the details
before the Board of Mental Hy-
giene, since that agency will take
over the institution when it is con-
verted to a mental-correctional in-
stitution.
