JURORS URGE
EARLY CLOSING
FOR ALL BARS
May-Term Body Would
Also Prohibit Liquor
Sales On Sunday
Closing of bars at midnight on
weekdays and all day Sunday was
advocated yesterday by the retiring
May-term grand jury in a report to
the judges of the Criminal Court.
The proposed restrictions, it was
explained, would apply to all types
of establishments selling alcoholic
beverages.
The jurors said they had ob-
served minors visiting taverns and
being sold liquor. The investigating
body also contended that it had
seen card-playing for money in
such places.
Advocating that all bars be
closed at midnight and not be re-
opened until 8 o'clock the next
morning-two hours later than the
present law permits-and that they
remain closed all day Sunday, the
grand jury said it observed that
most disturbances occur after mid-
night.
Criticize Jails, Hawkers
The jurors also complained of
overcrowding and idleness in the
State's adult penal institutions.
urged the elimination of sidewalk
hawkers on downtown streets and
suggested that parents exert them.
selves in the care and raising of
their children to prevent both juve-
nile and parental delinquency.
The jurors further urged that a
line be drawn on the floor near the
front of streetcars and busses and
that passengers be required to re-
main behind that line, thus afford-
ing the operator clear vision to his
right and freedom to operate the
vehicle.
Painting of the city markets also
was proposed by the grand jurors,
who pointed out that visitors usual
ly make it a point to visit those
landmarks. particularly Lexington
Market.
Cheltenham Needs Cited
The reports said many improve-
ments have been made at the
Cheltenham School in recent
months, but declared that one of
the major problems there is the
lack of trained personnel. There
also is a lack of equipment for:
training boys in the trades, the
jurors said.
If the school is to serve as a cor.
rective institution, boys must
leave with more knowledge of how
they can go about making an honest
daily living, the jurors declared.
The jury had words of praise
for the Baltimore Police Depart-
ment and for the State's attorney's
office.
Myles Katz, foreman, in his re-
port. urged citizens to support a
$8,000,000 loan for public build-
ings because part of the money is
to be used for a 300-bed tuber-
culosis hospital at the City Hos-
pitals.
