HOPKINS MEN CHOSE THEM
Debating Teams And Hullabaloo
Staff Named.
The speakers on the debating teams of
the senior and junior classes at the Johns
Hopkins University for the interclass de-
bate to be held in McCoy Hall the early
part of March have been chosen by Dr.
John C. French, instructor in English and
debating. The teams are as follows:
Seniors-Messrs. Arthur H. Mann, Rober Macau-
lay and Albert Hutzler.
Juniors-Messrs, W. Clyde Marvel, Allan H.
Fishor and Isador Blum.

RELICS USED AT ALBAUGH'S
Historic Furnishings In "The Cow-
boy And The Lady."
Some of the stage furnishings used in
""The Cowboy and the Lady" this week at
Albaugh's are of historic value. These
were loaned to the management by Mr.
Charles Gay, of the Fawcett company, who
lived in the West many years.
The pistol which Mr. Gay carries as
Dave, one of the cowboys, is the weapon,
says Mr. Gay, with which Bob Ford shot
Jesse James; the spurs were worn by
Calamity Jane, who acted as scout and
guide for General Crook, and who was well
known on account of her remarkable fear-
lessness and daring. She was a friend of
Mr. Gay's, and gave him the spurs. The
"chaps," or leggins, were once the property
of Wild Bill.
