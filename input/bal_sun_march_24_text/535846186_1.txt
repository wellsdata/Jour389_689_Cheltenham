PLANS FOR A NEW HOUSE.
The Colored House of Reformation at
Cheltenham Preparing to Erect a
Modern Building.
Messrs. Ghequier & May, architects,
have completed plans for a new building
at the Colored House of Reformation, at
Cheltenham, to replace the building which
was burned several months ago. The new
structure will cost about $8,000.
It will be built of Baltimore sand brick
and will be three stories high, with a
double pitch roof, covered with tin. It
will have a front of 57 feet 6 inches, and
a depth of 34 feet. A wing will be 31
feet 3 inches by 34 feet, also three stories
high. The first story will be 9 feet high,
while the others will be 13 feet high.
On the first floor will be located a play-
room, bathrooms, a cellroom and a work-
room. The second floor will have a sit-
ting room, a bedroom and a schoolroom.
In the wing will be located a workroom
and a storeroom, and also closets for the
storage of the clothing of the boys. The
third floor will have two bedrooms and
two dormitories.
The building, together with the old
buildings, will be equipped with modern
plumbing arrangements. Running water
will be supplied from a large tank. Bath
rooms and lavatories will be placed in a
circular building, to be erected in the
corner created by the wingand main build-
Ing. They will be equipped with iron
bath tubs and stationary wash stands.
An additional building will be erected
for a plant for heating the buildings by
steam through coils of pipe. The con-
tract for the work will be awarded in a
- few days.
