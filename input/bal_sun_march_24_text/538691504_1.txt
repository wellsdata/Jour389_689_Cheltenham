A complete investigation of the
Cheltenham School for Boys, a Negro
reformatory, by a committee appointed
by Governor Nice was recommended
yesterday by the grand jury.
In a special report filed in Criminal
Court, the jurors, through their fore-
man, C. Delano Ames, criticized the
administrative and physical facilities
of the institution, which was taken
over by the State by a legislative act
in 1937.
Types Of Training Advocated
The investigating committee, the
report said, should be composed of
Negro and white members "from prop-
erly trained institutional executives
who would set up a proper course of
training and management
along
the lines of the Maryland Training
School for Boys at Loch Raven."
The report advocated:
Military training.
Vocational and academic education.
Parole and supervision.
Dietary and sanitation training.
Moral and religious training.
Segregation of communicable dis-
case cases.
Installation of a group of Negro
teachers, cottage masters and in-
stitutional executives under
mixed board of managers, the
members of which would not re-
ceive compensation or hold execu-
tive positions.
a
Lack Of Cooperation Charged
The jury also recommended the im-
mediate adoption of a report made by
Harry C. Jones, State Employment
Commissioner, which said that in-
capable and unfit employes should not
remain on the institution's pay roll.
"From our observations and in-
quiry," the jury report said, "there is
apparently no responsible head of this
institution to whom the other em-
ployes can look for leadership or ad-
vice. There is a very decided lack
of cooperation between the parole de-
partment, vocational training and ex-
ecutive departments of this institution,
and it would appear to use that this
lack of cooperation begins at the top.
In other words, with its board of man-
agers.
Committee On Report
"It would be our recommendation
that if this board is not willing to act
upon the recommendations of the var-
ious departments of the State in
the solution of their difficulties that
they step out or be replaced by the
Governor with a suitable board which
will definitely take the matter to heart
and make a job of it."
The special report was submitted
by a grand jury committee headed by
Alfred D. L'Esperance, and consisting
of W. Emerson Brown, Philip G.
Schwaab, Sr., and George H. Harper.
