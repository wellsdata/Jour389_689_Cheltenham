JOSEPH J. JANNEY.
Funeral services for Joseph J. Jan
ney, 1923 Park avenue, secretary-treas-
urer of the House of Reformation for
Colored Boys at Cheltenham, who died
Tuesday, were held at 3 o'clock yester-
day at Friends' Meeting House, Park
avenue and Laurens street. Burial was
in Friends' Burying Grounds, Harford
road. Those who eulogized the life of
Mr. Janney were Mrs. Pauline Holme,
John R. King, commander of the Mary-
land division, Loyal Legion; Edward
C. Wilson, principal of Friends' School,
and the Rev. Dr. George F. Bragg,
colored, rector of St. James' Colored
Protestant Episcopal Church. A delė-
gation of the Loyal Legion and Board
of Directors of the House of Reforma-
tion attended. The pallbearers were
Enoch Harlan, Isaac Walker, Charles
T. Matthews, Thomas N. Taylor, Ed-
ward C. Wilson and Benjamin B. Sun-
derland.

MRS. SARAH L. HITCHCOCK.
As the result of a fall last week, Mrs.
Sarah Lavenia Hitchcock, 85 years old,
died Wednesday night at the home of
her daughter, Mrs. William A. Ren-
ninger, 5222 Florence avenue, Arlington.
She was the widow of Jesse A. Hitch-
cock.
Mrs. Hitchcock is survived by three
sons-Henry F., John A. and Joseph C.
Hitchcock, and three daughters-Mrs.
Renninger, Mrs. J. J. Moore and Miss
Anna Katherine Hitchcock. The funeral
will be held at 2 o'clock tomorrow after-
noon at the home. The Rev. H. B.
Parsons, pastor of Twenty-fourth Street
Methodist Episcopal Church, assisted by
the Rev. Martin L. Beall, pastor of Grace
Methodist Episcopal Church, will con-
duct the services.
