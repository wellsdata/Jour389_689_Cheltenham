RITES SLATED
FOR H. C. HILL
Pioneer Social Worker Was
Prisoner's Aid Officer
Funeral services for Howard C.
Hill, educator and social worker
who died last Thursday, will be
held at 2 P.M. tomorrow at the
Church of the Holy Nativity, Eger.
ton road and Garrison boulevard,
with the Rev. Hugh W. F. Powers
officiating.
Burial will be in All Hallows
Cemetery, Davidsonville, Anne
Arundel county.
:
Mr. Hill, 68, lived at 3607 Mo-
hawk avenue,
Came Here In 1911
In 1911 Mr. Hill came to Balti-
more as secretary of the Social
Hygiene Society and for 27 years
served as executive secretary of
the Prisoner's Air Society. He was
active in organizing the State
Parole and Probation department.
For twenty years Mr. Hill was an
inspector of county prisons for the
State Board of: Welfare and later
held the same position with the
Department of Correction.
After his retirement last Febru-
ary, Mr. Hill acted as business ad-
ministrator of the Baltimore Insti-
tute of Musical Art, 811 West
Lanvale street,
Cheltenham Board Member
He was a member of the board
of managers of the Cheltenham
School for Boys, a former president
of the Shakespeare Club of Balti-
more and a vestryman of the
Church of the Holy Nativity.
Mr. Hill was born in Frostburg,
Allegany county, the son of the
late Thomas and Elizabeth Mason
Hill. In 1900 he was graduated
from St. John's College, Annapolis,
For several years before coming
here he was principal of the Alle-
gany County High School, Cumber-
land.
He is survived by his wife, Cor-
nelia Houston Lansdale Ilill; five
daughters, Mrs. Edward B. Cagle
and Miss Janet F. Hill, both of Bal-
timore; Mrs. Andrew W. Conaway,
of Carroll county; Mrs. Walter T.
Savage, of Arlington, Va., and Mrs.
Richard F. Amity, of the Canal
Zone, and two sons, Howard P.
Hill, of Los Angeles, and Thomas
