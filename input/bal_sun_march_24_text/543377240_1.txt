Wins Suit For Custody
Of Brother's Children
Mrs. Jessie A. Slater, San Antonio,
Given Award In Circuit Court
At Rockville
Rockville, Md., July 15-At a habeas
corpus hearing before Judge Robert B.
Peter, in Circuit Court here, today,
Mrs. Jessie A. Slater, San Antonio,
Texas, was awarded custody of the
four minor children of her brother,
Rice Phelps, who died suddenly at his
home, Takoma Park, this county,
July 1.
Mrs. A. B. McCormick Hill, with
whom Mr. Phelps and the children
boarded for about two years, sought to
retain the children, declaring on the
witness stand she had instituted pro-
ceedings to adopt them and would
educate them and care for them.
Mrs. Hill, who is an employe of the
Prohibition Bureau, testified she and
Mr. Phelps had planned to get married.
Field Day At Cheltenham
Cheltenham, Md., July 15-Officials
of the Cheltenham House of Reforma-
tion for Colored Boys were hosts to-
day to public officials and private in-
dividuals who had been invited to
witness the annual field day of the
Institution. The program consisted of
baseball and other games, a dress pa-
rade of the inmates and an inspection
of the buildings and premises. Lunch
was served on the grounds at noon.
