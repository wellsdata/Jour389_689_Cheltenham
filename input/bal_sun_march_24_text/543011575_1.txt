3. Members of the Legislative

4. At least six bureaus have

though a resolution was adopted
by the House of Delegates at the

To Start With U. Of Md.
The leadoff budgetary request,
that of the University of Maryland,
is one which Dr. H. C. Byrd, presi-
dent of the university, described
recently as eliciting "silly and
ridiculous misconceptions" on the
part of members of the Legislature.
At a recent meeting of the Legis-
lative Council, Dr. Byrd declined
to discuss the university budget,
except as pertained to the medical
and law schools and then only in
a general fashion.
He told members of the Legis-
lative Council that he wanted to
make a public statement about the
budget before it was submitted
officially either to the council or
to the Legislature.
At the office of Walter N. Kirk-
man, director of the Bureau of
Budget and Procurement. it was
learned that Dr. Byrd has sub-
mitted a total budget request for
$4,044,112.48 for 1944 and 1945.
Special funds, which are gained
from current revenue, were esti-
mated at $2,600.000, and the gen-
eral funds sought from the State
amounted to $1.444.112.48.
Although the House of Delegates
in the last session of the General
Assembly adopted a resolution call-
ing for the submission of all State
department budgets not later than
October 15 of the year in which the
budget was to be drawn, some of
the budgets have not been sub-
mitted to the Bureau of Budget and
Procurement, it was announced
yesterday.
Two of the largest requests will
come from the Bureau of Education
and the State Roads Commission,
it was said.
Causes Of Delay Cited
Delay in the budget of the De-
partment of Education was said to
be because the State school sys-
tem had to determine first how
many students were going to be en-
rolled in the 1944-1945 period, in
addition to determining how many
teachers would be needed because
of either replacements or the need
for new teachers.
As for the State Roads Commis-
sion, it was said at the office of
the Bureau of Budget and Procure-
ment that the budget of that de-
partment was being held up be-
cause final figures had not been
received on the budgetary requests
of such departments as the State
Police Department and the office
of the Commissioner of Motor
Vehicles which depend for revenue
on the income from the gasoline
tax.
