Cheltenham Plans
Governor O'Conor last night
announced that Ingleside, the
Baker estate in Catonsville, will
not be the site of the new Chelten-
ham School for Boys.
The Governor said that condem-
nation proceedings on the property
have been abandoned on the advice
of W. Wallace Lanahan, chairman
of the State Board of Welfare, and
of F. Reid Isaacs, president of the
board of governors of Cheltenham.
Protests against the site began
early this month shortly after the
Governor had anounced that it had
been chosen for the school. A
petition signed by nearly 2,000
residents of the First district of
Baltimore county was recently
forwarded to the Governor by the
Board of County Commissioners.
The Governor did not announce
immediate plans for choosing an
alternate site.
Annapolis Negro Found
Guilty Of Manslaughter
:
Annapolis, Oct. 24 (P)-Tried on
an indictment charging murder,
Robert Forrester, 35, Negro, was
adjudged guilty of manslaughter
by Associate Judge James Clark,
sitting without a jury, in Anne
Arundel County Circuit Court
today.
The accused was charged with
causing the death of Charles W.
Willford, 40, another Negro, which
occurred under unusual circum-
stances.
The two engaged in a quarrel
that led to a fist fight in Annapolis
last September 6, during which
Forrester dealt Willford a heavy
blow that fractured the latter's jaw.
Lockjaw developed and Willford
died twelve' days afterward. Judge
Clark has reserved sentence in the
case.
Catholic Alumnae Plan
32d Annual Meeting
The thirty-second annual con-
ference of the Maryland chapter,
International Federation of Cath-
olic Alumnae, Saturday at the Lord
Baltimore Hotel will have as its
keynote the Pope's appeal to women
to take a more active part in gov-
ernment through politics and elec-
tions, Mrs. R. Harris Cooper, gover-
nor of the chapter, announced yes-
terday.
The Rev. Dr. James A. Hagner,
procurator and assistant secretary-
treasurer of the Catholic University
of America, will be the principal
speaker at the meeting, which will
open with a luncheon at 12.30 P. M.
Gen. Reckord To Speak
Maj. Gen. Milton A. Reckord,
who was provost marshal in the
European theater of war for eight-
een months before the German sur-
render, will speak at a meeting of
the Har Sinai Brotherhood Tues-
day night at the Har Sinai Congre-
gation Center, 6300 Park Heights
avenue.
