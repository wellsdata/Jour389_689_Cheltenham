The fifth annual indoor games given by
the Fifth Regiment at the armory last
night were fairly well attended. While no
records were broken the contests were all
spirited and many of them resulted in close
and exciting finishes.
The entry list of contestants was large,
but there were many entrants who falled to
contest.
While all of the events were entertain-
ing the gallery was most pleased with the
relay racing.
Several of the officials originally appoint-
ed failed to show up, and a delay ensued be-
fore the start so that others could be found
to fill the absentees' places.
The games committee, consisting of Wash-
ington Bowle, Jr., Frank S. Whitman, Geo.
J. Turner, S. Johnson Poe, D. M. Jenkins
and J. P. Houston, did their work well.
The officials as finally appointed were
busy fellows, and there was no idle time. In
fact, there was much doing at all times.
The local athletes carried off quite a bunch
of honors.
Hill's Hurdling Feat.
In a trial heat in the 100-yard hurdle
race D. S. Hill covered the distance in 12%
seconds. Hill made the same time in the
games held at the armory prior to last
night's set, but the hurdles were not in cor-
rest position. Last night the filghts were in
correct position, there being eight of them,
the first 15 yards from the start and the
final one the same distance from the finish.
In the finals Duker, of the M. A. C.; cар-
tured first place, with Randall, Mount
Washington, and Requardt, M. A. C., a
close second and third, respectively.
The two-mile roller skating relay race
brought the crowd to its feet. The contest-
ing teams represented the Walbrook and
Mount, Washington Clubs, and the Wal-
brook four won the medals.
W. D. Randall, Jr., for Mount Washing-
ton, nad Clark Jones, for Walbrook, were
the third men on their respective teams.
Randall is a big fellow, while his oppo-
nent was a mite of a lad. Randall had a
few yards start ahead of the little fellow,
but the spectators went wild as Clark
edged his way to the front on the second
lap and won handily.
The competitive drill among members
of the Fifth Regiment was an interesting
feature. The process of choosing the win-
ners was one of elimination and reminded
one of an old-time spelling match. A line
of soldiers was stretched out along the
floor, and the men were 'put through vari-
ofis forms of drill. One by one the lads in
blue were counted out until the winner
stood alone in his glory. Sergeant George
Erler, Company D, was first, with Cor-
poral James Lyon, Jr., Company A, sec-
ond, and Sergeant J. C. Scott, Company
E, third.
Te 440-yard dash was a close-pressed
race, and two of St. John's College men
won the first two places. A crowd of start-
ers dashed off at the crack of the pistol,
and there was much fouling, several run-
ners falling. J. Harrison sprinted across
the line in the lead, with Edwin Warfield,
Jr., son of the Governor, at his heels..
