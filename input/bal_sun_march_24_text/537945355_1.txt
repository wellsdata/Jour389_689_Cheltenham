CHARGES ABUSES
AT CHELTENHAM
Jones Accuses Some Cottage
Masters Of Disciplining By
"Fear And Force"
State Employment Head
Makes Allegation In Reply
To Mandamus Suit
Harry C. Jones, State Employment
Commissioner, yesterday declared tha
some cottage masters in disciplining
Negro youths at the Cheltenhar
School for Boys have used "fear and
force."
His charges were made in an answer
filed in Superior Court, to mandamus
proceedings brought by the board
of managers of the school to force the
commissioner to certify salaries of in-
stitutional employes he has declared
"totally unfit."
Hearing Date Unset
No date for a hearing on the man-
damus suit has been set. Meanwhile,
those employes declared unfit by Mr.
Jones to continue on the institution's
pay roll are being paid one-half of
their wages out of a special $30,000
fund allotted to the school when it
was taken over by the State.
To support his contention that cer-
tain employes of the school are unfit
for further duty, Mr. Jones, in his
answer, cited their records, their ex-
perience and their attitudes. Mr. Jones
stated that some of the employes in
question were physically disabled or
too old.
"Case Histories" Cited
"Case histories" of some of the insti-
tution's employes were cited by the
commissioner. One cottage master, he
said, "settles all disciplinary problems
himself by using the switch unhesi-
tatingly and does not care whether he
punishes the right boy."
Another cottage master, Mr. Jones
added, "handles disciplinary problems
himself, chiefly by fear and force. This
man . . . would probably be better
adapted to his former grade of night
watchman."
