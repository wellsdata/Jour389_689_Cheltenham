$10,000 FOR CHELTENHAM
But Mayor And Comptroller May Di-
vert It If They See Fit.
The recent discussion over the manage-1
ment of the House of Reformation for Col-
ored Boys, at Cheltenham, Prince George's
county, and the question as to whether the
moral tone of the place was all that it ought
to be, prompted the Board of Estimates
yesterday to refrain from making a direct
appropriation for the institution this year,
as has been the custom in the past, but to
set aside $10,000 as a fund for "the main-
tenance and care of colored youth and chil-
dren." The disposal of this fund will be in
the hands of the Mayor and City Comp-
troller, who are authorized to make a con-
tract with the Colored House of Reforma-
tion, and that institution can thus be kept
under closer supervision by the city au-
thorities.
1
1
The institution will, it is supposed, get
nearly all, if not all, of the fund, since the
other colored homes usually appropriated
for have already been taken care of for
next year. The officials designated can,
however, divert any part of it to another
colored asylum if they see fit. If the Col-
ored House of Reformation should get the
whole $10,000, however, it would still fall
short by $5,000 of the sum asked for by the
board of managers. This year's appropria-
tion was $0,000.
I'resident Jeffrey R Brackett and Mr.
John M. Gleen, of the Board of Supervisors
of City Charities, were before the Board of
Estimates, and on behalf of their own
board respectfully declined the proposition
previously made that they take charge of
the fund to be appropriated for the Colored
House of Reformation. They pointed out
that the institutions which the board at
present looks after take up all the available
time. They suggested the appointment of
a committee to visit not only the Chelten-
ham institution but all reformatories for
which the city makes appropriations, to
examine them and report on their needs
and deserts before the next municipal bud-
get is made up.
3
The suggestion was well received, al-
though no action was taken on it. A com-
mission for a similar purpose was in exist-
ence some years ago, and its work was
found very useful.
