ROBERTS DEFENDS
CHELTENHAM WORK
Board President Replies To
Criticism Of Child Wel-
fare League
- Robert F. Roberts, president of the
board of directors of the House of
Reformation for Colored Boys al
Cheltenham, yesterday defended the
institution against criticism leveled at
it in a report of the Child Welfare
League of America, Inc.
At the same time a local attorney,
Erwin I. Feldman, representing a
group of Negroes, announced they
would seek to have the management
of the reformatory taken over by the
State.
Conditions Condemned
The report of the league, which was
made public Saturday, recommended
that the State take charge of the insti-
tution. Physical and health conditions
there were severely condemned.
"We have a good institution and it
has been doing a good piece of work for
the State," Mr. Roberts said last night.
"The board responded to the league's
request for a survey, and the institu-
tion now is torn to pieces in it.
Considers Work Success
"The board thinks the institution
has been run well. I have been work-
ing for the last ten years with Mr.
Pyles (John B. Pyles, superintendent)
and I consider he has made a success
of his work.
"It is true that the place is crowded
and there should be new buildings.
At present the board is marking time
awaiting Governor Nice's budget."
Mr. Feldman explained he had been
retained by a group of Negro clergy-
men, school teachers, social workers
and others to promote State super-
vision for the institution. He explained,
however, that this did not include
management by Negroes exclusively.
Gets Sums From State
He will institute habeas corpus
proceedings in the local courts, he
added, when he or his clients hear of
any inmate at the reformatory being
treated other than humanely.
"The institution is operated as a pri-
vate concern, but gets $200 for each in-
mate each year from the State, and the
State, therefore, should operate it,"
Mr. Feldman said.
He pointed out that Governor Nice,
in his recent campaign, said that Chel-
tenham should be taken over by the
State.
