TO PRISON FOR FALSE ALARMS
Small Negro Boy Confesses After
He Is Sentenced.
After denying in the Juvenile Court
yesterday the charge that he had sent in
two false alarms of fire and endeavoring
to put the blame on another colored bor,
Earl Duncan admitted his guit. He did
not confess until Judge Carmody had
found him guilty and sentenced bim to
six months in the Colored House of Ref-
ormation, at Cheltenham.
Duncan was being taken out of the
courtroom by a patrolman when he ad-
mitted his guilt. He was taken back to
Judge Carmody and made full confes-
sion.
Duncan is 13 years old and lives at
928 Eutaw street. He was charged with
turning in false alarms from Box 334,
Biddle and Cathedral streets, and from
Box 315, Monument street and Plover
alley.
GORGAS PRAISES CRUSADERS
Surgeon-General Of Army Inspects
Result Of Mosquito Campaign.
That it is too early to expect much of
an improvement in the mosquito situa-
tion was the substance of a report Gen.
William C. Gorgas, Surgeon-General of
the United States Army, made to Mayor
Preston yesterday afternoon.
General Gorgas came to Baltimore
shortly before noon. He was accom-
panied by Major Robert E. Noble, his
assistant in Washington, and Major
Theodore C. Lyster, also of the Medical
Department, United States Army. They
were met by Sanitary Engineer William
D. Wrightson, of the United States Pub-
lic Health Service, who is directing the
Mayor's campaign against mosquitoes.
The party had luncheon at the Mer-
chants' Club as guests of the Mayor,
and later made an inspection of the
work done to date by the crusaders.
