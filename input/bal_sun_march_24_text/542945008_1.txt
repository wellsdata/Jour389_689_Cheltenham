CREDITS PROPOSED
FOR CHELTENHAM
Other Improvements Urged By
New Superintendent
| The Cheltenham School for Boys,
which now serves as a corrective
and disciplinary institution, will
become an accredited school whose
students may proceed to advanced
grades in regular educational insti-
tutions, according to a program
proposed by Royal A. B. Crump,
the school's new superintendent.
Mr. Crump's administrative plan
has been approved tentatively by
the school's board of managers, and
will be submitted to appropriate
State authorities for further ap-
proval and financial support.
☑ Mr. Crump developed this type
of educational program as superin-

Training School for Boys which,
like Cheltenham, has a student
body of about 400 delinquent Ne-
gro boys. The proposal provides for
correlated vocational and academic
departments from which students
may go on to higher grades in other
schools..

Health Department Favored
In a proposed health depart-
ment, at least one and possibly sev-
eral nurses will be added to the
staff. None is stationed at the
school at present,
Services of the present resident
physician and visiting dentist will
be continued. Permanent psychia-
tric services will be made available.
The only psychiatrist now attached
to Cheltenham is one of the con-
scientious objectors, and, in the
opinion of Mr. Crump, such serv-
ices are of a primary importance
to a school for delinquent and re-
tarded children.
Academic Training
Full-time academic training will
be provided all inmates of the
school from 8 to 14 years old, and
a half day of academic classes to
older boys, most of whom, Mr.
Crump says, naturally will be best
adapted to manual training.
Assuming that most of them will
want such training, Mr. Crump em-
phasizes that no pupil would be
forced to attend vocational school.

drilling and discipline, which, al-
though not of a pre-induction-
