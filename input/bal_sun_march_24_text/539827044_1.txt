The University of Maryland Biologi-
cal Society will hold its one hundred
and fifteenth program meeting at 8.30
o'clock tonight in Gordon Wilson Me-
morial Hall on the eighth floor of the
University Hospital.
- Miss Georgianna Gittinger, of the
University of Maryland, School of
Pharmacy, will read a paper on the
early use of quinine as a medicine
before the society.
- A second paper entitled "Metabolism
and Toxicity Studies With. Mannitol
and Sorbitol in Man and Animals"
will be read by Fred Ellis, research
fellow in pharmacology at the Univer-
sity's School of Medicine.

Four Appointments
Made By O'Conor
Member Of Cheltenham Board And
Three Justices Of Peace
Named
Governor O'Conor yesterday an-
nounced the appointment of Peter M.
Siewierski, Baltimore attorney, as a
member of the board of managers of
the Cheltenham School for Boys, to
succeed James A. Pratt, who recently
resigned.
Mr. Siewierski will serve the re-
mainder of a three-year term which
began June 1, 1940.
The Governor also announced the
appointment of three justices of the
peace for Montgomery county. They
Care George M. Wilmeth, Takoma Park:
James B. Doten, Silver Spring, and
Harry M. Williams, Rockville.
