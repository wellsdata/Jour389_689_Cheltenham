Are
Wanted At Trial Here
Of Charles Butcher
Maj. Gen. Lewis B. Hershey, di-
rector of selective service, and the
adjutant general of the United
States Army, or their accredited
representatives, and other high-
ranking draft officials have been
summoned as witnesses by Charles
Butcher, Harvard graduate, who
will be tried in Federal Court to-
morrow on a selective service viola-
tion charge.
Butcher contests the authority of
General Hershey to assign conscien-
tious objectors to public service
camps, and contends that although
the law says specifically that ob-
jectors' camps shall be under ci-
vilian control, the control actually
is almost entirely in military hands.
Accused Of Refusing To Quit Camp
Butcher, a conscientious objector,
is accused of refusing to leave a
camp at Cheltenham, Md., and re-
port to a camp at Marion, N. C. He
had been serving as a counselor
at the Cheltenham Reformatory for
Negro Boys and prior to that had
served at two other objectors'
camps, it was testified at a pre-
liminary hearing.
Wilfred T. McQuaid, counsel for
Butcher, has requested that sub-
poenas be served on General Her-
shey and the adjutant general of
the army or their representatives;
Col. Lewis F. Kosch, chief of the
camp operations division of selec-
tive service, and Lieut. Col. Frank-
lin A. McLean, of the camp opera-
tions division.
Records Requested
These officials were requested to
bring certain records with them,
some having to do with military
promotions and citations given to
officials in the draft system and
others having to do with adminis-
trative procedure of the camp
operations division.
Also summoned were Joseph
Weaver, executive secretary of the
National Service Board for Re-
ligious Objectors, and George B.
Reeves, of the National Committee
on Conscientious Objectors.
Butcher is 26, single, and lives
in Cambridge, Mass.
