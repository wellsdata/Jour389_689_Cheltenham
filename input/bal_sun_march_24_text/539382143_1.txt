Cheltenham Board
Tours Institution
Members of the new, all-Negro
board of managers of the Chelten-
ham School for Boys toured the
institution's buildings and grounds
yesterday during their second
meeting.
They also inaugurated a detailed
study of the school through the six
committees appointed at the
board's organizational meeting last
Friday at Annapolis.
Next Meeting April 5
Comments and recommendations
on the subjects of the investiga-
tions will be made at the next
meeting, scheduled for 1 P.M.
Monday, April 5, at the training
school.
Fields being covered by these
studies include curriculum, pupil
activities, physical facilities, staff
and supervision, administration
and the philosophy and purpose
of the school.
All Members Present
Dr. William E. Henry, president
of the board, reported that all nine
members were present for the day-
long meeting at the school.
It had been decided at the
board's initial session that first-
hand knowledge of the institution
was essential for each member and
that'a prompt inspection of facili-
ties was desirable.
At present the school lacks a
superintendent, and no acting
superintendent has been named by
the new board. Dr. Henry has been
delegated by the board to work
closely with H. R. Harris, assistant
superintendent.
