Cheltenham Officers Re-Elected.
The managers of the House of Reforma-
tion for Colored Boys at Cheltenham, who
were re-elected about a month ago, met yes-
terday and re-elected the officers whose
term of office had expired. They were:
President-Gen. Thomas J. Shrjock,
Vice-President-Henry S. Dulaney.
Treasurer-Edward Stabler, Jr.
Secretary-Joseph J. Jannes.
With these officers the board of man-
agers consists of:
Messrs. Thomas E. Carson, Edward Stinson, Rer.
G. P. Bragg, Dr. W. H. Gibbons, N. G. Grasty,
C. C. Magruder, John Hubner, J. H. Murray, Rob-
ert Crain, Harry 8. Cummins.
Boy's Death Accident, He Says.
Coroner Caruthers held an investigation
at the Northeastern Police Station last
night in the case of William Getner, 11
years old, son of Mr. Jacob H. Getner, 918
North Madeira street, who was struck by
an engine of the Pennsylvania Railroad at
Patterson Park avenue and Eager street on
Tuesday and almost instantly killed. The
crew of the engine, with several witnesses,
were examined, after which the Coroner
gave a certificate of accidental death.
Sailor Stricken On Street.
John A. Batchler, 50 years old, a sailor,
was seized with an attack of vertigo yes-
terday morning on Caroline street, near
Madison street, and fell unconscious to the
pavement. He was taken to St. Joseph's
Hospital, where he died several hours later.
Coroner Caruthers gave a certificate of death
due to Bright's disease,
