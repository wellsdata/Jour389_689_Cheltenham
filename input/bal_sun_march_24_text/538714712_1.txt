Cheltenham is now a State institution, but
the act of the Legislature by which it was
acquired names the present board of man-
agers. It is up to them to decide whether they
will attempt to continue to run Cheltenham
as a local institution or will implement the
will of the State, which is that the colored
boys there confined shall be given a genuine
chance for education and rehabilitation under
the ægis of the State. It is hard to believe
that all future Legislatures will have much
patience if Cheltenham continues to thwart
the public purpose.
CHELTENHAM AGAIN
Performing its duty of inspecting places
where convicted people are confined, the
grand jury visited the Cheltenham reforma-
tory for colored boys and found it pretty ter-
rible. The grand jury recommends that the
board of managers either improve the place
or make way for other members who will.
It is now two months since Col. Harry C.
Jones, the State Employment Commissioner,
issued his ultimatum declaring that he would
not authorize pay for the employés at Chel-
tenham until his recommendations for reform
were substantially carried out. Among the
recommendations was the dismissing of the
most incompetent employés and the retire-
ment from the board of members who are on
the Cheltenham pay roll. Up to now nothing
has been done on either of these matters, and
the impasse remains. Conceivably modern
