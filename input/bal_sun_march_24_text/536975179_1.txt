HAS FAITH IN PROMISES
Mr. Maurice Laupheimer Says Chel-
tenham Defects Will Be Remedied.
Mr. Maurice Laupheimer, chairman of
the grand jury committee, which visited the
Cheltenham Reformatory for Colored Boys
and submitted a report criticising the man-
agement of the institution, said yesterday
that he believed the defects in the conduct
of the institution would be remedied at
once. Mr. Laupheimer said:
"As chairman of the committee which vis-
ited Cheltenham, I drew up the report. This
was submitted and incorporated in the re-
port of the foreman, Mr. Henry B. Gilpin. I
know of nothing that I can say by way of
supplement to the report, and I am satis-
fied that the defects which we found and
pointed out will be remedied at once. I am
sure that my associates on the committee
concur in this belief.
"The institution is undoubtedly suffering
from an insufficiency of funds. If sufficient
money is provided the heating and lighting
facilities will be improved, as they should
be.
.
"The committee visited Cheltenham in its
official capacity and reported what it found
there. It did not go there for the purpose
of 'white-washing' any defects that might
be found, nor was the report intended as a
'roast' on the institution. The committee
merely called the attention of the manage-
ment to a number of defects found to exist
in the conduct of the institution. A promise
has been made that the defects will be rem-
edied at once, and, as I know several of the
gentlemen connected with the institution, I
have every faith in the fulfillment of that
promise."
