REIDT IS GIVEN
HEPBRON'S JOB
Named Director Of Criminal
Justice Commission
Wallace Reidt was appointed
managing director of the Baltimore
Criminal Justice Commission yes-
terday, succeeding James M. Hep-
bron who became executive vice
president.
The apointment was made at a
meeting of the commission's board
at the Emerson Hotel. Both Mr.
Reidt and Mr. Hepbron have been
with the commission since its
organization 26 years ago.
Born in Baltimore in 1898, Mr.
Reidt attended city and Baltimore
county public schools, the business
school of the Young Men's Chris-
tian Association and obtained his
law degree at the University of
Battimore Law School, He served
with the Canadian Army in World
War I and was seriously wounded
in 1917.
Made Many Surveys
He joined the staff of the com-
mission as secretary and statis-
tician in January, 1923, and. since
then, has made many surveys for
the city and State as well as the
commission.
He held the rank of captain in
the Maryland State Guard in World
War II and was chairman of a spe-
cial panel board of the selective
service that arranged for the indue
tion of prisoners from State penal
Institutions into the nation's armed
forces.
He has made for the past several
years an annual survey of the coun-
Ly jails in Maryland for the State
Department of Correction. At the
request of the board of managers
of the Cheltenham School for Boys,
Mr. Reidt recently acted as execu-
tive secretary for the board and
submitted a report that recom-
mended an all-colored board for
the school.
Author Of Manual
He is the author of a recent
manual prepared for the board
members of the Montrose School
for Girls. He became assistant man-
ager of the Baltimore Criminal
Justice Commission in 1938.
Mr. Reidt is a member of the
boards of the Ulman Foundation,
the Baltimore Eye, Ear and Throat
Hospital and the Montrose School.
He is secretary of the Baltimore
Youth Commission and a member
of the Youth Commission of Balti-
more county. He is a Mason and a
member of Boumi Temple, Shrine.
