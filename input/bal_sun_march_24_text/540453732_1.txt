MANAGEMENT COMPLIMENTED
Black Wig, From York, Wins In
2.16 Class Occidental Takes 6-
Furlong Dash With Ease.
[From a Staff Correspondent.]
Laurel, Md., Oct. 11. The racing today
at Laurel's Four County Fair was fully
up to the high standard set Monday, the
opening day of the meeting. The weather
conditions were excellent. Racing over
the track is doing the track good. Horse-
men today pronounced the footing to be all
that could be desired.
The management came in for a lot of
favorable comment for the wisdom shown
in constructing such a course. Some of the
officials were saying this evening that they
believed the new Laurel track to be the
best half-mile circle in Maryland. That
the track is a good, safe one is a certainty.
The attendance today was larger than
that of the opening day, and, with good
weather, it is anticipated it will grow
daily.
Meeting Is Well Conducted.
So far the race meeting has been fric-
tionless. The moment the starter an-
nounces the result of a race he invites the
money winners to come to the stand and
they are promptly paid the money parts
of the purse their horses won.
The wide stretch-60 feet-enables big
fields to score away on more even terms
than is possible on many tracks.
Because of the shortness of the day's
light and the large entry list, the harness
races, have to be started at 1.30 P. M. so
that the running horses may have their
chance..
There was much greater speculation on
the results of today's racing than on that
of Monday's.
:
The afternoon's sport began with a race
for the 2.16 class-pacers. It was won in
straight heats by Black Wig, a gelding
from York, Pa. Minnie Tillian, a mare
from Bath, N. Y., forced Black Wig out in
2.164.
The 2.20 trot was won off the reel by
Mald of Athens, from Governor, N. Y.
Little Joe Wiesenfeld's Expedition, Jr.,
was the Maid's warmest contender.
The country folk took much interest in
the race for country horses of the 2.40
class. Brosene Bros.' mare, Wannetta,
from Ellicott City, won the first heat and,
though she was the only one able to race
Van March, from Cheltenham, she just did
not have speed to do more than force
Von March to a record of 2.26%.
Occidental Takes The Dash.
The six-furlong running race was won by
Occidental, owned by W. R. Hall, of Car-
uthsville, Md. She was ridden by Mc-
Carthy, who took Ice from Elaborate to
the last furlong. Occidental, the favorite;
when let down, showed a burst of speed
and won by a neck from Elaborate, who
beat Lady Fitzherbert by three-fourths of
a length.
R. E. Biggs, who started the runners,
got them off in great shape. His starting
of the trotters and pacers also was well
done.
