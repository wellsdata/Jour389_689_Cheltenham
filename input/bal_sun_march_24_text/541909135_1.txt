CHELTENHAM
GETS TROPHIES
!
Should Help Solve Problems,
Judge Moylan Says
| At courthouse ceremonies mark
ing the presentation of eight
trophies to Cheltenham School for
Boys, to be competed for by var
sity and cottage teams, Judge
Charles E. Moylan yesterday de-
clared that the awards should help
solve disciplinary and run-away
problems at the school.
In turning over the trophies to
the athletic director of Cheltenham,
Judge Moylan pointed out that
whenever the school has been the
target for criticism, leaders in the
child-welfare field have called for
enrichment of the educational and
extracurricular program,
Praises Ex-Umpire
The judge said, in line with that
thought, that in the past several
years he has been interested in
the organization of a band, a glee
club and a drum and bugle corps.
The judge said Doll Derr, former
International League umpire, "has
demonstrated a real interest in the
welfare of these boys and has been
most helpful" in the efforts to aug-
ment the sports program at the
school.
One of the trophies, to be used
in connection with senior basket-
ball, was donated by Judge Moylan.
A junior basketball trophy was
donated by Dr. Walter B. Clemson.!
