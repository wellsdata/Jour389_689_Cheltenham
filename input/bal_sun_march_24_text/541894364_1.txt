Church Tribute
Paid To Founder
Representing all spheres of com-
munity life, hundreds of men and
women yesterday filled Douglas
Memorial Community Church, Mad-
ison and Lafayette avenues, to pay
tribute to the Rev. Dr. Frederick
Douglas, founder of the Negro
congregation, on the first anni-
versary of his death.
The large church was crowded at
morning services at which the Rev.
Joseph M. Evans, pastor of Metro-
politan Community Church in Chi-
cago, was the speaker. The Rev.
Marion C. Bascom, present rector
of Douglas Community Church, con-
ducted the service.
Music consisted of the favorite
hymns of Dr. Douglas, Miss Viola
Jones, a trustee of the church, was
the soloist.

Former Mayor Howard W. Jack-
son spoke of him as a citizen who,
among other things, had been a
parole officer at Cheltenham School
for Boys, a member of the rationing
board during World War II and a
member of the grand jury.
Dr. Dwight C. W. Holmes, presi-
dent emeritus of Morgan College,
emphasized the characteristics of
Dr. Douglas as a friend.
Music By Combined, Choirs
Other remarks were made by the
Rev. J. J. Barnes, pastor of Or-
chard Street Methodist Church,
who represented other denomina-
tions.
Music was by the combined seni-
or, young people's and Gospel
choirs of the church. The soloist
was Simon Scott, a trustee. The
Evangel Singers, a female sextett,
also sang. John H. Murphy, Jr.,
presided.
