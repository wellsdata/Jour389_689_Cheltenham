today.
It was the first fatal crash in five
months of operation at the Pyote
base.
The bodies of the eleven victims
were found yesterday near the
summit of a hill fifty miles north
of Van Horn, Texas. One of the
men was a radio instructor, flying
with a crew in training. His name
was not immediately released.
Crew Of 30 Reported
Saved In Torpedoing
Miami, Fla., June 1 (AP)-Capt.
Julius F. Swenson, of Pierpont.
N. Y., reported here that all thirty
men aboard his vessel, a small
United States merchantman, were
saved after the ship was torpedoed
by an Axis submarine early in May.
The attack occurred in the South
Atlantic, off a large West Indian
island. The men were picked up at
daybreak by United Nations patrol
ships.
