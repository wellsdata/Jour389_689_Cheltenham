WILL NOT GET
CHELTENHAM
R. F. Roberts, Chairman,
Predicts Directors Will
Vote Against Plan
MOVE IS STARTED
TO ACQUIRE PLACE
Board Of Public Works
Seeks Data About
Institution
Although the Board of Public Works
Jate yesterday moved to take over the
House of Reformation for Colored
Boys-also known as Cheltenham-as
a State institution, its efforts are ex-
pected to prove futile at least until
fall.
An act of the last General Assembly
empowered the Board of Public Works
to acquire Cheltenham if the directors
of the institution wished to deed it to
the State.
Robert F. Roberts. chairman of the
board of directors of the home, last
night said that in his opinion "the
board will vote against turning the
home over to the State."
Directors Not Consulted
Informed that the secretary of the
Board of Public Works had been in-
structed to communicate with him on
the subject, Mr. Roberts said:
"I am very sorry, but I am leaving
for California at 3.30 tomorrow after-
noor, for three months."
Mr. Roberts added that "if the Gov-
ernor had called us (the board of di-
rectors of Cheltenham) into consulta-
tion at the time the bill was pending
in the Legislature we would have been
glad to discuss the matter with him
and made our position clear.
Precipitated By Jury's Report
"For that matter he could have got-
ten our views on the matter before the
meeting today, but we have not been
asked to state our position.""
Action by the Board of Public
Works was precipitated by a report of
the May grand jury to Governor Nice
on alleged conditions at the insti-
tution.
After yesterday's meeting Governor
Nice said:
"An act of the last session of the
General Assembly conferred upon the
Board of Public Works power to take
over Cheltenham if the directors of
that institution wished to deed it to
the State. The act required that the
board, if it decided to take over the
institution, also must take over all
outstanding encumbrances.
"The board today has instructed its
secretary to communicate with the
board of directors of Cheltenham ask-
ing a detailed itemized list of all en-
cumbrances outstanding and request-
ing action by the board of dircetors
in accord with the act of the Legis-
lature."
To Communicate With Directors
Joseph O'C. McCusker, Deputy
State Comptroller and secretary of the
board, said he would send a com-
munication to the Cheltenham board
head this morning.
Besides Governor Nice, other mem-
bers of the board who attended the
meeting were William S. Gordy, Jr.,
State Comptroller, and Hooper S.
Miles, State Treasurer. Herbert R.
O'Conor, Attorney-General, also at-
tended the meeting to advise the board
on the law concerning the institution.
