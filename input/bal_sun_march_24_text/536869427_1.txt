J. J. JANNEY DIES AT HOME
For Thirty Years Officer At Colored
Boys' Institution.
-Joseph J. Janney, secretary and treas-
urer of the House of Reformation for
Colored Boys at Cheltenham, Md., a
retired business man, Civil War veteran
and member of the Society of Friends,
died last night at his home, 1923 Park
avenue, from the effects of a stroke of
paralysis which he suffered two weeks
ago yesterday.
.:
Mr. Janney was born in New Lisbon,
now Lisbon, Ohio, Jan. 9, 1841, and was
a son of the late Henry and Hannah
Schelfield Janney. He came to Balti-
more 50 years ago. He served through
the Civil War, enlisting in this city in
1861 as a member of Purnell's Legion.
He participated in the Virginia cam-
paign and was slightly wounded before
Petersburg, Va.
After the war Mr. Janney entered the
internal revenue service in this city.
He later entered the commission busi-
néss as a member of the firm of Pope &
Janney, and later went into the coal
business. He has been an official of the
House of Reformation for Colored Boys
during the last 30 years; was a life-
long member of the Society of Friends,
Erecorder of the Maryland Commandery
of the Loyal Legion for many years and
member of the Grand Army of the Re-
public.
Mr. Janney is survived by a daughter,
Miss Bertha Janney; two brothers, Wil-
liam H. and Dr. O. Edward Janney,
three sisters, Miss Marian E. Janney,
Mrs. Ellen S. Walton, of Glenburnie, N.
Y., and Miss Hannah H. Janney.
