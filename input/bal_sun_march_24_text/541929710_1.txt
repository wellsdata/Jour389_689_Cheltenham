STATE GROUPS
REPORT NEEDS
4 Institutions And Research
Department List Urgencies
Reports covering immediate
needs of four State institutions and
the Department of Research and
Education have been presented to
the State Planning Commission,
which will compile a program, of
urgent projects.
Henry P. Irr, commission chair-
man, said a study of the capital
improvement requests will be sub-
mitted to Governor Lane, and
should provide a basis for a bond-
issue bill to be offered at the next
General Assembly.
Listed As Immediate Needs
Miss Muriel E. Johnson, superin-
tendent of the Maryland Training
School for Colored Girls, listed the
following as immediate needs.
Central heating plant, $237,068;
sewage connection, $65,000; two
cottages for girls, $400,000; recep-
tion center and infirmary, $150,000;
addition to administration building,
$136,418; street lights for campus,
$525; superintendent's home, $25,-
000; storage house, $10,000, and
fattening pen and farrowing house
for the farm, $6,000.
The Montrose School for Girls
needs $462,600 worth of improve-
ments at once, according to Miss
Edith P. Gardner, superintendent.
This total includes buildings and
equipment for staff house, $113,-
100; dormitory for 25 girls, $128,-
000; addition to school building,
$211,900, and swimming pool,
$9,500.
