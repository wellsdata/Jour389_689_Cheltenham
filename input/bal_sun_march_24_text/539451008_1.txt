HENRY HEADS
NEW BOARD AT
CHELTENHAM
Group Meets For First
Time, Plans Visit To
School Monday
By ANNE W. HUTCHISON
[Sun Staff Correspondent)
Annapolis, March 5-Members
of the new, all-Negro board of man-
agers of the Cheltenham School
-for Boys held their first meeting
here today and made plans for an
all-day visit at the Negro training
-school on Monday.
Prior to the meeting, which was
held in the Senate chamber of the
State House, the board met with
Governor Lane in the executive
aoffices.
e
Dr. William E. Henry, president
e of the Bowie State Teachers Col-
lege, was elected president of the
nine member group.
Co-operation Promised
Governor Lane assured the board
members of his fullest co-operation
and said he had "great confidence
in the future of Cheltenham" under
their guidance and management.
"The fact that for twenty years
Cheltenham has had a reputation
for bad conditions and not fulfill-
ing its purposes has not discour
aged me," he said.
"I am confident that something
can be done about it."
Thanking the new board mem-
bers for agreeing to serve, he said
he had selected their names from
between 75 and 100 recommended
to him. He said that both Thomas
B. Sprague, chairman of the retir-
ing mixed board and Wallace
Reidt, associate director of the
Criminal Justice Commission who
had been working with the school
in recent months, would be avail-
able to the new board for advice
on any questions and problems
which might arise.
Early Survey Planned
In the course of the organiza-
tion's meeting it was decided that
the full board should make a sur-
vey of the institution as soon as
possible to learn the conditions
that exist there.
A number of committees were
named by Dr. Henry to give spe-
cial attention to certain points
during the initial visit Monday
which is scheduled for 10 A.Μ.
Serving on these committees
will be:
Curriculum - Edward Wilson
and Mrs. Violet Hill Whyte.
Staff and Supervision - Mrs.
Whyte and Mrs. Bertha B.
Proctor,
Physical Facilities-The Rev.
John Thomas Colbert and Dr.
James Arthur White.
Activities of Pupils-Lieut.
Col. William Brady and Charles
E. Cornish,
Philosophy and Purpose of
School-Mr. Wilson and Willard
W. Alten.
Administration-- Mr. Allen and
Mrs. Whyte.
Mr. Sprague, who was present
at the meeting, pointed out that
II. R. Harris, assistant superintend-
ent of Cheltenham, at present had
no official status as acting superin-
tendent, and it was suggested that
Dr. Henry work closely with him.
