O'CONOR BACKS
$12,550,000
POSTWAR FUND
To Ask Legislature To
Approve Means For Con-
struction Program
&
By LOUIS J. O'DONNELL
[Annapolis Bureau of The Sun]
Annapolis, March 15-Governor
O'Conor announced that he will
ask the Legislature to approve a
$12.550,000 construction program
to be undertaken immediately as
materials are available and to be
financed partly by surplus treasury
funds and a bond issue.
Legislation he will propose to the
General Assembly would pool
$6,000,000 from treasury surplus,
$1.725.000 in unspent appropria-
tions for postwar construction and
a bond issue of $4,825,000.
Funds Not Dedicated
The construction-loan bond issue
will differ from those of the past
in that the funds they would raise
would not be dedicated specifically
to any project. Bonds would be
issued only after the $7,725,000 on
hand had been expended on what-
ever projects are first undertaken.
Included as a project to be
financed from the $12,550,000 pool
is the proposed recreational area
somewhere on the shores of the
Chesapeake Bay.
Pooling the funds on hand and
the bond issue, without dedicating
the proceeds of the bonds to any
specific project, the Governor ex-
plained, will enable the State to
proceed with the building program
in the order in which materials be-
come available and as other circum-
stances dictate, and at the same
time save carrying charges by rea-
son of not having to issue bonds
immediately.
Listed On Program
Listed in the immediate postwar
construction program, for which
the Governor will ask this session
of the Legislature to authorize and
make fiscal provisions, are:
Three chronic disease hos-
pitals.
A new Cheltenham School for
Boys.
Additions to mental hospitals.
Construction for Training
Schools for Boys.
Construction at penal institu-
tions.
The University of Maryland
building program.
Teachers' colleges.
Additions to tuberculosis sana-
toria.
The bay-front recreational
area.
Morgan State College construc-
tion.
A new State Police headquar-
ters.
A new State Department of
Health building.
A new Commissioner of Motor
Vehicles headquarters.
The Governor said that any pro-
posal for a State office building in
Baltimore, to be purchased or con-
structed, would be handled sepaа-
rately and not as a part of the
State's construction program.
He said there was a possibility
that the Legislature would receive
at this session recommendations
relative to a centrally located office
building in Baltimore.
