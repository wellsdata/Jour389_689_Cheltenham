An examination for superintendent
of the Cheltenham School for Boys'
will be held this fall at the request of
the school's board of managers, State
Employment Commissioner Harry C.
Jones said yesterday.
The superintendency was included in
a schedule of twenty-two merit system
examinations issued by Jones for the
next quarter year.
Requirements High
Jones said the post woull pay $4,000
a year, and require "high qualifica-
tions in experience and training." The
superintendent would "plan, direct
and supervise" staff and activities of
the school for Negro boys, under di-
rection of the board of managers.
The announcement is the latest in
a long controversy over operation and
personnel of the school, which was
taken over by the State in 1937,
A court suit was instituted after
Jones held tests and reported twenty-
six school employes were "unfit for
the merit system," but the Court of
Appeals in January, 1939, ruled in
favor of the employes.
To Be Reclassified
Jones said yesterday that Benjamin
B. Sunderland, now acting superin-
tendent of the school, would "be re-
classified into his old position as parole
officer."
The board of managers is composed
of:
JUDGE JOSEPH H. ULMAN, Baltimore.
DR. G. CANDY ROBINSON, Baltimore.
DR. FRANK R. Isaac, head of the
Strawbridge Home for Boys.
JAMES A. PRATT.
JOSEPH H. BLANDFORD.
HAL B. CALGETT.
The first three were appointed and
the latter two reappointed by Gov-
ernor O'Conor last May.
Dates for the superintendency ex-
amination and the twenty-one other
tests will be announced later, Jones
said. Applications for all twenty-two
posts are to be mailed to the Employ-
ment Commissioner's office not later
than October 30.
