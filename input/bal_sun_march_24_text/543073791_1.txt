STATE BONDS SOLD
AT RECORD FIGURE
Issue Of $1,587,000 Purchased
By Syndicate For
100.0577
Money To Be Used For Con-
struction And Equipping
Of Institutions
(By the Associated Press]
Annapolis, July 25-The sale of
$1,587,000 in State bonds at the best
price in Maryland's history was an-
nounced today by the Board of Public
Works, which will use the funds for
construction and equipping of State
institutions.
The purchasing syndicate, headed by
Blyth & Co., Inc., of New York, and
Owen Daly & Co., of Baltimore, which
submitted the bid, agreed to purchase
the bonds, which bear 1½ per cent.
Interest coupons, for 100.0577. The
State will obtain the money at an
average interest cost of 1.24 per cent.
The next best sale was made July 27,
1938, when $3,413,000 on 1½ per cent
bonds went for 101.182, an average cost
to the State of 1.48 per cent. This was
in the term of Governor Nice.
Budget Balanced
Governor O'Conor, chairman of the
Public Works Board, said:
"It is most gratifying to note the
exceptionally high price at which
Maryland bonds were sold today. This
s positive proof that those who are in
the best position to know consider the
State's fiscal affairs in splendid con-
lition.
"For years, emphasis has been laid
upon the necessity of having a balanced
budget. We have now accomplished
this result. Furthermore, it has long
been felt that our State should not live
on borrowed money and defray cur-
rent expenses through loans.
Financial House In Order
"The program we have adopted
adheres strictly to such a policy. An-
other important factor is that after
pursuing the most painstaking effort
to determine the total State require-
ments for the next two years, we

an adequate amount. All these con-

in the high bids.
"I am pround that throughout the
country our securities are valued so
highly and that it is the consensus that
our financial house is in order."
