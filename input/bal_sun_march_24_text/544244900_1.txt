Fewer cases of murder were reported
to the January term grand jury than to
any similar body since 1920, according
to the grand jury's report submitted yes-
terday to Judge Eli Frank.
The report was one of the shortest
on record and contained fe
recom-
mendations. Crime and disorder are at
a minimum in Baltimore, the report
asserted.
According to the figures announced,
13 presentments of murder were made
during the term which closed yester-
day. The September term grand jury
presented. 25 murder cases; the May
term last year presented 15; the Janu-
ary term 18. In 1922 70 cases were
presented by the three grand juries.
Sixty-four cases were presented in 1921.
Only 35 cases were presented in 1920.
Assault Cases Set Record.
On the other hand, cases of criminal
assault reported by the January term
grand jury set a record for the city.
Presentments were made in 34 cases.
Comparative figures for the last 10
years show that such cases have been
increasing steadily. Seventy-two cases
were presented last year-a record num-
ber up to that time.
In its Penitentiary report. the grand
jury said conditions there were satis-
factory. Although no recommenda-
tions were made, salaries of guards
were called inadequate.
The strongest language in the re-
port was used by the committee that
inspected Cheltenham Reformatory for
Colored Boys. The institution con-
tributed little to the moral uplift of
the boys incarcerated there, it was de-
clared, and "little hope for improve-
ment in the boys at Cheltenham," was
expressed. The committee suggested
that the State assume control of the
institution.
State's Attorney Praised.
The work of Herbert R. O'Conor,
State's Attorney, was praised by the
body, which reported that his office was
"functioning capably and well." The
Police Department and the Criminal
Courts also were lauded for "estimable
and commendable work."
The attitude of the body toward
crime and other evils in the city was
indicated in one sentence of its report:
"It is gratifying to the members of
the grand jury to note the orderly con-
dition of our community." This was
in contrast with the report of the Sep-
tember. 1923. term grand jury sub-
nitted last January. which contained
many radical reform recommendations
striking at the root of the city's so-
cial and economic life.
In discharging the jury, Judge
Frank commented on tlíe members
"intelligent and diligent application to
Guty." J. Arthur Nelson was foreman
of the body and Moses Rothschild was
assistant foreman. The May term
grand jury will be sworn in Monday.
