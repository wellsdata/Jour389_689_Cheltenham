Legislation making it mandatory
for the Board of Liquor License
Commissioners of Baltimore to re-
fuse a liquor license to any person
having a criminal record was pro-
posed yesterday by the Baltimore
grand jury in a term-end report
submitted to Judge Herman M.
Moser in Criminal Court.
The jurors also advocated that
liquor-dispensing establishments be
required to close at midnight on
week days and that such places
remain closed all day on Sundays.
Acute Shortage Of Help
Reporting on inspections of the
State's penal and medical institu-
tions, the grand jury noted that in
practically every one of the institu-
tions there is an acute shortage of
adequate help, occasioned largely
by inadequate wage scales.
The main jury report, submitted
by Alfred E. B. Jones, foreman, pro-
posed that court reporters be as-
signed to police courts to record
testimony as given in the first in-
stance, shortly after the happening
of the event under inquiry.
The jurors noted that frequently
it has been found that some wit-
nesses' memories have proved to be
at fault or recollections became
hazy when the case reached the
criminal courts. Having stenogra-
phers in the police stations would
dend to discourage attempts to in-
fluence witnesses who are called
upon to give the same testimony in
the higher court, the jury con-
tended.
Grand Jury's Report
The grand jury made the follow-
ing reports on the institutions:
Maryland State Reformatory for
Males-This institution should be
used as an example of what can
and should be done in a prison.
The place is a credit to the State
of Maryland and the city of Balti-
more.
Springfield State Hospital-Un-
lerstaffed, but the buildings are
clean and well managed. Pay for
doctors, nurses, ward helpers and
others is very inadequate. A total

postwar building.
Baltimore City Hospitals-New-
er buildings in excellent condition,
but the old infirmary building is
poorly lighted and dismal, Many
buildings were closed during the
war because of lack of help. Jurors
suggested they be reopened.
"Remarkably Clean"
Crownsville State Hospital-
Buildings in excellent condition
and "remarkably" clean and well
managed. This institution is under-
staffed and unable to offer proper
treatment for the criminally insane.
Jurors suggested survey be made
to determine whether special facili-
ties for the criminally insane can
be provided.
Spring Grove State Hospital-
Buildings in good condition, but in-
stitution overcrowded and under-
staffed. One building is over 100
years old and is dark and dreary.
House of Correction-An over-
crowded condition tends to create
unhealthy and dangerous situation.
Present buildings are inadequate.
Maryland State Reformatory for
Women-Interior and exterior of
building immaculate and orderly.
High praise given to Mrs. Helen
de Corse MacArthur, head of the
institution. Jurors suggested
study of venereal disease problem,
enlargement of the institution and
more work to keep idle hands busy.
