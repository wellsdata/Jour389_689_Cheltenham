A Prince George's Industry
[Correspondence of the Baltimore Sun.]
CPPER MARLBORO', MD., Jan. 31.-Henry
W. Clagett, president of the board of county
commissioners of Prince George's county,
who conducts the manufacture of hosiery at
the House of Retormation, Cheitenhum Sta-
tion, on the Pope's Creek line of the Balti-
more and Potomac Railroad, shipped froin
that point yesterday 115,000 dozen pairs of
hose. These goods were sent to Mr. William
Islin, of New York. This is, perhaps, the
largest consignment of hostery on one day
trom any one manufactory in the United
States. Two years ago Mr. Clagett was in-
duced to begin the manufacture of hosiery
in Southern Maryland, and the House of
Reformation opened to him a good locality
for carrying on his work. He employs 165
of the inmates of this institution, and their
work yields an annual income of $3,500. This,
in a measure, helps to support the institution.
In the morning 5 boys are marshaled into
the working department by the superintend-
ent, Mr. E. A. Rhodes, and work until noon.
After dinner a like number resume the
duties, and thus the needles are kept busy,
cach division having its allotted time for
studies. Many of the workers-boys who
have been committed to this institution by
the magistrates of the different police
stations of Baltimore-show great aptitude
for this work, and under the guidance of
instructors employed for that purposo soon
become efficient and expert hands. The
manufacture of stockings in Southern Mary-
land was first begun at Hughesville, Charles
county, by the Bowling Bros., of Now Or-
leans, some eight years ago, but owing to
the poor railroad facilities they were obliged
to suspend operations, and the work was
afterwards resumed at St. Mary's Industrial
