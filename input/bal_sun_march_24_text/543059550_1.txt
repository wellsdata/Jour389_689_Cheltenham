The Cheltenham School has been in
the public eye for a long time. Bad
treatment of the boys confined there has
been a long-standing complaint. When
the State assumed jurisdiction over this
institution it was hoped and expected
that a real change would be made in the
manner of conducting the school.
No such change has been made! The
reasons for this are obvious. The board
which directs and supervises the school
is made up solely of political ap-
pointees. We believe that Governor
O'Conor should now accept the respon-
sibility for seeing to it that some real
changes are made in the conditions
prevailing at Cheltenham.
