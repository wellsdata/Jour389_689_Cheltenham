Letter From Germany
Sir:
TO THE EDITOR OF THE SUN
Inclosed you will find a copy of a
letter my daughter received from
Germany. I thought you might be
Linterested in publishing it.
A. C. SUSEMUIL.
Baltimore, March 18.
[INCLOSUREI
Dear Mary Virginia Susemihl
My daughter, Hille Gutman, 8 years
old, got the American Junior Red
Cross parcel you filled. She was
very glad to get it and she would
express her gratitude for the fine
things. My daughter is learning
English in her first year at school,
but she is not able to write in
English, so she asked me to write
for her.
We are D.Is now in a camp in
Germany. We had our native coun-
try on the Baltic Sea, our beautiful
Estoria. We had there a fur life,
but our homeland is occupied by
Russians and we cannot return.
You and your parents are very
happy people, because you can live
in freedom in your homes in the
U.S.A. We had such a life, but it
is as a fairy tale now. We all here
would like to go to America, but
There are no possibilities.
HILDE GUTMAN.
Augsbury, Hochfield,
Bavaria, Germany, Feb. 28.
Maryland Artists' Exhibition
TO THE EDITOR OF THE SUN Sir:
In commenting upon the pictures
That won the awards in the Mary-
land artists' exhibition, my 8-year-
old son hit the nail pretty squarely
on the head when he said:
"Looks as if they were done by
kids. Look at this one pointing to
the one of the bird that won a prize
for the best draftsmanship), It
looks like some kind of puzzle and
they put the wrong parts together!"
K. JONES.
Baltimore, March 18.
Cheltenham School
Sir:
TO THE EDITOR OF THE SUN
It seems to me that the action of the
"lame duck" board of the Chelton-
ham School in summarily discharg
ing Mr. Calvin Kiah from his
position of probationary superin-
tendent is open to question.
I am not so sure that Mr. Kiah
was given a chance to demonstrate
his ability to manage the school
and put into effect his modern, pro
gressive program. If he had been
given the sympathetic assistance of
his board and the wholehearted co
operation of his staff, there is every
reason to believe that he would
have made Cheltenhain a reform
school of which the State of Mary-
land could be proud.
In the field of public relations
Mr. Kiah did an outstanding piece
of work.. He personally visited
the homes from which his charges
come and thereby gained first-hand
information concerning their back-
ground.. An honor system was
introduced and to this the boys re-
sponded well.
It seems quite clear that Mr.
Kjah is entitled to be removed from
the category of an inenicient ad
ministrator and given an opportu
nity to prove his worth.
JOHN W. WOODHOUSE
Baltimore, March 18.
