Tawes, Mrs. O'Conor Lose Rations
State Comptroller J. Millard
Tawes lost his personal gasoline
ration for a year, and his official
ration was revoked with permission
to seek a new official ration.
State Treasurer Hooper S. Miles,
.appointed by the Governor to head
a central agency to control use of
State-owned cars, the OPA speci-
fied, must approve Tawes' new ra-
tion. Mr. Miles had turned down
a State-owned car for his own use.
The special OPA panel found
Comptroller Tawes had misused oc-
cupational ration coupons in tak-
ing a trip in a State-owned car to
Fort Benning, Georgia, to attend
his son's wedding.
Trip For Health
Three days later the OPA after
a hearing found that Mrs. O'Conor,
wife of the Governor, had violated
the same regulation in going to
South Carolina for her health. The
