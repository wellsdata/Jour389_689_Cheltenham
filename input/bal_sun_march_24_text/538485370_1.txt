REPORTS PROGRESS
AT CHELTENHAM
Head Of Reformatory For
Negro Boys Cites
Improvements
Hal T. Kearns Addresses
Grand Jurors' Asso-
ciation
Hal T. Kearns, superintendent of the
House of Reformation for Colored
Boys at Cheltenham, last night told
members of the Grand Jurors' Asso-
ciation of improvements which have
been inaugurated at the institution.
He said he had begun the develop-
ment of a case-work system, by which
each boy entering the institution is
given individual study and treatment.
He added that the housing problem
had been revised so that the inmates
were segregated according to type
and age.
Reformatory Overcrowded
The reformatory is overcrowded, he
told the former grand jurors, there be-
ing 400 boys at Cheltenham while
there are normal accommodations for
300 only.
"There are two classes of boys who
should not be there," he continued.
"They are the definitely feeble-minded
and those with pronounced criminal
tendencies.
"Maryland has not made provision
for the feeble-minded, however, and
the Crownsville State Hospital 'is
crowded. The ages of the boys at the
institution range from 10 to 16 years."
Improvement of the academic school
by the employment of three young
Negroes, graduates from Howard Uni-
versity, was reported by Mr. Kearns.
He also said the former policy of
having a consultant physician visit the
institution once a week had been dis-
continued, and another Negro, a young
doctor, graduate of the same univer-
sity, gives the infirmary his full time.
Mr. Kearns also said plans were
being made for construction of a can-
nery, extension of the farm work and
hog raising.
