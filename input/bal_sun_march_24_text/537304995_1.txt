To Look After Colored Boys.
The annual meeting of members and sub-
scribers of the House of Reformation for
Colored Boys, at Cheltenham, Md., was
held yesterday afternoon, and the follow-
ing managers were elected:
Edward Stabler, Jr., ,Jolin Hubner, ,
Josepli J. Janney, Henry S. Dulancy,.
Thomas E. Carson, Rev. G. F. Bragg, Jr.,
C. C. Magruder, Dr. W. H. Gibbons,
Edward Stinson, J. Henry Murray.
Nathaniel G. Grasty,
They, with Messrs. William M. Isaac
and Thomas J. Meehan, who were appoint-
ed by the Governor, and Gen. Thomas J.
Shryock and Harry S. Cummings, who rep-
resent Baltimore, will constitute the board.
The secretary, Mr. Janney, reported that
the institution is in fine condition. Many
Important improvements were made dur-
ing the year. The increased appropria-
tions on the part of the State and city
enabled the managers to be more liberal in
the way of pernianent betterment
