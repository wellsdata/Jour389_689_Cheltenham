MANAGEMENT IS CRITICISED
Mr. Mark 0. Shriver Also Considers
Moral Tone Lax-Needs Of Board
Of Charities.
The Board of Estimates yesterday took
up the request of the Board of Directors of
the Colored House of Reformation. at Chel-
tenham, for an appropriation of $15,000, an
Increase of $6,000 over this year's allow-
ance. The board listened to some severe
criticism of the institution by Mr. Mark O.
Shriver, a former member of the grand
jury, who had made an inspection of the
place. He said it was poorly managed and
the moral tone was not what it ought to
be. He admitted, however, that this might
be remedied If the inmates were placed un-
der closer supervision, which can only be
accomplished if the Institution receives
more money. The members of the board
who were present to urge the claims of the
reformatory declared that this was the
only way by which the conditions there
can be improved and that the disadvan-
tages are due entirely to lack of money.
The directors present were Messrs. J. J.
Janney. Henry S. Dulaney, Jordan Stabler,
Orrin C. Painter, Harry S. Cummings, col-
ored, and Rev. George F. Bragg, colored.
Superintendent J. W. Dorsey, of the insti-
tution, and City Councilman Hiram Watty
also attended the meeting. Rev. Mr. Bragg
suggested that better results could be at-
tained if the teachers and others in charge
of the boys were colored men. The board
took no action on the appropriation.

A meeting of the board of supervisors
will be held this afternoon to consider pro-
posed Improvements. It was explained
that the 142 acres desired are for farming
purposes, to keep the inmates employed,
as well as for a grazing ground for the cat-
tle and to raise fodder for them instead of
buying it It is also pointed out that at
the present rate of increase the buildings
will have to be extended sooner or later
An excess of $7,500 for the care of the in-
sane over last year's appropriation is in.
cluded in the estimate. This is required
on account of the intention to remove about
50 of the insane at Bayview to other insti-
tutions where the cost of maintaining them
is greater. But it is thought that these
places at Bayview will soon be taken by
newcomers.
To Open Ostend Street.
The Board of Estimates also took up the
question of opening Ostend street from
Leadenhall to Race street A curved lane
connects the two portions of Ostend street
at this point, and the tracks of the United
Railways and Electric Company run through
it The opening of the street will destrov
the easement, but the board has arranged
with the railway company that the latter
shall make no claim for damages, and that
the city shall allow it to relay its tracks
without charge when the street is opened.
General Manager William A. House and
Attorney Fielder C. Slingluff, of the rail-
way company, made the arrangement with
the Board of Estimates.
