Detective Tod Hall Leaves Hospital
Detective Tod Hall, who was operated
upon at the Maryland University Hospital
four weeks ago by Dr. Frank Martin, was
able to return home yesterday and enjoy
his Thanksgiving dinner with his family.
New Truant Omcer Elected.
The vacancy caused by the resignation
of Miss M. Elizabeth Ways, as truant of-
ficer, has been filled by the School Board by
the election of Miss May McDonnell.
Brief Locals.
Sheriff James A. Gannon, of Talbot coun-
ty, notified Captain Ward, of the Central
district, yesterday that his deputy would
come after Cornelius F. Williams, charged
with assault with intent to kill.
The Helpful Circle of the King's Daugh-
ters made glad the hearts of a number of
poor people by distributing Thanksgiving
dinners.
The choir of Zion Church will give a
concert on the evening of December 8 in
the church, Gay street, near Lexington.
The program will include both religious
and secular music. under the direction of
Mr. Edward Boekner.
Marshal Farnan was notified yesterday
that two boys had escaped from the Col-
ored House of Reformation, at Cheltenham,
last Wednesday. They are Joseph Jones
and David II. Barnes, both 19 years old,
the latter formerly residing at 1526 Brunt
street.
The Lend-a-Hand Club has extended an
invitation to Mr. D. Allen Willey to deliver
an address on the "Opportunities Afforded
the Writer of Today by the Magazines." Mr.
Willey will discuss the subject at the next
meeting of the club, to be held at the Ca-
sino at Mount Washington on Monday aft-
ernoon next.
The United States Civil-Service Commis-
sion announces an examination at Balti-
more January 4 and 5, 1905, for construct-
ing engineer in the United States Powder
Depot, Dover, N. J., at $200 a month, and
assistant inspectors at $1,200 a year in
the Bureau of Animal Industry, Depart-
ment of Agriculture.
