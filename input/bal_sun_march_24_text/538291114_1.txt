Largest Crowd Of Week
Visits Automobile Show
Many Line Up To Take Driver's
Reaction Test-Exhibit Will
Close At 11 O'Clock Tonight
The week's largest crowd visited the
Automobile Show at the Fifth Regi-
ment Armory yesterday afternoon and
evening. John E. Raine, manager of
the show, said last night.
Many persons in the throng lined up
and awaited their turn to test their
reactions to typical dangers confront-
ing a driver on the highway. Those
taking one test sat in the "driver's
seat" of a simulated automobile. and
performed-or tried to the necessary!
maneuvers to avoid dangerous situa-
tions.projected upon a screen in front
of them.
Today is the last day of the show.
Mr. Raine explained. The display will
be open from 10 o'clock this morning
until 11 o'clock tonight. Shortly before
closing time, the dealers having ex-
hibits will blow the horns of the many
automobiles on display, indicating that
the show is over.
State Aid's Budget
Is Put At $1,320,000
Board To Ask That Amount For
Fiscal Year Beginning
October 1, 1937
The Board of State Aid and Chari-
ties yesterday decided on a budget
request of $1,320,000 for the fiscal year
beginning next October 1. it was an-
nounced by Samuel E. Shannahan,
chairman of the board.
Increases totaling $100,000 and favor-
ing ten hospitals, a college and sev-
eral children's-aid societies are in-
cluded in the figure, Mr. Shannahan
said.
The board also went on record as
expressing "deep gratitude" to Enoch
Harlan and M. Hampton Magruder
members of the board of directors o
the House of Reformation for Colored
Boys at Cheltenham. It was mainly
as a result of their efforts, according
to Morris A. Rome, of the Charitie:
board, that the Cheltenham govern
ing body agreed to turn control o
the institution over to the State.
TO HOLD CHRISTMAS PARTY
B. & O. Veteran Employes Sched-
ule Event For Monday Night
The Baltimore and Ohio Railroad
Veteran Employes' Association will
hold its Christmas party Monday night
at Moose Hall, 406 West Fayette street.
Charles W. Galloway, vice-president
of the line, will be the speaker.
The program, over which Henry E.
Gardner, president of the organization,
will preside, will include dancing and
entertainment.
