[By the Associated Press]
Annapolis, Oct. 10-Approval of the
State Roads Commission reorganiza-
tion plan, which would oust eight
present employes, was deferred today
by the Board of Public Works.
Governor O'Conor said the board
felt additional data should be made
available before the board takes final
action. The board voted to call Ezra
B. Whitman, chairman of the State
Roads Commission, before its next
meeting.
Date for Whitman's appearance was
not fixed, as the board meets on call
of the Governor,
To Abolish 23 Jobs
Whitman's reorganization program
calls for discontinuance of eighteen
classified and five unclassified jobs.
Transfers to other posts in the service
would take care of all but eight pres-
ent jobholders. The eight would be
placed on the eligible list.
Harry C. Jones, State Employment
Commissioner, has approved the pro-
posals, but approval of the Board of
Public Works is necessary before the
action becomes final.
O'Conor said the board spent the
balance of the five-hour session con-
sidering budget amendments petitioned
by the various divisions of the State
government.
Some Affect 1939 Budget
Some of the changes sought affected
items in the 1938-39 budget, and others
pertained to the 1940-41 budget.
O'Conor said the Cheltenham School
for Negro Boys was granted per-
mission to transfer $18,000 in unex-
pended 1938-39 funds to a building fund
for improvements at the school.
In all, some fifty budget amendment
applications were considered.
