Steam Engineers Give Smoker.
The Baltimore branch of the National
Association of Steam Engineers gave a
smoker last night in its rooms in the Bor-
der State Bank Building, Park avenue and
Fayette street. Addresses were made by
Messrs. Stephen E. Nunn. the president;
James A. Townsend, J. G. Markard and C.
E. Gambrill. Refreshments were served
in abundance. Messrs. James A. Town-
send, J. G. Markard and H. G. Brandau
comprised the committee of arrangements.
Indoor Work Bnd For His Health
Patrolman Charles R. Begnelle, for some
time past engaged in clerical work at po-
lice headquarters, has been transferred by
the Police Board to the Northeastern dis-
triet at his own request. Indoor work, he
said, was detrimental to his health. The
place be filled was formerly held by Pa-
trolman George B. Dick,, Central district.
He or Patrolman John Kelly, Central dis-
trict, night telephone man, may get the
position.

Mrs. McKinley's Acknowledgment.
Col. William H. Love, secretary of the
Board of Trade, has received a card of ac-
knowledgment from Mrs. William McKin-
ley regarding the resolutions adopted by
the Board of Trade upon the death of Prest-
dent McKinley. The card, which has a
heavy border of black, bears the following:
Mrs. McKinley acknowledges with grateful appreci-
ation the tender expressions of sympathy extended
to her in her sorrow.
September, 1901, Canton, Ohio.
