NEGRO NAMED
CHELTENHAM
SCHOOL HEAD
O'Conor Says Race Will
Get Chance To Show
Management Ability
Royal A. B. Crump. Negro, yes-
terday was appointed superintend-
ent of the Cheltenham School for
Boys by Governor O'Conor, thus
bringing the school under an all-
Negro management.
The Governor said the move was
made in the "belief that Negroes
should be given an opportunity at
least to demonstrate their ability
to manage institutions harboring
only Negro inmates."
Mr. Crump formerly was assist-
ant superintendent of the Hanover
(Va.) Training School for Negro
Boys. He will assume his new
duties on September 1.
He succeeds Vance E. Thomas,
outgoing superintendent, who will
act in an advisory capacity while
Mr. Crump is "becoming acquainted
with his duties," the Governor said.
Whites To Be Transferred
The few remaining white em-
ployes at the school soon will be
transferred to other institutions so
that the policy of a complete Negro
administration likely will be
achieved in the near future, Mr.
O'Conor said.
Mr. Crump said last night that
his contemplated administration
program for Cheltenham, sub-
mitted some time ago to the institu-
tion's board of managers, places
emphasis on "vocational education
rather than a reformatory or penal
system."
"We plan to make Cheltenham
an accredited school where an edu-
cation comparable to that of any
other vocational school, whether or
not a reformatory, will be avail-
able," he stated.
