BOYS TO HAVE
KNIVES, FORKS

But Use At Cheltenham Must
Wait, Says Superintendent

New dishes' and tableware have
been ordered for the Cheltenham
School for Colored Boys, but they
are not intended for use until a
new cafeteria building is con-
structed, Vance E. Thomas, super-
intendent, 'said yesterday.
Meanwhile, the 300 boys at the
institution will continue to eat
their meals with tablespoons..out
of enameled tin bowls.
Mr. Thomas said he could not
recall exactly when the new
utensils were ordered, but that
War Production Board priorities
had delayed their purchase.

"Mass-Feeding" System
He added that the present "mass-
feeding" system at the institution
precluded the use of knives and
forks, "because it would be im-
possible for the staff to search the
boys and make sure they do not
leave the dining room with the
implements, with which they might
stab each other."

When a cafeteria is constructed,
however, he said, the boys will be
required to turn in their knives
and forks.

He said that at the present time
it would be impossible to station
staff members at the two doors of
the dining room, now in the ad-
ministration building, to make sure
the boys turned in their knives and
forks.
Plans for a new "culinary build-
ing" have been under discussion for
three months, Mr. Thomas said, ex-
plaining that approval by the Board
of Public Works and the State De-
partment of Public Welfare had
been delayed when they questioned
the practicality of constructing a
new, building at the institution
now, when plans call for the erec-
tion of an entirely new Chelten-
ham after the war.
