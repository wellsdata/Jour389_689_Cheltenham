Governor Signs 27 Measures Passed At Recent Session Of Legislature
APPROVES ACT
PROVIDING FOR
STATE BONDS
Executive Changes Mind
And Sanctions $9,052,000
Increase In Debt
Bills Become Laws After
Sasscer And Gorfine Also
Affix Signatures
[By the Associated Press]
Annapolis, April 15-Governor Nice
late today signed twenty-seven bills
passed by the recent Legislature.
They became law when the Gov-
ernor, Senate President Lansdale G.
Sasscer and Speaker Emanuel Gor-
fine affixed their signatures.
Among them was a measure author-
izing issuance of $9.052.000 in State
bonds. Earlier during the day, Gov-
ernor Nice announced he would de-
fer signing the bill. Less than half
an hour later he changed his mind
and signed it.
The bond issue bill includes $3.300,000
in bonds for restoration to the State
Roads Commission of highway reve-
nue diverted to balance the general
fund budget, of following the State's
usual policy of borrowing money for
current expenses.
State Wide Bills
Among State-wide bills signed today
were:
Setting the State property tax rate
for 1935 at 28 cents per $100 and for
1989 at 2014. The current rate is 2214.
Providing that in the future names
of Presidential electors shall not appear
on Maryland ballots. Voters will ballot
directly for the candidates and a vote
for a candidate will count as a vote for
each of his eight electors.
Authorizing establishment of non-
profit hospital insurance associations.
Providing that drug and grovers
stores shall not sell goods at less than
cost.
Authorizing the State to take over
Cheltenham Reformatory for Negro
boys.
Authorizing appointment of a com-
missioner to regulate and inspeet small
Joan companies. It is reported that
the post has been offered to J. Wilson
Ryon, former State's Attorney of Prince
George's county.
Authorizing State to use certain funds
for matching any Federal funds which
may be appropriated for aid to crippled
children.
Authorizing State Department of
Health to administer services for aid to
rippled children.
Regulating weights of trucks and
loads allowed on Maryland highways.
and allowing solid-tire trucks to carry
42,000 pounds.
Authorizing State Roads Commission
to issue refund bonds.
Authorizing appointment of cominis-
sion to study need for bridge building
in Maryland.
Authorizing countles and cities to es-
tablish reasonable facilities for public
recreation.
Other Bills
Other bills signed included:
11. B. 273. By Abramson-Authorizing
adoption of Audrey Ruth Leininger, of
Baltimore.
II. B. 545. By Barrick and Others-
To authorize $45.000 sewer bond issue
for Brunswick, Frederick county.
Н. В. 431, By Garrett Delegation-
Regarding issuance of beer licenses in

Н. В. 202. By Prince George's Dele
gation-Allowing three years in which

H. B. 646. By Worcester Delegation-
To set up Worcester county liquor dis-
pensaries in wholesale beer and wine
business.
Н. В. 672. By Somerset Delegation-
Authorizing $200,000 Somerset county
school bond issue.
S. B. 14. By Wilson-Authorizing
$06,000 Centreville electric plant bond
Issue.
S. R. 262. By Latimer-Authorizing
deputy treasurer for Calvert county.
S. B. 206, By Latimer-Authorizing
Calvert county to issue $45,000 in lateral
road bonds.
Another bill signed was one requir-
ing that persons desiring to become
undertakers must have a high school
education and must serve an appren-
ticeship.
