Frederick Lambden
Frederick Lambden, a druggist at
North avenue and Cumberland street
for thirty years, died Saturday at the
South Baltimore General Hospital,
which he entered July 5. Services will
be held at 10 A. M.. tomorrow in St.
Gregory's Catholic Church, and burial
will be in the New Cathedral Ceme-
tery.
:
Mr. Lambden started his practice of
pharmacy in 1902 after graduating from
the old University of Maryland. For a
time he was a pharmacist in Lauten-
bach's drug store at Eutaw and Sara-
toga streets. Then he opened his own
store at Druid Hill and North avenues,
moving from that site to the present
one thirty years ago.
Surviving are his widow, Mrs. Eliz-
abeth Carroll Lambden, and three
sons, F. Carroll Lambden, Francis H.
Lambden and Harry P. Lambden, and
one grandchild.

Mrs. Mary E. Toomey
Mrs. Mary E. Toomey, widow of
George W. Toomey, died Saturday at
the home of her son, Lawrence W.
Toomey, 1932 Griffiss avenue, Morrell
Park.
She was 83 years old and for sev-
enty-seven years had been going to
Sexton Methodist Episcopal Church,
of which she was a member. She also
was a member of the Martha Chapter,
Order of Eastern Star.
Her husband, who died ten years
ago, was a miller connected with the
Woodyear Mills that used to be lo-
cated on Gwynn's Falls.
The Rev. Ellis P. Frye, pastor of the
Sexton church, will officiate at funeral
services to be held at 10.30 Α. Μ.
Wednesday at her son's home.
Surviving, in addition to Lawrence
Toomey, are another son, Joseph H.
Toomey, and one daughter, Mrs. Jacob
J. Foerster, fourteen grandchildren
and six great-grandchildren.

John E. Dorsey
John E. Dorsey, who was formerly
superintendent of the Cheltenham
School for Boys, died yesterday in
Beverly Hills, Cal., according to word
received here. Funeral services will
be held in that city with burial in
Baltimore at Druid Ridge Cemetery.
Mr. Dorsey, who would have been 90
on July 25, was born in Baltimore and
lived here until a few years ago.
