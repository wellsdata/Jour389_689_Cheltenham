The Superintendent Of The Chel-
tenham House Of Reformation
For Colored Boys Invites "E. J.
K." And Other Critics To Inspect
The Institution And Says He Will
Pay Their Expenses If They WIII
Come.
TO THE EDITOR OF THE SUN-Sir: 1
note in the issue of THE SUN of March 21
a letter headed thus: "Arraigns Neforma-
tory Institutions in Which Prisoners Con
tract the Seed of Disease," and signed "E.
J. K.," Golden Hill, Md.
In reply to the charge as stated by "E.
J. K." I beg leave to use the columns of
your paper to extend an invitation to him
or her to visit this institution, accom
panied by the State health officer of Dor
chester county or of Baltimore city, or
both, and make a thorough investigation
of general conditions in order to be in po-
sition to substantiate the charge, if there
be any.
To offset any excuse that might be of-
fered for not paying us a visit, I wish to
state that I will bear any reasonable ex-
pense to the parties mentioned to make
the trip for this investigation,
I also respectfully request a representa-
tive of THE SUN to be present at the in-
vestigation.
JOHN B. PYLES,
Superintendent.
Cheltenham, Md., March 21,
