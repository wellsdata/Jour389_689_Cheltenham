Factory At Cheltenham
Destroyed By Flames
Broom Storage House Also Wrecked
By Early Morning Blaze Of
Unknown Origin,
Fire of unknown origin destroyed the
broom factory and the broom storage
house of the House of Reformation for
Colored Boys at Cheltenham, Prince
George's county, carly Sunday, it was
learned in Baltimore yesterday.
The fire caused the destruction of
thousands of dollars' worth of broom
corn, finished broms and equipment, and
will temporarily throw out of their regu-
Jar work about 60 inmates, who will be
distributed through other industrial de-
partments. The broom shop employed
next to the largest number of boys of
any department of the institution. It
was decided at a meeting of the members
and subscribers held yesterday at the
office of Joseph J. Janney, the secretary
and treasurer, in the Vickers Building,
to rebuild both buildings at once.
The fire started about 5 o'clock Sun-
day morning. The two buildings, each
being about 10 by 60 feet. were isolated
from the dormitories and the other build-
ings and there was no danger to the in-
mates. They were aroused by ore. of
the assistant superintendents and with
the institution's apparatus fought the
fire the best they could. The flames swept
through the factory and from there to
the storage house.
Plans for the new buildings were
quickly decided upon. Besides this, the
managers, on the part of the subscribers,
were elected. They wre: Edward Stab-
ler. Jr., Mr. Jauney, C. C. Magruder.
Nathaniel G. Grasty. H. S. Dalaney,
the Rev. George F. Bragg, Jr.. Dr. W. H.
Gibbons, Seneca P. Broomell, J. S.
Rawlings, A. T. Robinson, Lee B. Bol-
ton and J. II. Blanford. The officers for
the year will be elected March 7.
