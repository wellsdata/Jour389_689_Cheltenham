PEDESTRIAN
IS KILLED WHEN
AUTOS COLLIDE
Police Unable To Learn
Which Car Struck Miss
Edith Maser
BOTH OF DRIVERS
TO FACE CHARGES
Victim Was Crossing Park
Heights Avenue At
Time Of Crash
A 27-year-old woman, walking across
Park Heights avenue, at Violet avenue,
last night, was almost instantly killed
when two automobiles collided at the
intersection and one or both of them
hit her.
After questioning the drivers for
three hours, Northern district police
were unable to tell whose machine
had killed Miss Edith Maser, assistant
buyer in a downtown department
store, and charged both with having
caused her death.
Had Stepped From Trolley
Miss Maser, going from her home at
2117 Presbury street to visit her sis-
ter, Mrs. Charles Yaker, at 2814 Violet
avenue, had stepped off a northbound
trolley car and started to walk across
Park Heights avenue when the two
machines collided.
One, southbound on Park Heights
avenue, was driven by Albert W. Boss,
57, of the 3100 block Sumter avenue.
The other, operated by Edward J.
Offit, 29, of the 4600 block Park Heights
avenue, was eastbound on Violet ave-
nue.
After the crash, Miss Maser was
found unconscious in the trolley-car
tracks. She was taken to the West
Baltimore General Hospital by an-
other motorist, Morris A. Richmond,
of 3701 Park Heights avenue. She died
of a skull fracture a few minutes
after admission to the hospital.
Question Put Up To Coroner
The question of whose automobile
killed Miss Maser will be placed be-
fore Dr. Hubert Gurley, automobile
coroner, at an inquest November 20.
Several other persons were injured
in other accidents in and near the
city.
James Cannon, 51, of 3211 Bayonne
avenue, suffered a scalp laceration
which required eleven stitches to
close when his car overturned after
the steering wheel broke on the Crain
Highway near Cheltenham. He was
treated at the South Baltimore Gen-
eral Hospital.
Accused Of Drunken Driving
Ralph Grogan, of Fullerton, was
charged with driving under the in-
fluence of liquor after his automobile
allegedly sideswiped another car on
the East Joppa road, about two miles
from Towson. The other machine was
driven by Edward Brown, of Media,
Pennsylvania.
Grogan was arrested by Patrolman
Martin Chenoweth, who stopped him
as he drove into Towson.
Boy Knocked From Bicycle
A 16-year-old Negro was seriously
injured when he was knocked from
his bicycle by one car and struck by
another automobile as he fell. The
accident occurred on Myrtle avenue,
near Lanvale street.
Elwood Ross, 1100 block Stockton
street, was bumped from the bicycle
by a car driven by Samuel Skurow,
3000 block Oakmont avenue, according
to police. The second automobile
which struck the boy was coming
from the opposite direction, and was
driven by William Williams, Negro,
700 block Barnes court, police said.
Ross was taken to the University
Hospital, where physicians said he
had suffered severe skull injuries.
Williams was charged with driving
without an operator's license. No
charges were placed against Skurow.
Three Escape From Fire
Three persons escaped with slight
Injuries from a burning truck which
had struck a pole and overturned on
Dulany Valley road, several miles
north of Towson. The vehicle was de-
stroyed by fire.
Blair F. Kluger, his wife and infant
child, of Sunbury, Pa., were treated at
the office of Dr. R. O. Sellman, of
Towson, for shock and slight cuts and
bruises.
Kluger told Sergt. Talbott Kelly, of
Towson police, he had lost control of
the vehicle on a curve. The owner of
the truck, Vincent Palmayano, also of
Sunbury, who was following in an-
other truck, took the Kluger family
homes.
The Towson Fire Department ex-
tinguished the blaze.
Aided By Grid Team's Doctor
A doctor attending a football team
playing in Herring Run Park found
his services utilized by a boy who was
injured in an automobile accident.
Samuel Brocato, 7, of the 4500 block
Harford road, was hit by a car driven
by John P. Jenkins, 21, of the 2300
block Southern avenue, a player who
had just left the game.
Jenkins took the lad back to the
field, where Dr. Joseph Castagna,
physician and coach of the Iroquois
team, took seven stitches in a cut over
the boy's left eye. Samual later was
taken to St. Joseph's Hospital.
