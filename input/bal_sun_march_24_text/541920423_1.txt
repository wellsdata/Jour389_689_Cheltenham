Calvin L. Kiah will again become
superintendent of the Cheltenham
School for Boys on Friday.
His appointment to the post
which he left last February is
temporary, pending examination by
the State Employment Commis
sioner.
He previously served under a
former board of managers which
rejected him for permanent ap-
pointment at the end of a six-month
probationary period.
The new appointment was made
Thursday by the present board of
managers, composed entirely of
Negroes. at a meeting at the
school. The vote was 5 to 0, with
the president and secretary of the
board abstaining. Two members
were absent.
