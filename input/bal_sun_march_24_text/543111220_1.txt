BY 125 IS QUELLED
State And County Police Arc
Called As Boys Smash
Windows, Benches
Two Alleged Ringleaders Are
Put In Solitary After Un-
explained Revolt
Special Dispatch to The Sunl
Marlboro, Md., Sept. 15-Heavily
armed squads of State and local police
were rushed to the nearby Chelten-
ham School for Boys tonight to quell
Ja riot of inmates.
The disturbance had been ended,
about 125 boys participating had been
herded into a cottage and two alleged
ringleaders placed in solitary confine-
ment by attendants by the time the
officers, armed with shotguns and
tear gas, arrived.
Begins At Lunch
The mutiny began at lunch, when
hoys living in two cottages at the
Negro Reformatory began hurling the
luncheon dishes at each other. At
dinner they refused to say grace, then
surged into a yard, smashing windows
and breaking up playground benches.
B. B. Sunderland, superintendent,
said the damage to the institution was
slight.
Blames "Troublemaker"
He described one of the boys placed
in solitary as a chronic troublemaker,
and said the other was being held on
suspicion of helping incite the riot.
Neither he nor the police who an-
swered the riot call could ascribe any
definite reason for the outbreak.
Police, who said the food was good
and the discipline not severe, said
they believed it was "just plain
devilment."
