The State's juvenile delinquency
problem boils down to the fact that
there are not sufficient facilities for
mentally retarded children, it was in-
dicated last night by heads of insti-
tutions that were criticized at a Gavel
Club open forum,
The only institution equipped to
care for juvenile public wards of
feeble minds is Rosewood State Train-
ing School, and there is a waiting list
of about 200 at that institution.
Rosewood is the only place per-
mitted, under fairly recent regula-
tions of the Board of State Aid and
Charities, to take mentally retarded
cases.

Situation At St. Mary's
Brother Gerard Joseph, superin-
tendent of St. Mary's Industrial
School, which formerly received num-
bers of these cases, revealed the exist-
ence of this rule,
"Recently," he said, "we have been
raising our standards to comply with
the new ruling of the Board of State
Aid and Charities which says no in-
stitutions that care for normal chil-
dren may take in mentally retarded
individuals, who might be bad in-
fluences upon the normal children.
"The rule relieves us of the neces-
sity of receiving cases we are not
equipped to handle, and for whom we
can do nothing. In addition, it gives
our normal charges better chances for
rehabilitation, since they are not in
contact with the mental cases."

Stand Taken By Stanton
At the Gavel Club forum, Monday
night, Robert F. Stanton, Commis-
sioner of Police, said the Juvenile
Court often turned loose children
who should be in custody. These chil-
dren, he asserted, were "repeaters"
in crime, and gave the police much
trouble.
Dr. James W. Harry, supervisor of
probation for the Juvenile Court,
rose to the court's defense, saying it
often had trouble in getting institu-
tions to accept "repeaters," most ef
whom were mental cases.
Some of the speakers contended the
State needed a committing agency
that could force the institutions to
accept whatever cases were sent to
them.

Last night, Dr. Harry said, he felt
the solution was the appropriation
of more funds, either to build more
facilities at Rosewood, or to estab-
lish another institution for mentally
retarded juveniles.
