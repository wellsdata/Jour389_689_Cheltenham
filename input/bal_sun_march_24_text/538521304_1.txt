Vote Fraud Cases Cited
In connection with its investigation
Into the alleged fraudulent vote count-
ing in Fourth district precincts during
the election, the report said:
"We exhausted every possible means
of information regarding the irregu-
larities in the last general election,
and recounted the ballots in an effort
to fix responsibilities for them.
Fraud Admitted
"In some precincts these were due
in our judgment to fatigue and not to
fraudulent intent. In other cases, un-
doubtedly fraud was practiced, and
while the evidence pointed toward cer-
tain persons as being guilty, there still
remained, however, as to them, a
reasonable doubt in our minds.
"Under such circumstances we were
unwilling to make presentments which
might result in placing the stigma of
a criminal prosecution on innocent
parties."
Accidents Referred To
Referring to automobile accidents,
the jurors reported:
"The ever-increasing toll of death
and serious injuries due to automobile
accidents has been of grave concern to
us. While doubtless just how to di-
minish the number of such accidents
is a matter of continuous study by the
proper authorities, we recommend:
"1. A simplification of traffic laws to
suit present conditions.
"2. Such reorganization of Traffic
Court and the Automobile Com-
missioner's office as my be neces-
sary to make readily available
at the Traffic Court, as part of his
trial, the record of each traffic
offender.
"3. We recommend that the license
to drive of chronic offenders be
taken from them. The law should
be changed so that the person
seeking reinstatement of his li-
cense should prove beyond a rea-
sonable doubt, his fitness to ex-
ercise this privilege.
"4. In view of the conflicting testi-
mony as to facts easily ascertain-
able at the time of a crash, or
shortly thereafter, we recommend
that in serious cases certain of the
police be assigned to investigate
and file written reports of such
accidents."
Sherman Praised
In addition, the foreman's report
commended the Police Department
and the State's Attorney's office, "in
particular the workings of the office
dealing with non-support cases which
has been so ably, administered by
John A. Sherman and Albert H. Blum
during this term." Mr. Sherman and
Mr. Blum formerly were Assistant
State's Attorneys,
Concerning State and city institu-
tions, the foreman suggested that "the
State and City Forester be instructed
to survey all the State and city insti-
tutions and funds be provided to plant
wherever desirable forest trees and
native shrubs from the State nurs-
eries."
Teacher Needed
The grand jury committee appointed
to investigate the Montrose School for
Girls reported that an instructor to
teach handicraft was needed, as that
department was closed. The former
instructor received only $5 a week,
which "is not sufficient compensation,"
the report stated.
The committee which investigated
the House of Reformation for Colored
Boys at Cheltenham reported that it
could not "condemn too strongly the
health conditions, especially tubercu-
losis," there. It asserted boys with tu-
berculosis should be transferred to
Henryton for proper treatment.
Prison Committee Reports
The Penitentiary committee report
disclosed that of the 276 inmates at
the State penal farm at Roxbury, in
Washington county, the average age
was 21 years and that the average
school grade reached by the inmates
was the fifth.
The report showed also that during
its four-month term of office the jury
acted on 1,630 cases. Of the defendants
in these, 1,491 were indicted, 122 dis-
missed and the remainder reconsid-
ered and ignored.
Of the indictments 15 were for mur-
der; 12, manslaughter; 121, false pre-
tenses and forgery; 126, assault and
assault and robbery; 297, burglary; 41,
liquor without license; 547, larceny;
64, gambling; 8, bets on races; 36, re-
ceiving stolen goods, and 74, non-sup-
port. Witnesses summoned to appear
before the jurors totaled 3,604, it was
disclosed.
