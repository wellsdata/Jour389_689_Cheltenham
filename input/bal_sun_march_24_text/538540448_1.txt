The Cheltenham School for Boys
will be staffed "as soon as practi-
cable" entirely by Negroes.
The order to replace present
white teachers and other employes
with Negroes was entered in the
form of a resolution by the school's
board of managers at a meeting
yesterday afternoon.
The meeting, originally sched-
uled for next Thursday, was moved
ahead for the convenience of some
of the members.
:
To Include Superintendent
Asked if the order included the
position of superintendent, Howard
C. Hill, acting president, replied:
"Yes, eventually."
The new move is meant to end
the controversy that reached a
climax more than a month ago
when the board adopted a resolu-
tion to end the segregation of white
and Negro employes at the school.
In its resolution yesterday it re-
affirmed the principle which it had
set forth at that time.

Opportunity For Transfer
Mr. Hill said the resolution
means that the white employes will
be given an opportunity to be
transferred to other institutions
or to seek other. employment be-
fore the replacements are com-
pleted.

All Members At Session
Mr. Hill said all nine members of
the board of managers attended
yesterday's meeting. He said he did
not know until after the meeting
that Governor O'Conor had ap-
pointed Joseph P. Healy to member-
ship on the board.
The controversy that raged since
the board's resolution of March 25
resulted in the resignation of
Joseph H. Blandford, president of
the board.
Resignations of several employes
followed. The latest of these, re-
ported to Mr. Hill, are Leroy
Chisholm, teacher of plumbing, a
graduate of Hampton Institute,
and Walter C. Hones, elementary
teacher for the last five years, who
charged that Vance E. Thomas,
superintendent, "bungled the
chance" to settle amicably the
racial segregation problem.
