Kirkman Urges New Plants Or
Annexes For Difficult Cases
Two alternative methods for han-
dling especially difficult juvenile
offenders in Maryland were placed
before the Legislative Council yes-
terday by Walter N. Kirkman, State
budget director.

The proposals, for the housing
of an estimated 100 white and 100
Negro
serious youthful delin-
quents, were:
That a new and separate insti-
tution be erected.
That especially designed secu-
rity units be incorporated in the
present boys' training - school
plants.
Discussion of expansion of train-
ing-school facilities followed sev-
eral hours of discussion before the
council of the State liquor laws,
particularly as they apply to Balti-
more city.
Hearings Continued
Hearings on both subjects will
be continued October 3 at the next.
regular session of the council.
In presenting his proposal, Mr.
Kirkman said a new and separate
juvenile institution would be "very
expensive."
However, he said, the cost of a
new security unit at the Maryland

He said the security unit for

The State administration, he

In discussing the financial
aspects of these proposed im-
provements in the training-school
system, Mr. Kirkman said he had
consulted State officials concerned,
including J. Milton Patterson, di-
rector of public welfare; Harold E.
Donnell, superintendent of prisons,
and James A. Pratt, superintendent
of the Maryland Training School
for Boys.
Separate Handling
These men, he said, were agreed
that construction of security units
at the two boys' institutions would
best fill the gap in the training-
school setup.
He said he had been advised that
use of buildings at Montrose
School for Girls and the Maryland
Training School for Colored Girls
could be altered so that the limit-
ed number of particularly difficult
girls committed to those institu-
tions could be handled separately.
The consensus among officials
with whom he talked, he said, was
that care of delinquents under 16
was definitely a training-school
problem.
Final action on any new con-
struction rests with the Department
of Public Welfare and the Board of
blic Works.
