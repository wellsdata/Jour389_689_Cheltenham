Boys Held For Arson
In Reformatory Fire
Trio, Inmates At Cheltenham In-
stitution, Said To Admit
Causing Blase
Three inmates of the House of Ref-
ormation for Colored Boys, at Chelten-
ham, Md., have admitted starting a
fire in the broom factory of the insti-
tution March 10, the police said yester-
day.
4
Joseph F. Dougherty, investigator
for the State Insurance Department,
said that he gained admissions from
the boys, but that they denied causing
another blaze in the broom factory
several weeks previously. The last fire
did considerable damage.
The youths, George Johnson, 16, of
Baltimore; Thomas Dashields, 17. of
Somerset county, and Granville Has-
kill, 17, of Kent county, were held at
the Marlboro Jail charged with arson.
