Three Cheltenham
Escapees Captured
| Sunderland, Md., Sept. 6 (AP)-
Three youthful fugitives from the
Cheltenham Training School for
Negro Boys were recaptured at
gunpoint today by a garage owner
with whose automobile they were
tampering. State Trooper Walter
Wahl reported.
Thomas Blake, owner of Blake's
Garage, was roused by a noise
early this morning and encoun-
tered the trio busily engaged with
the automobile. Keeping the lads
in check with a rife, he notified
the State trooper and Sheriff Roy
C. Howard.
Wahl identified the escapees as
Elvon Garrison, 15;
Cornelius
Scott, 14, of Baltimore, and Robert
Smallwood, 14, of Easton.
He said the boys evidently fled
the State correctional institution
sometime last night.
Bartender Freed On Bail
On Slot-Machine Charge
Paul Navgrocjis, 27, a bartender
in the 300 block of South Paca
street, was released yesterday on
$500 bail, pending action by the
grand jury on charges of permit-
ting a slot machine to be operated
for profit at that address.
He was arrested by Sergt.
Thomas Joyce, of the vice squad,
yesterday afternoon and pleaded
not guilty before Magistrate Pres
ton A. Pairo in the Western Police
Court.
