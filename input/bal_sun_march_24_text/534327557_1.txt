The Piscataway boys went down to defeat Sat-
urday. Cheltenham trimming them to the tune of
10 to 5.
Reed pitched an excellent game for Cheltenham,
allowing Piscataway seven hits, which were well
scattered. Both teams played a fine fielding game.
Mckie, at first. for Cheitenhain, and Hatton, at
second, for Piscataway, being the stars.
Cheltenham won the game by heavy batting in
the second and eighth innings. Score:
