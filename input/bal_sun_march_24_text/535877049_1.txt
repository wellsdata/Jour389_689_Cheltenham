THE LATE GENERAL HORN
Prominent Union Veterans To Act As
Pallbearers At His Funeral
Today.
The funeral of Gen. John W. Horn, for-
merly warden of the Maryland peniten-
tiary and superintendent of the House of
Reformation for Colored Boys at Chelten-
ham, who died at his farm at Mitchell-
ville, Prince George's county, on Monday,
will take place this afternoon at 2 o'clock
from the residence of his sister, Mrs. Mar-
garet Ferguson, 1128 McCulloh street.
The funeral will be in accordance with
the service of the Grand Army of the Re-
public, of which General Horn was past
commander of the Grand Army of the De-
partment of Maryland. Religious exercises
will be conducted at the home by the Rev.
Dr. X. Z. Wilson on the part of the fam-
ily and the Rev. Jacob L. Grimm, pastor
of Otterbein Church, on the part of the
Grand Army, of which Mr. Grimm is chap-
lain. The service at Loudon Park will be
in charge of Department Commander Geo.
W. Johnson, Gen. D. L. Stanton, Lieut.
M. A. Brian, Col. John E. Hough and
Capt. A. S. Cooper.
The active pallbearers on the part of the
G. A. R. will be Gen. David L. Stanton
and Capt. Frank M. Smith; on the part
of the Loyal Legion, Col. George W. F.
Vernon and Capt. John R. King; from the
Union Veterans, Capt. Robert M. Cameron
and George C. Irelan; from the Sixth
Maryland Infantry, Dr. Hugh A. Maugh-
lin and Capt. O. H. P. Mathias.
The honorary pallbearers will be: From
the G. A. R., Col. Theodore F. Lang, Dr.
George R. Graham, Capt. Frank Nolan,
Col. M. J. Rose, Capt. E. T. Daneker,
Capt. Louis W. Zimmerman; from Mary-
land regiments, Col. Wm. A. MeKillup,
of the Sixth Infantry; George R. M. Bow-
erman, of the Fourth Infantry; Gen. Jas.
M. Deems, of the First Infantry; Col.
Henry A. Cole, of Cole's Cavalry; Judge
Charles E. Phelps, Seventh Infantry; Col.
William Louis Schley, of the Fifth In.
fantry; Col. B. F. Taylor, of the Second
Infantry; Col. Joseph M. Seidsberg, of the
Third Infantry, and Capt. William Gib-
son, of the Purnell Legion; from the Loyal
Legion, Lieut. Noble H. Creager and Capt.
Peter Leary; from the Union Veteran Le-
giou, Charles L. Marbury and Henry
Streib; from the Sixth Maryland Infantry,
Louis Rosenberg and Ebenezer Harman.
Mr. Henry Eppers will be bugler.
The various pallbearers will meet at the
G. A. R. headquarters at 1.30 o'clock and
go in carriages to the McCulloh street
house. Other members of the various com-
manderies will asseinble at the entrance to
Loudon Park and be in line under their re-
spective different commanders at 2.30
o'clock.
A simple service wil be performed at the
grave. Instead of the former salute of
cannon,taps and the bugle will be sounded.
The members of the Loyal Legion met
at the Carrollton yesterday and passed
resolutions of condolence on the death of
General Horn.
