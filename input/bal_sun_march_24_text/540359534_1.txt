CENSURES SCHOOL.
AT CHELTENHAM
Grand Jury Calls Education
Farce-Urges Purchase By
City And State
MOVIE EXITS CRITICIZED
Closing Of Theaters Lacking
Safety Outlets Recom-
mended In Report
Charging that the education inmates
receive at the institution is a farce,
the retiring grand jury for the January
term yesterday criticized the manage-
ment and, conditions at the Chelten-
ham School for Colored Boys and reс-
ommended the purchase of the place
by the city and state.
1
The recommendation was contained
in a report to Judge Robert F. Stan-
ton in the Criminal Court. The grand
jury for the May term will be organ-
ized Monday.
Cites Sullen Obedience
Pointing out that Cheltenham was
privately owned, although aided by
city and state, the report said "history
fails to reveal a triumvirate that was
successful or lasted for any great
length of time." Discussing the pre-
vailing educational system, the re-
port continued:
"The method in vogue does not
awaken interest. It does not lead out
of the mind of the boss many latent
and useful qualities-many of God's
plans die because no teacher, improp-
erly prepared, can inspire respect and
love, but all he gets is sullen obedi-
ence.
"We recommend the purchase of the
place by the city and state and the
selection of trained Afro-Americans
who will give the boys selective work,
some recreation and much education."
Melvale School Called Poor
Of the eleven institutions visited by
members of the jury, Melvale School
for Colored Girls was declared the
poorest. The city and state, accord-
ing to the report, are falling short of
their obligation at the school and
should arrange for a new home.
The jurors praised the officials at
Melvale for the work they are accom-
plishing with the poor facilities.
Movie Exits Condemned
Hazardous exits in a number of East
Baltimore street motion-picture thea-
ters were criticized by the jurors. They
pointed out that if a fire should occur
in the picture booth of any of the
houses concerned the patrons would be
trapped.
The rear exits were declared to be no
exits at all, as they open on blind alleys
or alleys littered with boxes and other
obstructions. The report recommended
the closing of the theaters until proper
rear exits could be installed.
The jurors also pointed out the lack
of a regulation governing the number
of persons who can stand between the
rear seats and rear wall of a motion-
picture theater. They, recommended
that such a regulation be instituted.
