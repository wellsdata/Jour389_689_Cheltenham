Obituary
Dr. James Burch Joyce
Funeral services were held on
Tuesday for Dr. James Burch
Joyce, of 3809 St. Paul street,
retired physician and former mem-
ber of the city health department,
who died last Sunday after a short
illness. Burial was in Greenmount
Cemetery.
Dr. Joyce, who was 72, was a
graduate of St. John's College and
the University of Maryland. He
was a member of Grace Methodist
Church. Roland Park, and served
as layman to the Methodist Epis-
copal General Conference in Kan-
sas City in 1928. He also belonged
to a number of Methodist church
organizations.
He is survived by two daughters,
Mrs. Joseph T. Hazen, of Joyce,
Md., and Mrs. Frederick A. Weiss,
Jr., of Baltimore; two brothers,
Cyrus N. Joyce and Dr. John C.
Joyce, and a sister, Mrs. Walter C.
Munroe, all of Joyce. His wife, the
former Miss Flora Bolgiano, of
Baltimore, died nine years ago.
Charles C. Carver
Charles C. Carver, proprietor
since 1915 of the firm of Charles
F. Eareckson & Co., manufacturers
of sails, awnings and tents, died
Thursday at his home, 2801 Elsinor
venue.
Funeral services will be held at
11 A. M. today at funeral par-
lors at North and Pennsylvania ave-
nues. Burial will be in Druid Ridge
Cemetery. The Rev. Allen J. Miller,
of the Protestant Episcopal Church
of the Messiah, will officiate.
Surviving Mr. Carver in addition
to his wife, the former Marian
Gould Travers, is a sister, Mrs.
Richard W. Worthington.
Problem Boy Goes
To Welfare Unit
A 13-year-old Negro boy yester-
day was turned over to J. Milton
Patterson, of the Department of
Public Welfare, after the boy was
refused admission to Cheltenham
School for Colored Boys and
Crownsville State Hospital.
The action was taken by Judge
Charles E. Moylan, of the division
of juvenile causes, after he found
the boy to be a delinquent. Judge
Moylan announced that he had or-
dered the boy held in a station
house after efforts to place him in
an institution had been unsuccess-
ful. The judge pointed out that
the boy had been before the court
on various charges since August
1943 involving thefts of a re-
volver, a bicycle and some cash.
Judge Moylan explained that
under a special provision of the
new Juvenile Court setup, when a
minor is refused admission to one
of the State institutions, the case
is turned over the Department of
Public Welfare for solution. He
said he did not intend to release
the boy on probation since that
type of action had failed on pre-
vious occasions.
He revealed that the court psy-
chiatrist had reported that the boy
was not a fit subject for Crowns-
ville and that Cheltenham had re-
fused admission to the minor be-
cause of a low intelligence quotient.
