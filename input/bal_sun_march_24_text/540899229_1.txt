GRAND JURY
OFFERS UCB
PROPOSALS
Recommends That A
Commissioner Replace
3-Man Board
Naming of a commissioner of
employment to replace the present
three-man board in the administra.
tion of the affairs of the State Un
employment Compensation Board
was recommended in a special re
port submitted yesterday by the
retiring September-term grand
jury.
In a lengthy review of the ne
tivities of the UCB and a discus
sion of the large number of frauds
recently brought to light after a
series of articles appearing in
The Sun, the grand jury suggested
immediate action to prevent further
frauds.
It also urged replacement of un-
qualified personnel and a program
of publicity to acquaint employés
with the nature, purposes and
sources of the UCB fund.
Favor Amendments
In addition, the jurors said they
believed the present unemploy-
ment compensation law should be
amended and greatly strengthened,
and indorsed in principle the 23-
point program adopted by the
Legislative Council and presented
to the State Legislature.
The grand jury stated that when
it first took office last September,
it was called on to deal with a
large number of cases involving
frauds against the UCB.
"This situation," the jurors re
ported, "produced a feeling of
grave concern in the minds of the
jury, for fresh in our memories
were the allegations in the morn-
ing press of incompetency and vari-
ous abuses and laxities found in
the administration of the fund by
the Unemployment Compensation
Board."
Witnesses Were Questioned
The jury stated that it was then
decided that the public interest re-
quired an investigation into the op-
cration of the board. In furtherance
of the probe, a great number of wit-
nesses were questioned, the report
said.
The report continued, saying that
an aftermath of the recent war was
the closing down of many war in-
dustries and the resultant layoff of
workers. The UCB suddenly found
itself deluged with workers seeking

unemployment compensation, par
ticularly workers who refused to
seek other jobs at lower salaries.
Large numbers of discharged vet
erans also were added to this pool.
forming what the jurors termed
"complacent '52-20' club, whos
members were satisfied to take
vacation at public expense."
Employes Lacked Experience
Called upon to increase its per
sonnel, the board acquired a num
ber of incompetent employés whos
lack of experience was responsibl
for some erroneous payments, the
jurors continued.
"In our study of the unemploy
ment compensation law of the State
of Maryland," the report of the
jurors said, "the committee noted
the far-reaching negation of the
high purposes of the act, which
was conceived as a measure to as
sist those unemployed through no
fault of their own, and to encourage
employers to provide more stable
employment conditions,
"Contrary to those purposes are
payments, permitted by statute, for
voluntary quits, layoffs for mis
conduct connected with work and
willful acts endangering safety of
others. The result of our present
public policy is a weakening of
moral fiber in many individuals,
who seek recompense from the
State for not working.
"Not In Best Interests Of State"
"This deplorable state of affairs
probably can be attributed to dis.
interest on the part of the general
public, which up to now has seen
no immediate cause for concern..
"On the other hand, those indi-
viduals in our State, whose inter-
ests, whether individual or politi-
cal, lie with the cause of labor,
have been active over a period of
years to further all possible bene-
ties, which have found expression
in some phases of our State law
in our opinion, are not in the best
interests of the State and country.'
The jurors found that the UCE
is handicapped in the prompt de
tection of frauds due to the time
interval between benefit payments
and submission by employés o
earnings lists as required by law
The investigating body contended
that "prompt detection is a mos
effective deterrent."
Meet Three Times A Week
"In reviewing the activities o
the three members of the board
we have found that the board meets
three days each week to hold hear
ings on cases of appeals from de
cisions of reviewing referees.
"The present chairman has been
the most active member of the
board and, while he is eminently
qualified for this position and has
compiled an imposing record dur-
ing his years with the board, he has
found that his private business re-
quires as much time as he spends
in the business of the board. As far
as we have learned, the associate
members of the board have been
active mainly in the hearings of ap-
peals, with few or no administra-
tive assignments."
Higher Salaries Urged
The board recommended adop
tion of corrective measures ad.
vanced by the Legislative Council
subcommittee for benefit payments
for other than voluntary unemploy
ment. Higher salary classifications
to permit the best possible type of
personnel also were proposed.
Another recommendation of the
jurors was that employers immedi-
ately notify the board of all new
employés to help in the prevention
and prompt detection of frauds.
The report on the UCB was sub-
mitted by William J. Bullen, R.
Harwood Bagby, Edgar L. Heaver
and Edward F. Requard.
In the main grand jury report
signed by John B. Duvall, foreman,
the jurors noted the "deplorable
conditions" at the Cheltenham
School for Boys.
