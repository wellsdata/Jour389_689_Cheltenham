The Board of Public Works also

Other Funds Earmarked
Except for the public-buildings
item, however, money from the
General Construction Loan of 1947
is earmarked for specific projects
or repairs, and funds from earlier
loans are earmarked for particular
institutions.
In addition, the board is oversee-
ing expenditure of postwar funds
allocated last year to projects on
which some commitment had been
made prior to the decision to scrap
the old postwar program.
As of last July 1, the postwar
fund balance was $5.350.954.31, in-
cluding $888,120.27 in unallocated
monies.
Unexpended Funds
Early in the fiscal year, the State
Planning Commission turned back
to the board $201,817.85 in unex-
pended funds which had been set
aside for State aid to local subdivi-
sions for planning purposes. This
money was added to the unear-
marked postwar funds.
The postwar fund balance now is
$3,888,930.44. And even though
$616,004.40 of this is "free money,"
there is little chance the Board of
Public Works will issue a "come-
and-get-it" call to State depart-
ments.
Presumably it will use the bal-
ance in the same manner in which
it has proceeded to date-offering
assistance in each case only after
the department concerned has es-
tablished the merit of its project
and displayed an empty pocket.
