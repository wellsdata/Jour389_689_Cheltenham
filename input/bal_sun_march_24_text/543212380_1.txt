Blandford Is New Head
Of Cheltenham School
State Takes Over Reformatory
For Boys As Board Agrees To
Provisions Of Bill
The board of managers of the House
of Reformation at Cheltenham has ac-
cepted the provisions of the Chelten-
ham bill passed by the General As-
sembly, it was disclosed yesterday.
Joseph H. Blandford has been named
president of the board of the new
State-controlled organization, to be
known as the Cheltenham School for
Boys.
Enoch Harlan, president of the
board of managers of the old insti-
tution, said the reformatory for Negro
minors would become the property of
the State on October 1. Since its
foundation in 1870, the institution has
been operated privately. In addition
to Mr. Blandford, other officers of the
new organization are:
HAL B. CLACETT, vice-president.
JOHN E. MCCORMACK, secretary-treas-
urer.
THE REV. FREDERICK DOUGLASS and
WILLARD W. ALLEN, members of the
executive committee.
The board of managers of the old
institution also remain as members of
the new board.
