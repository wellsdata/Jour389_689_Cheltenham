APPOINTED HEAD
OF CHELTENHAM
Officer At Atlanta Peni-
tentiary To Take Charge
In Few Weeks
The board of managers of the Chel-
tenham School for Boys yesterday
named Vance Errett Thomas, 49, chief
parole officer at the Atlanta Federal
Penitentiary, superintendent of the
Negro reformatory in Prince George's
county.
Thomas is expected to assume the
$3,500-a-year superintendency within
four or five weeks after he finishes his
duties at Atlanta, said Howard C. Hill,
of Baltimore, president of the Chel-
tenham board of managers.
B. B. Sunderland, present superin-
tendent, will resume his former posi-
tion as chief parole officer when
Thomas takes over.
Thomas was second on a list of five
eligibles certified for the superintend-
ency by Harry C. Jones, State Employ-
ment Commissioner, after an examina-
tion taken by twelve applicants. The
board sent his name back to Jones for
final certification.
Native Of Kansas
Hill said. Thomas "has had a good
deal of experience in administrative
work, vocational placement and parole
work." Thomas is a native of Kansas
and was a parole and social worker
at the Petersburg, Va., Federal re-
formatory before going to Atlanta in
1937.
He is married and has two daugh-
ters, 6 and 11.
Cheltenham was taken over as a
State institution in 1937. It receives
Negro boys on commitment by any
court, Juvenile Court or magistrate.
Baltimore city or the counties from
which the Negrocs are committed
must pay the State $200 a year for
their care and training. The remaining
amount necessary is paid by the State
Treasury.
