Arraigns Reformatory Institutions
In Which Prisoners Contract
The Seed Of Disease.
TO THE EDITOR OF THE SUN-Sir: Are
people interested to know that our "re-
form" institutions have just claimed two
more victims from our country district?
Cheltenham has just sent home to us a
colored boy, weak and ill from tubercu-
losis. A year ago he went away. appar-
ently a sturdy boy. The penalty for his
boyish wrong doing will probably be his
life, and possibly the lives of his family
from the infection.
At about the same time word came from
the House of Correction that a man from
Crapo (12 miles away) had just died of
tuberculosis.
We have locked up these poor creatures
and laid down the conditions under which
they must live or dic. Therefore, we are
all
every citizen of Maryland - blood
guilty for the health-breaking and death of
our imprisoned awbreakers. There is
plenty of money to spend for worthy pur-
poses and some unworthy ones but not
a cent to house and train and care for
our prisoners in decency and kindness.
To have in Maryland prison institutions
where prisoners have enough care, out-
door work and receation to give them bet-
ter health-to give each man useful training
and discipline according to his physical
condition-to send them away from the
present life, with a short experience at
least in decent, healthful living-with the
feeling that their State has helped them.
while under restraint will this make
them worse or more dangerous?
Does anyone really think you "reform"
people by robbing them of health or even
