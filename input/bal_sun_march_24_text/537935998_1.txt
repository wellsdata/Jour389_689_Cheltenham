Court Hears Motion
To Quash Salary Suit
Decision On Litigation Over Chel-
tenham Employes Deferred
Until June 20
Upper Marlboro, Md., June 8 (AP)-
The Circuit Court took under advise-
ment today, until June 20, a motion
to quash a salary suit by Joseph K.
Cox, employe of Cheltenham School
for Boys, against the State Employ-
ment Commissioner.
Cox asked for a court order to com-
pel Commissioner Harry C. Jones to
pay salaries of twenty-four employes
of the school whom Jones held to be
"unfit for any State service."
Charles T. Leviness 3d, Assistant
Attorney General, argued that Judges
Joseph C. Mattingly and William M.
Loker, of the Circuit Court, were
without jurisdiction.
COTTMAN LOSES ON APPEAL
Ruling Revoking His Auto License
Is Sustained
A jury in City Court yesterday
affirmed a ruling of Walter R. Rudy,
Commissioner of Motor Vehicles, in
which he refused to restore the opera-
tor's license of J. Stewart Cottman.
The license of Cottman, a salesman,
of the 1000 block West Cold Spring
lane, was revoked permanently in
1931 after his conviction on drunken
driving charges. He contended he had
stopped drinking and was qualified
to drive an automobile again.
