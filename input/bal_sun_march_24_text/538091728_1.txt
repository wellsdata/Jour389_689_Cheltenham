CHELTENHAM
SECURITY UNIT
TO BE RUSHED
Temporary Place To Be
Provided To Handle
More Difficult Boys
Immediate action will be taken
to provide a temporary security
unit at the Cheltenham School, the
Rev. F. Reid Isaac, president of the
board of managers of the institu-
tion for Negro boys, announced yes-
terday, following a meeting of the
board..
At the same time the Rev. Mr.
Isaac announced acceptance of the
resignation of Royal A. B. Crump-
superintendent since September,
1944-from the superintendency of
the school.
He had been asked by the board
to remain, but insisted last week on
immediate action on his resigna-
tion, submitted in June.
H. R. Harris, recently named as-
sistant superintendent, was named
acting superintendent.
More Runaways Lately
Yesterday's decision by the board
to remodel one of the existing
buildings at the institution for the
housing of difficult offenders was
based on a "larger number of run-
aways recently," the Rev. Mr. Isaac
said.
He explained that the board had
been working on the problem for
several weeks, and that it had been
decided the need for a building pro-
viding more effective custody had
to be filed immediately without
waiting for construction of the per-
manent security unit outlined in
postwar plans for the school.
"The school has been getting
itself in shape to care for boys in
the older group, from 16 to 18,
who must be admitted under new
regulations," he said.
1
Many Defectives There
"Also, we still have a good many
defectives."
He said the building to be con-
verted will provide complete segre-
gation of its inmates from the
"open" program of the rest of the
school.
Facilities and program for school-
ing'and recreation will be worked
out later, he said, pointing out that
protection of the community from
the runaways was the pressing
problem.
Meanwhile, the board of mana-
gers of the Maryland Training
School for Boys continued its con-
sideration of the question of a suc-
cessor to James A. Pratt, superin-
tendent, whose resignation has
been before the board for several
months.
