O'Conor Suggests
$1,500,000 For 2
Chronic Hospitals
By THOMAS J. O'DONNELL
[Annapolis Bureau of The Sun]
Annapolis, Jan. 7-Governor O'Conor
-today recommended that $1,500,000 of
State funds from a $4,172,000 pro-
posed bond issue be set aside for the
construction of two chronic disease
hospitals, one on the Eastern Shore
and the other on this side of the bay,
to replace the numerous and much-
criticized almshouses in the various
counties.

The plan for the two new hospitals
provides that the counties maintain
them on a per-capita basis, with the
amount each would contribute gov-
erned by the number of persons sent
to the institutions.

Would Aid Baltimore
It was also recommended that some
of the money be given to Baltimore
city for use in the extension of its
City Hospitals, in order that addi-
tional accommodations might be pro-
pro-
vided.

The Governor's recommendation
was made on the advice of a commis-(
sion appointed by him to study plans
for the elimination of almshouses,
which have been described as poorly
managed, inadequate and unsanitary.

- A real fight may result in both
Senate and House on this proposal, as
it is anticipated that some of the
counties will object strenuously to pro-
viding funds for the maintenance of
institutions the operation of which
will be in the hands of the State.

Counties Give Money
Under the present, setup, the_in-
dividual counties provide the money
for and operate the almshouses. One
of the arguments against the new plan,
is that it probably will cost more per
person under State operation.'
The Governor also recommended
that $806,000 be earmarked in the bond
issue, for new construction at all of
the State's five hospitals for the men-
tally diseased.
