CHELTENHAM
PLANS VIEWED
New Superintendent Tells Of
His Program
A system for diagnosis and classi
fication of every person entering
the institution will be set up at the
Cheltenham School for Boys, ac
cording to plans of the new super
Intendent, Lawson Jerome Veney
who will take over his duties on or
before March 1.
Mr. Veney said yesterday that
the classification system he con
templates would not require addi-
tional personnel but would be done
by members of the staff as part of
their duties.
Classification committees, con-
sisting of the superintendent, his
assistant and the supervisors, have
been functioning in this manner for
years in institutions under the Fed-
eral Bureau of Prisons, he ex-
plained.
Wants All To Participate
Mr. Veney does not believe in
"a one-man school," but said his
idea is for "the entire staff to par-
ticipate on various levels. "There
must be delegation of authority
and responsibility."
Even "the youngsters them-
selves" would help with the plan-
ning, he continued, adding that
"sometimes types of programs
that we think are suitable for a
child from the adult point of view
do not appeal to the youngsters'
interests or fit his needs.
"The program should be geared
to fit the youngsters and the pro-
grams have to be flexible" because
different groups in the institution
at different times have different
needs.
Diagnostic Procedures
Under Mr. Veney's plan, diagnos
tic procedures for every youth
would include psychological and
medical examinations as well as so
cial workers' studies.
Such a system, he said, "will in-
sure the fact that the youngsters'
problems are not only fully known
to the staff but also understood by
them."
To give real help, "we have to
know the conditions which contrib-
uted toward making the child the
kind of youngster who gets in
trouble."
As To Training Period
He said that "for the average
youngster, two weeks should give
each staff member time to spend an
hour or more with him to decide
what can be done in the way of
training and treatment."
Thereafter. "each youngster
would be seen by supervising per-
sonnel at least every two months or
at any other time when any of the
personnel feel that the youngster's
program is not satisfactory for
him."
If one program does not fit the
child's need, he will be reclassified,
it was said.
Seeks Maximum Attention
Mr. Veney said a good classifica-
tion program aims at maximum pos-
sible attention for each youngster
and the establishment of proce-
dures to insure adequate training.
"Any institution that provides
merely custodial care is doing noth-
ing for the child but keeping him
out of the community," he declared.
"A training school that does not
fulfill the functions of training will
be a breeding ground for adult of-
fenders."
Mr. Veney said he could not
comment on reports of bad condi-
tions existing at Cheltenham be-
cause "I have had no opportunity
at all" to inspect the institution;
that he had last visited the institu-
tion two years ago "just for a few
hours" and not for the purpose of
inspecting it.
Wants To Study Problem
: "I understand there is a pretty
big job to be done there," he con-
tinued. "I want to study the pro-
gram, personnel and budget, then
move on from there to see what
progress we can make to insure
satisfactory training and treatment
for the youngsters under our care."
Mr. Veney said he did not know
how soon his proposed classifica-
tion program can be established.
"Staff training for participation
is necessary," he explained.
"However, I'm sure it can be
done."
Asks More Advise
He said he could not give more
specific plans "until I've had a thor-
ough opportunity to work with the
staff at Cheltenham, learn the con-
ditions under which they are work-
ing and the handicaps, as well as
the children and their needs.
"One thing I'm sure of-Chel-
tenham can be and will be the type
of training school of which the
people of Maryland can be proud,
"It will take a great deal of time
and work by the staff and the
people in the community" to ac-
complish this end, he added.
Regarding public responsibility,
he said "this is a publie agency,
and the people in the community
should be aware of developments."
