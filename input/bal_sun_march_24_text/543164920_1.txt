State To Give Tests
For Five Types Of Work
Applications Must Be Filed With
Employment Commissioner
By Sept. 30
Notice of examinations for five types
of positions in the State classified serv-
ice was given yesterday by Harry C.
Jones, State employment commis-
sioner. Although the date for the ex-
aminations has not been scheduled,
applications must be filed with the
commissioner by September 30.
The positions for which examina-
tions will be held are:
Elementary teacher, Cheltenham
School for Boys, open only to col-
ored applicants; salary. $720 a year.
Instructor in physical education, open
to white and colored applicants;
salary, $750-$900 a year.
Occupational therapist, open to white
and colored applicants; salary, $1,-
000-$1,200 a year.
Public health nurse, open to white and
colored applicants; salary, $1,500-
$1.800 a year.
Stenographer-secretary, open to white
and colored applicants; salary,
$1,200-$1.500 a year.
At present, vacancies exist only in
the first three positions, but vacancies
are expected to occur in the public
health nurse and stenographer-secre-
tary categories during the winter?
Bermuda Clipper Flight
Prevented By Weather
Imperial Airways Also Cancels
Hop Of Cavalier From
Island
Pan - American Airways yesterday
cancelled the regular flight of the
Bermuda Clipper from Baltimore to
Bermuda because of the generally bad
weather over the Atlantic.
Imperial Airways, whose flying boat
Cavalier was scheduled to fly from
Bermuda to New York, cancelled that
trip also, Pan-American officials re-
ported.
Some damage was done by high
wind and heavy seas at the Port
Washington base of the two air lines,
members of Pan-American's local staff
reported, 'although the damage was
minor compared to that suffered at
other points on Long Island.
2 Sentenced On Charge
Of Passing Bogus Bills
One Defendant Given Year And
Day, Other Three Years.
Both Fined $100
Nicholas V. Ferracci and Natale
D'Ambrosio were found guilty yes-
terday by Judge W. Calvin Chesnut, in
the Federal Court, of passing counter-
feit $10 bills both here and in Hagers-
town last summer.
Ferracci, who was arrested when he
tried to pass one of the notes in pay-
ment for whisky in a South Baltimore
saloon, was sentenced to a year and a
day with a $100 fine, and D'Ambrosio
drew three years with a $100 fine.
