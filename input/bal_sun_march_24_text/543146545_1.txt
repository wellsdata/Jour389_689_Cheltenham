Negro Sent To Jail
After Year At Cut
Term Doubled By Magistrate Eck-
hardt For Violation Of
Traffic Laws
+
A Negro said to have been released
from the House of Correction nine
days ago, after serving a year for auto-
mobile larceny, yesterday was sen-
tenced to serve two years in jail on
similar charges.
He was James Miller, 21, of the 100
black West Preston street. Magistrate
George Eckhardt, Jr., sentenced him
(in Traffic Court to serve a year for
unauthorized use of an automobile, a
year for operating the automobile
after his application for a license had
been refused, to run consecutively, and
Gued him $5 and costs on each of two
minor charges.
Miller was arrested by the crew of
a radio cruiser yesterday morning nine(.
blocks from the home of David Dogo-
loff, of the 1900 block North Payson
street, whose machine he was said to
have stolen a few minutes before. He
was picked up for speeding.
Governor To Meet
Cheltenham Group
-
Will Confer With Committee Rep-
resenting Board Of Managers
Next Wednesday
Annapolis, May 27 (P)-Governor
Nice today arranged to meet a com-
mittee representing the board of
managers of the Cheltenham School
for Boys at his office here Wednesday.
The meeting will be held at 3 P. M.
after the meeting of the Board of
Public Works to sell $900,000 in State
Office Building bonds.
The Governor recently served an
ultimatum on the board of the insti-
tution. He demanded that it either
reorganize the school as recommended
by Harry C. Jones, State Employment
Commissioner, submit a satisfactory
alternative plan, or resign.
