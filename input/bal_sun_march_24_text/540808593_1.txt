A six-year capital improvement pro-
gram for State agencies and institu-
tions, calling for a total expenditure
of $9,334,270, was recommended by the
State Planning Commission in a 169-
page report issued yesterday.
The report, compiled by the commis-
zion in collaboration with the De-
partment of Budget and Procurement,
covers the most urgent needs of the
State in the fiscal years from 1941 to
1947.
It recommends to Governor O'Conor
and the 1941 General Assembly that
$4,172,000 be provided for the fiscal
biennium beginning October 1, and
that the State figure tentatively on
spending $1,722,560 in 1943-44 and 1944-
45 and $3,439,710 in 1945-46 and 1946-47.
From Agency Requests
These figures, the report stated, were
worked out from requests of the
agencies and institutions themselves,
which asked altogether for $18,509,051
for the six-year period.

Largest Items For 1944
Largest items in the 1943-44 and 1944-
45 biennium are $592,000 for State men-
tal hospitals. $252,000 for Rosewood
State Training School, $180,000 for
Morgan State College, $146,000 for the
Cheltenham School for Boys and $164,-
000 for tuberculosis sanatoria.
In the 1945-46 and 1946-47 list are
$1,626,000 for mental hospitals, $6-12,-
500 for Rosewood, $266,000 for Chelten-
ham, $268,000 for Maryland Training
School for Boys, $208,500 for Maryland
Training School for Colored Girls and
$428,000 for Morgan State College.
Both the lists for the second and for
the third bienniums in the six-year
period will be subject to review two
years hence.
To finance the immediate $4,172,000
program, the State Planning Commis-
sion recommends a bond issue for this
amount to be authorized by the cur-
rent General Assembly.
$33,865,000 Debt
This would bring the State bonded
indebtedness in 1943, after bonds com-
ing due in the meantime have been
retired, to $33.865,000.
But, while the report recommends a
new bond issue, criticizes the State's
policy in borrowing in the past and
recommends that an attempt be made,
when a sufficient total of outstanding
honds have been retired, to reach a
pay-as-you-go basis on capital im-
provements.
The report was prepared by Thomas
F. Hubbard, of the Johns Hopkins
School of Engineering, assisted by the
consultant staff of the National Re-
sources Planning Board, headed by
Melvin E. Scheidt, senior planning
technician.
