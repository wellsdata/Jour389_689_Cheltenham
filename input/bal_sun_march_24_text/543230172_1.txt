Suit For Cheltenham
Wages Is Dismissed
Court At Upper Marlboro Says Ac
tlon Should Have Been Filed
In Baltimore
Upper Marlboro, Md., June 20 (AP)-
The Prince George's County Circuit
Court dismissed without prejudice to-
day proceedings to compel the Mary-
land State Employment Commissioner
to pay the salaries of twenty-four
employes of the Cheltenham School
for Boys.
Presiding Judge Walter J. Mitchell
said State officers should be sued in
the jurisdiction in which their offices
were located. He said the court's ac-
tion was not based on the merits of
the case.
Attorneys for the plaintiff, Joseph
K. Cox, an employe of the school,
said a similar suit would be filed in
Baltimore.
