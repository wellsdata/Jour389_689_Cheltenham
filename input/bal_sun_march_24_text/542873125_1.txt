Grand Jury Sees
Cheltenham Head
Governor O'Conor, other mem
bers of the Board of Public Works
and Thomas B. Sprague, new head
of the Cheltenham School for Boys,
yesterday appeared before the Bal
timore grand jury.
Supreme Bench rules forbid
court officials or grand jury wit-
nesses from disclosing the purpose
or the nature of their appearance
before the investigating body.
Cheltenham School is one of the
State institutions which each Bal-
timore grand jury visits and inves-
tigates.
In addition to Governor O'Conor,
the grand jury also was visited by
J. Millard Tawes, State comptrol-
ler; Joseph O'C. McCusker, chief
deputy comptroller, and J. Hooper
Miles, State treasurer.
Cheltenham Repairs Approved
Approval of an expenditure of
