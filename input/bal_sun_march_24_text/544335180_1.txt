The Board of Estimates yesterday au- |
thorized the Supervisors of City Chari-
ties to increase from $150 to $180 per
capita the annual allowance for the
maintenance of boys and girls com-
mitted to reformatory institutions as
city charges.
ر
If the increase dates from January
1 last it will amount to $22,800 for
1923. It will be taken from the Board
of Estimates' contingent fund.
Institutions affected are St. Mary's
Industrial School, House of Good Shep-
herd, Mount street; House of Good
Shepherd for Colored Girls, Calverton
road; Industrial School for Colored
Girls, Melvale, and the House of Refor-
mation for Colored Boys, Cheltenham,
Prince George's county.
There are 760 city boys and girls in
these institutions.
Appeal For St. Mary's.
•
The increase of $30 is a result of an
appeal from St. Mary's Industrial
School for more money for the care of
323 city boys in that institution. St.
Mary's requested $200 per boy, but T.
Foley Hisky, representative of the
school, said it could get along with $180,
the allowance to the Maryland School
for Boys, Loch Raven, and the Mary-
land Training School for Girls, fixed by
the Legislature.
All institutions should be treated
alike, the board said.
Albert G. Towers, president of the
Board of Supervisors, said he would
favor the increase, and it is expected |
to be approved at a meeting of the
board next Friday.
In their budget for 1923 the super-
visors requested $200 per capita for
the city boys and girls in reformatories.
The Board of Estimates cut it to $150.
More Funds Needed For Sick.
N. G. Grasty, secretary of the super-
visors, called the attention of the Board
of Estimates to the fact that the year's
appropriation for the care of sick in
hospitals under contract with the super-
visors would be spent by October.
!
He said an additional appropriation
of $36,000 would be needed to take care.
of the patients until December 31. The-
situation, Mr. Grasty said, was the re-
sult of a cut of $36,000 by the Board of
Estimates last fall in the allowance for
hospital patients. It will be necessary
to make the additional appropriation
out of the contingent fund.
Care Of Insane.
Mr. Grasty also said the Supervisors
faced a serious situation in the care of
the insane. The hospital for the insane
at Bay View is overcrowded, he de-
clared, and the Supervisors are con-
sidering the building of a barracks for
the overflow at a cost of approximately
$25,000.
The insane who are city and state
charges are increasing at the rate of
100 a year, Mr. Grasty said. The city
has 960 beds at Springfield, 560 at
Spring Grove and 270 at Mount Hope.
All are filled.
There are 94 on the Springfield wait-
ing list. The city pays these institu-
tions $125 a year per capita. A sim-
ilar payment is made by the State to
provide sufficient money for the proper
care of the patients.
