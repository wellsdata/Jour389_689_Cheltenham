Two charitable institutions yester-
day were named to share in the esti-
mated $60,000 estate of Enoch Harlan,
senior member of the law firm of
Harlan & Harlan and former president
of the board of the House of Reforma-
tion at Cheltenham, according to the
will filed in Orphans' Court.
Mr. Harlan died last Friday while on
vacation at St. Petersburg, Fla., He
Deft $100 each to the trustees of the
McKim Free School and the Boys'
Home Society of Baltimore, for which
he served as a member of the board.
Four friends were named to re-
ceive $100 each and Mr. Harlan's four
children each were to receive $1,000.
The residue was left to his wife, Mrs.
Mary B. Harlan, who was designated
executor of the estate. Mr. Harlan lived
at 2014 Garrison Boulevard.
Navy Awards Contract
For Laboratory Annex
Buildings Near North Beach, Md.,
To Be Erected At Cost Esti-
mated At $100,000
Washington, May 7 (AP)-The navy
has awarded a contract estimated at
$100,000 to the Charles H. Tompkins
Company, Washington, for construc-
tion of the Chesapeake Bay field annex
and testing laboratory near North
Beach, Md.
The annex will be operated in con-
nection with the Naval Research
Laboratory at Washington.
The contract covers construction of
quarters of a resident officer, a dormi-
tory, laboratory building and a guard
house.
