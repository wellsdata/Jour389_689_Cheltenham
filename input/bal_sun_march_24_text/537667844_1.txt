Plans Under Way
For Cheltenham
Governor O'Conor has disclosed
that negotiations are under way
for purchase of a site for construc-
tion of the projected new Chelten-
ham School for Boys.
"The State is even now making
final arrangements for the purchase
of a new location," he said, adding
that the new school would be erect-
ed as soon as building conditions
permit.
Present facilities at Cheltenham
will be turned over to the De-
partment of Mental Hygiene for
the care of colored feeble-minded
children.

Ad Club Plans Party
For Needy Children
Approximately a thousand tickets
have been distributed for the Ad-
vertising Club of Baltimore's an-
nual Christmas party for under-
privileged children to be held at
10 A.M. Sunday at the Hippodrome
Theater.
The tickets, including provisions
for free transportation, were
handled by the Babies Milk Fund
Association and the Children's
Fresh Air Society. E. Lester
Muller, who plays Santa Claus an-
nually to the needy children, is
chairman of the party. The
children will be entertained by a
motion picture and several vaude-
ville acts and will receive toys,
fruit, candy and five pennies from
Santa Claus.
The Advertising Club's annual
Christmas party for members and
their guests will be held at 12:15
P.M Saturday at the Emerson
Hotel.

Waverly Christmas Party
Boys and girls of two Waverly
organizations will be guests at a
Christmas party to be given by the
Square and Star Club of Waverly at
8.15 o'clock tonight in the parish
house of St. John's Episcopal
Church, Greenmount avenue and
Thirtieth street. The guests will
come from the Waverly Assembly
No. 4, Order of the Rainbow for
Girls and the Tuscan Chapter,
Order of DeMolay.
