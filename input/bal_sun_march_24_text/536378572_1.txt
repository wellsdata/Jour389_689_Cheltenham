DIRECTORS FOR CHELTENHAM
Council Committee Indorses The
Mayor's Nominees.
The City Council Committee on Police
and Jail met yesterday afternoon, and aft-
er a lengthy discussion decided to report
favorably on Mayor Hayes' ordinance to
appoint Gen. Felix Agnus and Hariy S.
Cummings, colored, City Directors of the
House of Refuge for Colored Boys at Chel-
tenham.
A contention arose in the committee over
the nomination of Cummings, and Mr.
Howser vigorously opposed him, wanting
to substitute the name of R. M. Hall, col-
ored, who is now on the grand jury. Mr.
Guyton favored the ordinance as intro-
duced. Mr. Howser's was the only vote
against a favorable report.
The ordinance will come up in the Second
Branch next Monday night, and a fight is
anticipated, as Major Venable and several
other members have declared their inten-
tion of voting against Cummings.
Building Inspector Preston's ordinance to
provide for municipal inspection and super-
vision over all electrical wiring will also
be reported favorably with several changes.
The annual salary of the two inspectors
is fixed at $2,500 and $1,500, respectively,
and it is provided that for minor electrical
work a permit is not necessary. Wiring
that is never exposed to view is also ex-
empt from inspection.
Two other ordinances known as the frame
extension ordinances were also passed
upon favorably. One of these gives the
Building Inspector power to compel all
frame extensions hereafter built to be as
nearly fireproof as possible. The other
authorizes the Building Inspector to make
as nearly fireproof as possible without tear-
ing them down all frame extensions here-
tofore erected. The Building Inspector is
given discretionary powers in both these
ordinances.
