Cheltenham School
Board To Meet Soon
Joseph N. Ulman, a member of
the board of managers of the Chelt-
enham School for Boys, said yester-
day the board will meet in an in-
formal conference "as soon as the
members can be brought together."
But the members will not, he
said, be required to act on the
resignation of Joseph H. Blanford
from the presidency of the board
because of the abolition of restric-
tions segregating the institution's
white and Negro employes. He
noted that Mr. Blandford tendered
his resignation to the Governor,
not to the board, and it is up to the
Governor to act on it.
Judge Ulman said he could not
say whether the change in policy
toward employes will be discussed
at the coming conference. Howard
C. Hill, vice-president of the board,
could not be reached for a state-
ment.
