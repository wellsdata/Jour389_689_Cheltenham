CHELTENHAM
HEAD SLATED
C. L. Kiah, Veteran And Edu-
cator, Likely To Get Post
Appointment of Calvin L. Kiah
a principal of Cambridge, Md., as
superintendent of the Cheltenham
School for Boys, Negro training
school in Prince Georges county.
was indicated yesterday at a joint
meeting of the institution's board
of managers with the State Board of
Public Welfare.
Prompt appointment of a super-
Intendent, thorough physical ex
amination of the school's plant by
the chief engineer of the State and
procurement of new buildings, pos-
sibly quonset huts, to serve as ad-
ditional staff quarters, dining room,
kitchen and vocational shops, were
decided upon at the meeting as
immediate steps to be taken.
Lane Attends Session
Governor Lane, who had asked
for a "full report" on conditions at
the training school, was among
those present at the discussions
held in the offices of the State
Department of Public Welfare. He
promised his co-operation in the
program to be undertaken.
Mr. Kiah, a 36-year-old veteran,
tops the eligible list from which
the Cheltenham board of managers
is expected to name the superin-
tendent at a meeting next week.
Thomas B. Sprague, president of
the board, explained.
A native of Princess Anne, Mr.
Kiah received a bachelor of arts
degree in 1932 from Morgan Col-
lege and his master's degree in edu-
cation in 1937 from Columbia Uni-
versity.
Training Cited
He served as a teacher in Upper
Marlboro from September, 1933,
to May. 1935; as principal at
Prince Frederick from September,
1935, to May, 1938; and became
principal at Cambridge in Septem-
ber, 1938.
H. R. Harris, who has been acting
superintendent since October, 1945,
will remain at the institution in his
permanent position as assistant
superintendent.
Mr. Sprague said that because of
Mr. Kiah's lack of institutional ex-
perience it was planned that the
Superintendent of the Maryland
Training School for Boys at Loch
Raven serve as an adviser for
several months.
