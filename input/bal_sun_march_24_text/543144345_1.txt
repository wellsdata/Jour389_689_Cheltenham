11 Jailed After Second
Riot At Cheltenham
Disorder Termed "Reactive Hang-
over" From Thursday's Trouble
At Reformatory
Upper Marlboro, Md., July 17 (AP).
The second riot in two days at the
House of Reformation, in nearby
Cheltenham, resulted today in the
jailing of eleven Negro youths whom
Superintendent Sunderland of the re-
formatory termed ringleaders.
Arthur Hepburn, superintendent of
police of Prince George's county, said
all 105 inmates of one cottage smashed
tables, benches and windows last night
in what he described as a "reactive
hangover" from a riot Thursday night
when 100 boys escaped. All were re-
captured.
Hepburn said the youths iailed prob-
ably would appear before Police Court
Judge George B. Merrick Monday.
State's Attorney Alan Bowie had not
determined what charges he would
lodge against them.
