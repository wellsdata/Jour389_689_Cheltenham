- MR. SMITH URGES HASTE
Wants Sites For Hospitals And Home
Selected Without Delay.
City Comptroller Smith had a confer-
ence yesterday morning with Mayor Hayes
in reference to the building by the city of
an infectious-disease hospital, a home for
consumptives and a parental home for a
certain class of boys now at the House of
Reformation for Colored Boys at Chelten-
ham.
As explained in THE SUN last week, the
city has an option on 130 acres of property
near Bayview Asylum, and the plan under
consideration is the erection of a colony of
buildings for the care of persons now
charges of the city. Comptroller Smith
suggested to the Mayor that on the first-
fair day this week the Mayor, Health
Commissioner Bosley, the members of the
Municipal Hospital Commission, members
of the Board of Supervisors of City Chart-
tles, himself and any others who may be
Interested in the project make a trip of
inspection over the land and select the
most avallable sites for the proposed build-
ings. The Mayor agreed that this step
should be taken as speedily as possible.
Mr. Smith said Inter that he belleved
there should be no, delay in carrying out
the idea of crecting these three buildings,
as the situation was urgent. "No better
Investment," he said, "according, to my
judgment, could be made by the city, and
it should be done at once. The land out
there is eminently fitted for such build:
ings, and there can be no reasonable ob-
jection to any of them. In fact, there is
no-one to object.
"I have urged the Mayor to name a day.
to go over the property with those infer-
ested in the plan, and he has agreed. I
suggested that the next fair 'day be se-
lected."
