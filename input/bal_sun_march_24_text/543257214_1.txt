MISLAID?
Among the consequences of dilatory tactics
at Annapolis in this legislative session one
of the most tragic would be the failure of the
- Legislature to act upon the bill, now pending,
by which the Cheltenham reformatory for
colored boys is to be taken over and admin-
istered by the State of Maryland. The bill
reposes in the Senate Committee of Finance,
where it has been for several weeks, although
no explanation has been advanced for the
delay in reporting a bill upon which agree-
ment has been reached between the State
and the Cheltenham board of trustees.
Experts in child welfare and criminology
are all agreed upon the necessity of passing
this bill. Chief Judge Samuel K. Dennis, of
the Baltimore City Supreme Bench, has said
that it is "a blot upon the State" that no
• adequate institution has been made available
- for colored boys who are either homeless or
delinquent. Thomas J. S. Waxter, former
judge of the Baltimore Juvenile Court, is
equally emphatic in asserting the need of
State control for Cheltenham. Committing
magistrates and officials who are forced to
send colored boys to an institution inadequate
to care for them are overwhelmingly in favor
. of this bill.
•
Nevertheless, it stays in committee for
reasons unascertained, although there are
_suspicions that the ancient prerogative of
"back scratching" is being indulged in the
legislative corridors: "You lick Bill X and
*I'll lick Bill Y." Only it would be rather
- shameful if colored boys, wards of the State
of Maryland, should be the victims of this
kind of "practical politics."
