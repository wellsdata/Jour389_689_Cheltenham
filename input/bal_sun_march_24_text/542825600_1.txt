ATHLETIC GEAR
GIVEN SCHOOL
Judge Moylan Urges More
Gifts To Cheltenham
In furtherance of the expanding
recreational program at the Chel-
tenham School for Boys, Judge
Charles E. Moylan, of the division
for. Juvenile Causes of the Supreme
Bench of Baltimore, yesterday pre-
sented $500 worth of athletic equip-
ment and games to the institution.
Money for the gifts came from
the North Baltimore Optimist Club
and the Downtown Lions Club,
Judge Moylan said, after he had
told the organizations the story of
an unused gymnasium building and
needs of Cheltenham.
Pool Table Also Given
Yesterday's
presentations
in-
cluded outdoor basketballs, volley
balls, sets of badminton, table ten-
nis, indoor and outdoor quoits,
boards for indoor games, checkers,
dominoes, and other games for the
younger boys in the cottages of the
school.
Judge Moylan announced also
that a hand-carved pool table, with
all equipment, had been contrib-
uted by Martin R. Crook, of Balti-
more.
The table will be delivered and
put into use as soon as it can be dis-
mantled, moved and reassembled,
the jurist said.
Other Gifts Requested
"There are some members of
the board of governors who can
afford to give the school other
tables," Judge Moylan said. "I can-
not understand how boards of gov-
ernors can meet month after month
and fail to realize that the job of
rehabilitation of these boys can-
not be accomplished just on the
taxpayers money, but only if each
man personally tries to help."
H. R. Harris, acting superintend-
ent, told the assembled staff and
student body how instruments for
a 26-piece band, provided more
than a year ago by the Morres
Schugar Lodge in Baltimore, had
been allowed to gather dust in an
attic until Judge Moylan insisted
they be put to use.
Bugle Corps Outfitted
Mr. Harris told also how Judge
Moylan had been instrumental in
inspiring the Downtown Lions Club
of Baltimore to provide instruments
for a Drum and Bugle Corps for
Cheltenham.
The 271 boys, ranging in age
from 12 to 18, who are now in the
school, were slicked up for the
occasion and gave an enthusiastic
demonstration for Judge Moylan
and members of his staff.
Choruses from the cottage dormi-
tories sang, and other youngsters
put on an impressive tumbling act
under the direction of Robert
Anderson, supervisor of recreation.
Boy Scouts Raise Flag
The small Boy Scout troop,
under Scoutmaster L. C. Chisholm,
conducted flag-raising ceremonies
with military precision, to the ac-
companiment of the drum and
bugle corps.
Judge Moylan, during the cere-
monies inside the gymnasium,
urged more and larger Boy Scout
troops to take advantage of the
1,200 acres which make up the
grounds of Cheltenham.
The judge also made a presenta-
tion of his personal trophy,
awarded to the winning basketball
team of the season, to the quintet
from Carver cottage.
Boys To See Orioles
Mr. Harris told the boys that he
hoped to arrange for twelve of
their number to see the Baltimore
Orioles play baseball once a month
during the season-with Mr. Harris
guaranteeing transportation, and
Doll Derr, loaned by the commis-
sioner of motor vehicles, organiz-
ing the program.
The privilege of being taken to
Baltimore to see the games would
be earned, Mr. Harris said.
Judge Moylan, commenting on
the record of runaways from Chelt-
enham which reached a high of 700
for one year, said:
"It seems to me that here we
have a chance to establish a new
track record. I'm told there is only
one home-made high-jump on the
track. We can't forget that some of
the world's greatest athletes have
been Negroes. I'm interested now
in building up a real track team."
No Escapes In April
Since the institution of the rec-
reational program, with equipment
provided, the run-away figures have
dropped from 117 last September
to a low of 13 in January, Judge
Moylan said. There was a climb to
17 in March, but up until last night
there had been no escape in April,
the jurist said.
"I can't send these boys here or
other boys and girls elsewhere and
just forget them," Judge Moylan
said. "Under the law they are still
the wards of the court and I stand
in loco parentis to them.
Action Vs. Talk
"I am told that no county judge
in the State has ever visited Chel-
tenham, and that in the face of
many condemnations from outside (
mothing has been done.
"I don't see why we should wait
for long-range planning when there(
are certain definite steps which can
be taken while interminable meet-
ings are being held."
Judge Moylan, members of his
staff, and other visitors were guests
at dinner in the home of Mr. and
Mrs. Harris.
