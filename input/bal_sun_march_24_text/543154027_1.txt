Herbert R. O'Conor, Attorney Gen-
cral, fired the first gun in the State's
legal battle with the board of the Chel-
tenham School for Boys by filing a
petition yesterday in the Circuit Court
for Prince George's county, asking to
have the board's suit quashed.
The suit was filed on behalf of
Joseph K. Cox, one of the Cheltenham
employes, to test the right of the State
to refuse to accept a group of more
than twenty employes classified
unfit for the State service.

which will be followed eventually by
a court argument of the State Employ-

Parley With Nice Planned
Meanwhile, a committee of the Ne-
gro reformatory's board prepared to go
to Governor Nice to discuss the whole
situation with him. It will comprise
Howard C. Hill, Dr. John O. Spencer,
M. Hampton Magruder and Joseph
Blandford, chairman,
The board contends that, in taking
over Cheltenham, the State is com-
pelled to receive its employes into the
Merit System, regardless of fitness.
The State refuses to accept them unless
they pass the same fitness test applied
to other State employes.
