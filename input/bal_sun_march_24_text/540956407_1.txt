CHELTENHAM
ACCORD SOUGHT
"Fairly Radical" Compromise
Move By Board Of Man-
agers Seen
Matter To Be Considered In
Light Of Recent Develop-
ments, Hill Says
A "fairly radical" compromise
move by the board of managers of
The Cheltenham School for Boys to
settle the controversy over its re-
cent order to abolish segregation of
white and Negro employes was seen
yesterday as Howard C. Hill, acting
president, planned to call a special
meeting at the school before the
end of the week.
Mr. Hill said the matter would be
considered in the light of recent
developments, particularly the re-
port of the Prince George's county
grand jury which described a "de-
plorable situation" and "chaotic
condition within the institution" as
a result of the board's order, but in-
dications were that the action would
fall far short of the grand jury's
recommendations that the non-seg-
regation order be nullified and
nineteen conscientious objectors
assigned to the staff and six regular
Negro employes be "promptly re-
moved."
"Issues Confused"
Meanwhile, at Philadelphia, it
was indicated the American
Friends Service Committee, as-
signor of the conscientious objec-
tors who, according to the grand
jury report, were "primarily re-
sponsible" for the action of the
board and the existing conditions,
was ready to cooperate in any solu-
tion, but "prefers not to act in any
way that would embarrass the
board."
Kenneth W. Morgan, educational
director of the Civilian Public Serv-
ice section of the Friends Service
Committee, said representatives of
the committee have been to Chel-
tenham to investigate the situation
and were expecting to be called
"very soon" to a meeting of the
board of managers.
Mr. Hill, however, announced it
was not planned to call represent-
atives of the Friends Committee to
the meeting, which probably will be
held Thursday or Friday.
"While there will have to be
something fairly radical done," he
continued, "it will have to be a
solution on which intelligent groups
can meet. If you dismiss the con-
scientious objectors, there will be
repercussions. There will be other
repercussions from some of the em-
ployes if the order is rescinded, and
still others if we seek to enforce it."!
