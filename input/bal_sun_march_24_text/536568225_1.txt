RIVER VIEW TILTING
Tournament Of Brave Knights On
The Banks Of The Potomac.
[Special Dispatch to the Baltimore Sun.)
WASHINGTON, Aug. 20.- Many of the best
familles of Maryland and Virginia were
represented in the throng of 4,000 persons
which witnessed the sixteenth annual tour-
nament at River View, Md., on the Poto-
mac river, this afternoon. Two thousand
five hundred residents of the District of
Columbia went to the tilting grounds on
the steamer Samuel J. Pentz. Fifteen
knights on prancing steeds tried their prow-
ess with the lance in amicable strife for
the honor of crowning the Queen of Love
and Beauty and her maids. The successful
competitor was Mr. W. L. Harron, Knight
of Washington Boy, and his choice of
queen was Miss Mamie A. Townsend, of
Cheltenham, Md. Each knight was al-
lowed to make three tilts at a series of
three rings. Mr. Harron caught all three
rings each time, as did also Richard C.
Cleveland, of Virginia, and G. A. Smith, of
Maryland. For this reason they rode over
again to decide who should crown the
queen, their score in the final match being:
Harron.....
Smith...
Cleveland.

After the principal tilting the ties were
ridden' off. This evening at 8 o'clock the
successful competitors crowned the queen
and her malds as follows:
Mr. Harron crowned Miss Townsend queen
of love and beauty; Mr. G. A. Smith crowned
Miss Lavina Lederer, of Accoteek, Md., first
maid of honor; Mr. Richard Cleveland
crowned Miss Johanna Highland, of Alex-
andria, Va., second maid of honor; Mr. G.
B. Blandford crowned Miss Tillie Row-
land, of Piscataway, Md., third maid of
honor. Each of these four knights re-
ceived prizes, and L. D. Love received the
fifth cash prize.
Officers of the Day-Chief marshal, Roger
I. Manning; heralds, Alonzo Kirby, Berja-
