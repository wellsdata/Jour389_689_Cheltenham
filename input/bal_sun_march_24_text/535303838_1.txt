For Southern Maryland Farmers.
Upper Marlboro, Md., Nov. 18. Mary-
land farmers living along the Pope's Creek'
branch of the Philadelphia, Baltimore and
Washington Railroad are to be visited next
week by a farmers' educational train, the
third to be operated in this section. The
train will begin its tour. November 27, and
in two days will visit Collington, Mulli-
ken, Leeland, Croome, Cheltenham, Upper
Marlboro, Brandywine, Waldorf, White
Plain, Cox, Lothair and La Plata. The
train will be in charge of Dr. Richard S.
Hill, director of Farmers' Institutes of
Maryland.
To Give Prizes To Boy Corn Growers
Hillsboro, Md., Nov. 18.--The Caroline
schoolboys' corn-growing contest will be
held at Ridgely Agricultural High School
November 24, when exhibits will be made
of corn grown and the records of the culti-
vation of acres entered for prizes will be
examined. About 30 boys have each grown
an acre in the contest, and the prizes to be
awarded aggregate more than $300. The
judges will be Professor Lane, of the
United States Department of Agriculture;
Prof. W. T. L. Taliaferro, of the Maryland
Agricultural College, and W. Oscar Collier,
of Talbot. Exhibits in the girls' contest in
cooking and sewing will be made at the
same time, and Mrs. T. J. Smithers and
Mrs. Cotta Davidson, of the household de-
partment of the Easton Fair, will award
the prizes.
