TO HOLD SESSION TOMORROW
Twentieth Century Club To Meet
At Hotel Emerson.
The Woman's Twentieth Century
Club will meet at 2.30 P. M. tomorrow
Jat the Hotel Emerson. Immediately
preceding the meeting, tht executive
board will arrange details for the open-
ing of the Rainy Day Fund to be used
for Christmas welfare work.
The annual subscription luncheon of
the club will be held at 1 P. M. Tues-
day at the Hotel Emerson. Amos W.
W. Woodcock, United States District
Attorney, will speak on "Poetry in
Politics," and Mrs. T. J. Copeland
will discuss "Woman's Influence at
Home and Abroad."
