FOR A COLORED BOYS' HOME
The City May Have An Institution
Of Its Own.
the
President Jeffrey K. Brackett, or
Board of Supervisors of City Charities;
Mr. John E. Dorsey, superintendent of the
House of Reformation for Colored Boys,
and members of the board of directors of
that institution appeared yesterday before
the Board of Estimates and for more than
an hour discussed the question of the city's
entering into a contract with the institu-
tion whereby a certain amount of money
would be appropriated for its use next
year. It was stated that there are now at
the Institution about 100 city charges, who
cost about $137 a year each for mainte-
nance, and the suggestion was made that,
the city should pay this sum for each boy
committed to the place by the city. In the
ordinance of estimates just passed $10,000
is set aside for the maintenance of colored
youths, and the question of entering into a
contract with the Cheltenham Home was
to be determined later.
Mr. Brackett made a suggestion that the
city shouid establish a parental home for
colored boys who are homeless or neg-
lected, but not vicious, and said that the.
lot of ground adjoining Bayview Asylum,
recently purchased by the city, would be
the proper place for its location. The Su-
pervisors of City Charities, he said, had al-
ready considered this plan and thought
well of it., Messrs. Brackett and Dorsey
and Secretary Grasty, of the Supervisors
of Charities, were appointed a committee
by the Board of Estimates to make an in-
vestigation along this line and also to se-
cure data concerning the number of city
charges at Cheltenham who wouia de en-
gible for transfer to such a home. This
committee will submit a report to the
Board of Estimates, "after which the ques
tion of the contract will be determined.
