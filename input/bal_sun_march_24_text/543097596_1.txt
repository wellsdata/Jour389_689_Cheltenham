[Annapolis Bureau of The Sun)
Annapolis, March 21-The job of
Harry C. Jones, State Employment
Commissioner, teetered in the balance
tonight so far as the Legislature is
concerned.
By a vote of 6 to 6, with one member
not voting, the Senate Finance Com-
mittee tied on the question of whether
a favorable report should be returned
on a House bill that would legislate
Mr. Jones out of office and replace him
with a three-man policy-making board.
The Senate committee's vote indi.
cated that nothing has been done by
the administration forces of Governor
O'Conor to sidetrack the measure,
which has been described as a "ripper".
bill, holding dire possibilities for the
State's Merit System.
The Senate committee's vote on the
Jones bill was taken during a three-
hour session that body held tonight
in an effort to clear its files.

To Have Choice
Senator A. Raymond Marvel (Dem.,
Talbot) is the sponsor of a bill that
would require primary elections pre-
paratory to the filling of Congressional
vacancies. He will be given his choice
as to whether the measure shall die in
committee or be killed on the floor.
The Finance Committee voted for an
unfavorable report tonight.
Appearing at the invitation of the
Finance Committee, Mr. Jones this
afternoon told the Senators that he
rarely ever made an important move
in the administration of the Merit
System without consulting the At-
torney General. And, he added, the
Attorney General from whom he
asked for most of the opinions he has
on file to guide him was Governor
O'Conor.
Received Forty Opinions
"Where the previous Employment

asked for and received but one opin-
ion," he read to the committee from a
letter he had sent Senator Roe, the
chairman, "from 1935 to date I have
asked for and received forty opinions,
and I have conducted the Merit Sys-
tem strictly in accordance therewith.

ent Employment Commissioner with an
unpaid, three-man body, which would

During House debate, while the pro-

chamber, it was said that Mr. White
had introduced it solely because of Mr.
Jones' actions in attempting to get rid
of employes of Cheltenham Reform
School for Negro Boys, which is in Mr.
