Talkative Husband Tried
On Non-Support Charge
John Piscor Beconies So Loquacious
Magistrate Has Him Locked
In Cell.
If money talks, it had said nothing to
Mrs. John Piscor, she told Magistrate
Joseph J. Rettalinta yesterday in the
Eastern Police Court. It was her hus-
band who did the talking. That was
why she had him stummoned on a non-
support.charge.
Mr. Piscor denied some of his wife's
statements, but unwittingly comfirmed
to the magistrate's satisfaction what
she said about his talking. He, talked
so long and so rapidly that the magis
trate inquired if it would be necessary
to hit him over the head with a club to
stop him.
At that Mr. Piscor subsided. But
not for long. After his second outburst
the magistrate ordered him locked in a
cell to ponder such proverbs as "talk is
cheap" and "silence is golden."
Finally he was brought back into
court and dismissed.
"But even if your wife hits you with
a rolling, pin, for your own sake don't
say a word," was the magisterini nd
monition.

It was "almost Christmas.".
Judge T. J. C. Williams, of the Juve-
mile Court, was reminded anew of this
fact when he went to the courtroom
Festerday. The benches were deserted,
save four Negro boys, charged with
stealing from a store in the 1100 block
South Charles street
• And the judge was glad, for he "never
likes to punish boys around Christmas
time," he said.
Instead of sending the boys to the
Cheltenham Reformatory, the judge
paroled, them in the care of George
Ralph, Negro probation officer of the
court. Then he adjourned court until
January 4.
