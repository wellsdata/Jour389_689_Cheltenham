In the statement issued after its

Number Of Boys Whipped
"A check of the boys." the state
ment said, "disclosed that a num
ber of them had been whipped re-
cently in violation of the orders
of the board that no boy should be
whipped unless in the presence of
the acting superintendent."
The statement noted further that
a boy whose tonsils had been re-
moved last Thursday at Provident
Hospital was found Saturday "to
have been placed in the security
cottage without proper attention."
It said the board was "consider-
ing appropriate deciplinary action
against the staff members involved"
in the circumstances uncovered.
Further light on the situation in
the security cottage was offered to-
day by Mr. Reidt, who described
finding what "in my opinion was a
booby trap" in one of the unlighted
basement rooms and said there was
"every indication that the boys had
been using these rooms for a kan-
garoo court."
Supervisor Discharged
The "booby trap" consisted of a
door balanced at one end on a pipe
against the wall and held up at the
other on a piece of window sash.
One cottage supervisor was dis-
charged Sunday as a result of the
board's Saturday night inspection.
That inspection was described to-
day as the beginning of "a series of
inspections on the part of the board.
Jorking to needed improvements."
Before the Saturday visit the
board had called on the State's
new chief engineer, John B. Funk,
and the Department of Budget and
Procurement for a thorough study
of the institution's physical needs.
