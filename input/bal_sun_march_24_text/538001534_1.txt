Benjamin Talbott Ridgely, former
vice-president of the Black and
Decker Manufacturing Company and
well-known Baltimore county farmer,
died yesterday at his home, Barrett's
Delight, near Towson. He was 85.
Funeral services will be held at 1
P. M. tomorrow at the home. Burial
will be in the family cemetery.
A member of the old Talbott and
Ridgely families, he was the son of
Dr. Benjamin Rush Ridgely and Mrs.
Catherine Todd Ridgely. The Ridgely
farm has been in the family more
than 200 years.
Was Bank Director
Besides taking an active interest in
farming. Mr. Ridgely was a director
of the Cockeysville National Bank and
a director and vice-president of the
Black and Decker Manufacturing
Company at Towson.
Surviving members of the family are
his wife, Mrs. Elizabeth S. C. Talbott
Ridgely; two daughters, Mrs. James T.
Arrel, of Poland, Ohio, and Mrs. S.
Duncan Black, of Towson; two sons,
Neville R., of Towson, and Herbert
W. Ridgely, of Atlanta; fifteen grand-
children and six great grandchildren.
Special Board Convenes
On Cheltenham Post
To Function In Selection Of New
Superintendent For School
For Boys
The State's three-year attempt to
smooth out the operations of the Chel-
tenham School for Boys entered a final
phase yesterday when a special board
convened to review applications for
the Cheltenham superintendency.
The board was appointed by Harry
C. Jones, State Employment Commis-
sioner, to review the applications and
prepare and conduct the merit system
examinations. Fifty-one persons have
applied for the post, vacated when
Superintendent B. B. Sunderland
resigned.
When the new superintendent is
named, Mr. Sunderland will return to
his former post as chief parole officer
of the Southern Maryland Negro
Reformatory.
