Wilkerson To Speak
At Communist Meeting
In a Lincoln-Douglass anniver
sary meeting. sponsored by the
Communist party of Maryland and
the District of Columbia, Monday
night, Doxey Wilkerson, former
Howard University professor, will
be among the speakers. The meet
ing will be held in Baltimore
Garden, formerly the 104th Medi-
cal Regiment Armory.
Mr. Wilkerson is now a member
of the national committee of the
Communist party, U.S.A.; is a vice
president of the International La-
bor Defense, and instructor in the
Carver School and the Jefferson
School.
Other speakers will be William
Z. Foster, chairman of the Com-
munist party, U.S.A., and Elizabeth
Gurley Flynn, also of the national
Communist committee.
Muriel Rahn, star of "Carmen
Jones," and Richard Dyer-Bennett,
ballad singer, will entertain.
Cheltenham School Post
Applications Accepted
Because an insufficient number
of qualified persons applied for the
position of superintendent of the
Cheltenham School for Boys, the
State Employment Commissioner
again is taking application for the
post.
Notice of the vacancy has been
sent to other states in the hope
that qualified applicants may be
found, it was learned.
The State Department of Em-
ployment and Registration also cur-
rently is receiving applications for
the assistant superintendency of
the Maryland Training School for
Colored Girls.
Penn Alumni To Hear
University President
Dr. George McClelland, presi-
dent of the University of Pennsyl-
vania, will address the Penn alumni
dinner to be held at 7.30 P.M. Tues-
day at the Hotel Stafford.
Movies of last fall's football
games will be shown by George
Munger, Pennsylvania football
coach. Leonard Dill, secretary of
the general alumni association,
also will be present.
The committee in charge of the
arrangements
includes Harry
Wood, Joseph Archer, Harry Allers
and Paul Morris.
