TRIO CHARGED
WITH LARCENY
Men Held After Purse-Snatch-
ing By Boys
A purse-snatching by two 11-year-
old Negro boys resulted in the ar-
rest of three young Negro men on a
larceny charge, it was testified in
the Southern District Police Court
yesterday when the men were held
in $2,500 bail each for action of
the grand jury.
According to the testimony of
one of the 11-year-old boys, the
story begins early on the night of
January 12.
That night, it was testified, the
two boys snatched a purse contain-
ing $35 from Mrs. Olive E. Rensch,
of 6913 Kenleigh road, as she was
walking near Charles and Mont-
gomery sts.
Is Thrown In Churchyard
After taking the purse, the two
boys ran north on Charles st. and,
suddenly losing their nerve, threw
the pocketbook in a churchyard at
Charles and Hill sts.
Shortly thereafter, the two boys
met the three men, who asked them
what they were running away from.
The boys, it was testified, told
them of the purse-snatching and
where they had thrown the pocket-
book.
Later, upon hearing that the men
had gone to the churchyard and
found money in the purse, one of
the boys, becoming increasingly
afraid of what he had done, told
the police his story.
Three Held For Grand Jury
The three held for the grand jury
by Magistrate Solomon Liss were
identified by police as Raymond
Robertson, 18. of the 600 block
Dover st.; Vernon Matthew. 18, of
the 1000 block West Vine st., and
Willie Lee Smith, 23, of the 500
block Sharp st.
- One of the boys already has been
committed by the Juvenile Court to
the Cheltenham School for Colored
Boys for his part in the purse
snatching. The other has been re-
leased in the custody of his parents
for a hearing.
