Lothian Small To Address
Open Forum Here Today
Official of League Of Nations So-
ciety Also Will Speak At
Schools Tomorrow
Lothian Small, of Scotland, assist-
ant secretary-general of the Interna-
tional Federation of League of Na-
tions Societies, will speak on the
League on Nations and labor at 3
P. M. today at the Baltimore Open
Forum in the Auditorium Theater.
Mr. Small, who will arrive in Bal-
timore this morning, will be the guest
of Theodore Marburg for a few days.
He will be introduced this afternoon
by Dr. John H. Latane, professor of
American history and lecturer on in-
ternational law at the Johns Hopkins
University.
At 9.45 A. M. tomorrow Mr. Small
will address the Goucher College as-
sembly in Catherine Hooper Hall and
will speak at the State Normal School
in Towson two hours later. His sub-
ject on both occasions will be What
the Youth of Europe is Intending.
Negro Boy Makes Escape
From Police Station Here
Awaiting Transfer To House Of
Reformation, Youth Kicks
Hole In Wall
A 12-year-old Negro boy yesterday
escaped from the Northern District
Police Station, where he was held
awaiting transfer to the House of
Reformation for Colored Children at
Cheltenham, the police reported.
The boy was sentenced Friday on a
charge of incorrigibility and taken to
the detention room of the police sta-
tion, located on the second floor.
Yesterday, after kicking a hole
through the plaster and lathing of the
room, he crawled out into the hall and
mingled with a group of children lined
up to receive food and clothing. He is
believed to have escaped through a
rear door.
ARCHITECT IS
SPEAKER
Annual Dinner Held By Women's
Society
Approximately 100 persons last
might attended the annual dinner of
the Women's North Carolina Society
of Baltimore held in the Hotel Emer-
son. Benjamin A. Ruffin, Richmond,
(Va.) architect, delivered the princi-
pal address.
L. L. Watts, of Charlottesville, a
member of the Virginia Legislature,
introduced the speaker. Mrs. Helen
Bayley Davis read some of her own
poems. Mrs. John Edward Graves is
president of the society, and Mrs.
Thomas J. Copeland acted as toast-
master.
