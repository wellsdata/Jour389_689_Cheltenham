KIAH TERMED
INELIGIBLE AS
SCHOOL HEAD
Can't Be Reinstated At
Cheltenham, Board
President Says
Dr. W. E. Henry, president of
the board of managers of Chelten-
ham School for Boys, said last night
that Calvin L. Kiah definitely was
not eligible for reinstatement as
superintendent of the Negro train-
ing school,
He made the statement following
receipt of an opinion from the State
Law Department that, while ' Mr.
Kiah retained his place on the elig-
ible list for the classification of su-
perintendent, he could not be re-
appointed to the head post at Chel-
tenham,
There is, however, no other po-
sition in the State service for a
male superintendent in a Negro
training school.
List Set Up Year Ago
The eligible list established
nearly a year ago will expire June
18, and since the new all-Negro
Cheltenham board has not yet
moved to make an appointment
from the list, it is expected that
the board will request a new list
when the time limit on the old one
has run out.
Mr. Kiah was appointed to the
superintendency last September 1,
pending a six-month probationary
period. Just before the proba-
tionary period expired February
26, he was rejected by the old board
for permanent appointment.
Dr. Henry, who also is president
of State Teachers College at Bowie,
said a delegation supporting Mr.
Kiah had appeared before the
board of managers yesterday dur-
ing its regular monthly meeting at
the institution and that he had ex-
plained the ruling by Richard W.
Emory, deputy attorney general, to
the group.
Harris Is Assistant
He explained last night that H.
R. Harris, who recently requested
the employment commissioner for a
ruling on his status, is assistant su-
perintendent, a position he holds
under the Merit System, and that
the new board had not made Mr.
Harris acting superintendent..
During yesterday's board meet-
ing, Dr. Henry said, a temporary
building program recommended by
John B. Funk, chief engineer of
the State, was approved by the
board. It is expected Mr. Funk will
submit the plan to the Board of
Public Works on behalf of the
school, he said.
The construction program in-
volves erection of a new building
containing a dining room, kitchen
and classrooms and renovations to
several of the old buildings, par-
ticularly the administration build-
ing, it was explained.
