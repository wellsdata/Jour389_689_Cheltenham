5 Delegation Asks Governor Nice To

A delegation representing the Citi-
izens' Committee for Colored Boys yes-
terday asked Governor Nice to ap-
prove plans for reorganizing the Chel-
tenham School for Boys, a Negro re-
formatory in Southern Maryland.
The group, headed by Mrs. Asbury
• Smith, urged that a plan prepared by
Harry C. Jones, State Employment
Commissioner, be accepted and that
the Governor make the new appoint-
ments to Cheltenham's board from a
list submitted by the committee. It was
suggested that a woman be included.
The Governor told the group the
Jones plan had his "full approval," but
that he could make "no commitments
as to Cheltenham personnel." He said
he had a number of other names under
consideration, and that he did not in-
tend to make the appointments until
June.
