The Maryland State Planning
Commission and the Department of
Budget and Procurement yesterday
submitted to Governor O'Conor a
post-war construction and improve-
ment program with a proposed total
cost of $17,751,054.
The program revises the major
unfinished portion of the 1941 study
of the commission and, while it is
spread over three biennium peri-
ods-six years-it does not name
the years because of the uncer-
tainty as to the future availability
of materials and labor.

Highway Plan
The report includes a program
submitted by the State-wide High-
way Planning Survey, covering five
years of a twenty-year plan. Its
chief projects are the Baltimore to
Washington Parkway and the Wash-
ington to Annapolis Parkway.
It is noted that the inclusion of
the highway survey does not imply
the commission's approval of it.
