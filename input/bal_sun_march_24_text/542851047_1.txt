Oldest Documents
Believed to be the oldest docu-
ments extant pertaining to what is
now known as the 5th Regiment,
Maryland National Guard, two dis-
charge certificates issued to mem-
bers of the Baltimore Independent
Blues were brought to the public
eye by descendants of the militia-
men here.
One of them is issued to John
Keener, second sergeant, and is the
property of G. H. Pouder, executive
vice president of the Association of
Commerce. The other is owned by
Vernon S. Vavrina, acting history
supervisor for junior high schools.
It was issued to Daniel Stever, his
great-great-grandfather. Identical
in form, they cite participation in
the Battle of Bladensburg, on Au-
gust 24, 1814, and in the Battle of
North Point, on the September 12
following.

SUNDAY
Baltimore, Md., Sunday, September 15, 1946
The Sun Cab Company last week
filed application before the Public
Service Commission for the elimi-
nation of group taxicab riding, the
elimination of the 312-mile travel
limit, approval of increased rates
of travel outside the city limits and
a flat rate for service to and from
the Pimlico race track during the
racing season.
'The application, filed through the
company's attorney, James J. Lind-
say, pointed out that the commis-
sion's orders providing for group
riding and a travel limit of 342
miles beyond the corporate limits
of Baltimore city were wartime
measures, the reasons for which no
longer exist.
The group-riding and cab service
orders were issued by the Public
Service Commission in 1942 in or-
der to conserve "critical materials"
and to facilitate the movement of
traffic necessary to the successful
prosecution of the war.
Mr. Lindsay pointed out in the
application that tires' and gasoline
are no longer critical materials and
that new equipment, although not
yet readily available to meet the
demands of the industry, is gradu
ally becoming available.
The Sun Cab Company, he said,
now has in operation 57 new cabs
of its total operating fleet of 221.
Mr. Lindsay said that the cab in-
dustry in Baltimore is no longer
burdened with an excess of traffic,
due to a lessening of the passenger
load and the granting of permits for
additional cabs. These factors, he
said, warrant the changes asked.
The proposed rates for travel
beyond the city limits, which Mr.
Lindsay called "both sound and
equitable to all concerned," would
be the total of the meter charge to
the city limits and double the
charge from the city limits to the
destination after the meter is reset.
