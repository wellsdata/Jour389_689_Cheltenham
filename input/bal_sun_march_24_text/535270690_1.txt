APPOINTMENTS TOMORROW
Few Changes In Commissions Ex-
pected At City Hall.
Mayor Preston will complete his list of
spring appointments tomorrow, sending to
the City Council the names of a number of
men and women who are to serve as di-
rectors on the part of the city in the man-
agement of several reformatory institu-
tions and also as members of unpaid com-
missions.
It was stated at the City Hall yesterday
that there would be but few changes. The
members of the Commission on City Plan
will be reappointed, as will also Gen.
Thomas J. Shryock and City Councilman
Harry S. Cummings, colored, as directors
in the management of the Colored House
of Reformation at Cheltenham.
Managers of the Maryland School for
Boys are wondering whether or not the
Mayor will reappoint Gen. Lawrason Riggs
as a director in that institution. He is
president of the board. The statement was
made yesterday that General Riggs would
