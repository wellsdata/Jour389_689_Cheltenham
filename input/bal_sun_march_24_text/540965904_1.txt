Harry C. Jones, State Employmen
Commissioner, last night announce
an eligible list of five men for thi

According to Mr. Jones, thirteer
applications for the post were re

A certification of the first five names
will be sent to the president of the
board of managers of the institutior
for submission to the board.
To Return To Old Post
B. B. Sunderland, present superin-
tendent at Cheltenham, will return to
the post of parole officer when the
new appointment is made. He was
parole officer in 1937, when the insti-
tution was taken over by the State.
In the 1942-43 budget submitted by
Governor O'Conor to the Legislature,
the superintendent's salary was placed
at $3,500.

Royal Alexander Crump, Negro, of
Hanover, Va., was the sixth man in
the examination ratings. He is 53 years
old, and has been for the past twelve
years assistant superintendent of the
Virginia Manual Labor School for
Colored Boys at Hanover. IHe was edu-
cated at Hampton.
Now CCC Adviser
Mr. Zulick,, top man in the eligible
list, is 36 years old, and is now District
Educational Advisor for the Civilian
Conservation Corps for Pennsylvania,
Maryland, Virginia and the District
of Columbia. He has held the post for
five years. He is a graduate of the
University of Maryland, the National
University Law School and has had
special university courses in education.
He taught at the Hyattsville High
School for five years.
Mr. Zulick has under his supervision
forty camps, ten of which are for
colored enrollees, and is responsible
for their academic and vocational
training.
Mr. Thomas is 49 years old and is
now chief parole officer of the United
States Penitentiary at Atlanta. He has
been a professional parole and social
worker for many years. He was em-
ployed in vocational work at the
Lincoln School of Commerce, Lincoln;
Neb. He was educated at Cotner Col-
lege with post-graduate courses 1
sociology and economics.
Assistant To Warden
Mr. Swasey is 48 years old and is at
present warden's assistant at the Fed-
eral Penitentiary at Lewisburg, Pa.,
where he has been employed for the
past six years. Before that he served
for several years in a supervisory са-
pacity in two juvenile correctional
institutions. He is a graduate of Bates
College and has been principal and
superintendent of schools.
Mr. Brennan is 40 years old. At
present, he is a special teacher and
supervisor in industrial arts for the
Washington county Board of Educa-
tion. For eighteen years he was em-
ployed as head of the industrial arts
department at Hampton Institute, a
training school for colored boys. He
was educated in California and in
Brooklyn, N. Y.
Mr. Jones is 59 years old, and is
director of education at the Chelten-
ham school, to which he was appointed
a year ago. For nine years, he was
superintendent of the National Train-
ing School for Boys in Washington.
and for five years he was superin-
tendent of the Colorado State Indus-
trial School for Boys. His previous
experience was in the teaching field
and in military service. He is a gradu-
