CHELTENHAM
Ii disorders at Cheltenham require the
summoning of police officers to quell the dis-
turbance, it is strongly indicated that the
management of Maryland's reformatory for
colored boys is not yet up to the expectations
of the people of the State. When the institu-
tion was taken over by the State, it was
assumed that the glaring defects in adminis-
tration at Cheltenham would be promptly
remedied so that there would be an end to
the procession of rumors and dismal stories
from that stepchild among Maryland institu-
tions. Up to now it does not appear that the
hopes of the State have been realized. True,
there have been obstacles to swift reform, but
months have passed since Maryland assumed
control of Cheltenham, and the place is still
administered in a manner which causes those
in charge to send for outside aid. What can
the matter be? And when can we expect an
end to these unpleasant episodes?
