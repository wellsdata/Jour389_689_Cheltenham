BOARD LENDS
CRAWFORD TO
CHELTENHAM
Education Staff Member
To Serve School On
Part-Time Basis
At the request of the board of
managers of the Cheltenham School
for Boys, the State Board of Edu-
cation agreed yesterday to lend the
services of George Crawford, assist-
ant supervisor of curriculum, to the
Negro training school,
Mr. Crawford, staff member of
The State Department of Educa-
tion, will serve at Cheltenham on
La part-time basis "for an indefinite
time, maybe until a superintendent
is selected," according to the ap
proved request by the institution's
board.
The request, made through
Edward N. Wilson, secretary of the
Cheltenham board, said the board
wished Mr. Crawford "to work
with the cottage masters and the
director of education in formulat-
ing a program of activities."
Dr. Thomas G. Pullen, Jr., who
was re-elected State superintendent
of schools for another four-year
term at yesterday's meeting, ex-
plained, when the assignment was
questioned, that Mr. Crawford's
duties would be to help set up the
institution's educational program.
Similar Aid Given In Past
He said the State Department of
Education gave similar assistance
to other training schools.
Dr. W. E. Henry, president of
the State Teachers College at
Bowie, recently was elected head
of the new all-Negro board of man-
agers of Cheltenham,
As yet that board has taken no
action on appointment of a new
superintendent of the training
school. A vacancy has existed in the
position since February 27 when
Calvin L. Kiah was rejected for
appointment as permanent superin-
tendent after serving a six-month
probationary period.
In the interim, H. R. Harris, as-
sistant superintendent, is serving as
acting superintendent.
Board Officers Named
The State Board of Education
opened its session with re-election
of Tasker G. Lowndes as president
and election of Nicholas Orem as
vice president. Mr. Orem was nomi-
nated by Wendell D. Allen, vice
president since 1942.
The board voted to acknowledge
an appeal taken by the City Barber
School from the State superintend-
ent's action in ordering the school
closed April I because it had failed
to meet requirements for approval
under the new licensing law.
It was planned that a hearing in
the ease would be held at the
board's next meeting, probably in
June. The school is permitted to
continue operations pending the
hearing, the first requested under
the licensing law.
