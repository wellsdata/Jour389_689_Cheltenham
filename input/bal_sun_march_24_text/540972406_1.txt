MOSER CITES
NEED IN NEGRO
JUVENILE CASE
Favors Maximum Secur-
ity Unit For Colored As
Well As White Boys
Some provision for a maximum
security unit should be made for.
difficult Negro juvenile offenders,
Judge Herman M. Moser said yes-
terday after suspending a four-year
sentence to the House of Correc
tion that he had previously im-
posed on a 13-year-old Negro boy.
"There is just as much need, pos-
sibly a greater one, for the estab-
lishment of such a unit for these
'tough' Negfo offenders as for white
boys," declared Judge Moser.
"I am in favor of the State insti-
tutions which now take care of
juvenile cases being made the.
places for the establishment of
these security units."
Recommitted To Cheltenham
The boy whose sentence was sus-
pended was convicted last Feb-
ruary for participation in a series
of burglaries. He was sentenced to
the Cheltenham School for Boys
and ran away from the institution
last May 27.
On June 14 he was given a four-
year sentence to the House of Cor-
rection by Judge Moser on charges
of burglary committed after he
escaped from the reformatory.
This latter sentence was sus-
pended by the jurist, who recom-
mitted the defendant to Chelten-
ham.
