A bill to give the State control of
Cheltenham Reformatory for Negro
Boys has been drawn up and approved
by the reformatory's board of trustees,
Morris A. Rome, member of the
Board of State Aid and Charities,
announced yesterday.
It will be introduced to the General
Assembly by a number of Republican
Senators as an administration measure,
Mr. Rome said. Among those sponsor-
ing it will be Senator Melvin L. Fine
of Baltimore and Senator J. David
Baile of Carroll county.
Board Is Named
The bill provides that the institution
shall be known henceforth as the
Cheltenham School for Boys. All of
its property in Prince George's county
is to be deeded to the State, with the
exception of $30,000 in cash, which is
to be kept by the present trustees and
invested as a trust fund for the school
and employes.
The following are named in the bill
as a board of managers:
The Governor of Maryland, to serve
ex officio.
WILLARD W. ALLEN, JOSEPH C. BRISCOE,
FREDERICK DOUGLASS, GEORGE MC-
DONALD and JOHN O. SPENCER, to
serve for a period ending June 1,
1938.
ENOCH HARLAN, HOWARD C. HILL, JOHN
E. MCCORMACK, BLOSSIE R. MILLER
and JAMES E. STEUART, to serve for
a period ending June 1, 1939.
JOSEPH H. BLANDFORD, HAL B. CLAGETT,
J. AMOS DAVIS, WILLIAM H. GIBBONS
and M. HAMPTON MAGRUDER, to serve
for a period ending June 1, 1940.
At the expiration of their present
terms the managers may be reap-
pointed, or their successors appointed.
for a term of three years, the bill
provides. Appointive power is placed
in the hands of the Governor.
