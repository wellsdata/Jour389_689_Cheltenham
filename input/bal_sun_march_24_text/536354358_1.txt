MAYOR IS DETERMINED
Will Appoint Colored Director
Regardless Of Objections.
WANTS RACE REPRESENTED
To Have Whole Board Of Negroes
Wouldn't Be Advisable-Harry S.
Cummings Recommended.
"I don't care who objects; a colored man
Is going to be appointed as a member of
the board of directors of the Colored House
of Reformation!"
Such was the reply of Mayor Hayes yes-
terday to a Sus reporter's query as to
whether he thought any of the members
of the board would remonstrate against
serving on It with a colored man.
"Do you think a colored man could look
after the interests of the inmates better
than a white man?" the Mayor was asked.
"I think that there should be a colored
man on that board," said the Mayor. "The
colored boys will talk more freely to one of
their own race than to a white man, and
will acquaint him with their needs. The
principle is the same as in the schools.
here should be colored teachers for col-
cred pupils. It would be impracticable to
have the Cheltenham board composed
wholly of colored men, because they would
probably lack the business and executive
ability which the white men possess, but I
certainly think that the race should be rep-
resented."
A petition in favor of the appointment of
James W. Highes, 519 St. Paui street, was
drawn up and signed by the members of
the grand jury yesterday. He is the col-
ored member of the grand jury. Messrs.
George B. Skinner and William L. Wilcox,
of that body, cailed on Mayor Hayes yes-
terday morning to present the petition.
The Mayor declined to give any hint as to
whom he would appoint, saying that he
would send the nomination up to the City
Council on Monday evening.
City Couneliman Hiram Watty, whom
the Mayor asked to recommend some one
for the position, stated last night that he
Tad recommended Harry S. Cummings, a
well-known colored lawyer, who was fer-
merly a member of the City Council.
