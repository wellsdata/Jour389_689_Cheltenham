2 ABSOLVED
BY ATKINSON
Policeman Cleared Of Charge
Of Beating Youths
After studying full reports on
the case, Hamilton R. Atkinson,
police commissioner, said yester
day there was no foundation for
charges by two 14-year-old Negro
boys that police beat them with
nightsticks as they were being ar
rested for attempted burglary last
week.
In absolving two Northeastern
district patrolmen, Commissioner
Atkinson said the boys' charges
"may have been made to excite
sympathy with the view to obtain-
ing leniency, particularly because
both boys had previous records of
burglary and larceny extending
since 1944."
"There Was No Brutality"
Mr. Atkinson asserted he was
convinced the policemen were not
guilty of mistreating the boys.
"I will not stand for any bru-
tality-but in this case there was
no brutality," he said.
The boys made their complaint
in Juvenile Court Friday morning.
Arrested Thursday night, they com.
plained also that they were not
given breakfast before being taken
to court the next morning.
Judge Charles E. Moylan, who
presides in the Division for Juve
nile Causes, reported the allega
tions to Commissioner Atkinson)
after interrogating the boys.
The judge had convicted the
youthful defendants and sentenced
them to the Cheltenham School
for Boys. As they were leaving the
courtroom he noticed marks on the
forehead of one and on the side of
the head of the other boy.
In a statement on the case, Com
missioner Atkinson said the boys
had broken into a store directly
across the street from the North-
eastern Police Station.
Their presence was discovered
before anything was taken, he said,
but police at first could not locate
the burglars.
Then, using a flashlight, they
observed the boys "standing in a
crouched position under the steps
in the rear of the building, the com-
missioner said.
"Were Not Struck"
Mr. Atkinson's statement con-
tinued:
"They were forcibly pulled from
this position, and it is possible that
both boys sustained slight injuries
by coming in contact with the in-
side or side portions of the steps,
but they were not struck by the ar
resting officers, nor did either boy
complain of having been assaulted
until interrogated in court."
The brutality charges were in
vestigated for the Police Commis-
sioner by Chief Inspector M.
Joseph Wallace.
Inspector Wallace declared he
examined the injuries of both boys
and in neither case was there any
sign of a "Jump or welt such as un
doubtedly would have been caused
if the boys had been hit with a
nightstick."
Slept Until 9.30 Α.Μ.
Commissioner Atkinson said the
boys' complaint that they were
taken to Juvenile Court last Friday
morning without breakfast also had
been investigated.
He said five boys were incar-
cerated in the juvenile detention
rooms at the Northeastern Station
that morning, and that the North-
eastern matron ordered five break.
fasts which were delivered.
However, he said, the boys were
still sleeping when the breakfasts
arrived and the matron did not
insist that they get up.
Asserting he would not condone
mistreatment of minors or any
cruelty on the part of members of
the department, Commissioner
Atkinson added that the problem
of coping with "experienced
juvenile delinquents" sometimes is
just as hard as dealing with older
criminals.
