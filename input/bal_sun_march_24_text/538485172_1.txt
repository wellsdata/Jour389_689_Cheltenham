Stresses Heredity.
As Life-Span Factor
Dr. Barker Says Best Assurance
Of Longevity Is To Have
Had Old Ancestors
New York. Jan. 10 (P)-Whether a
man will live to a ripe old age is
determined before his birth and does
not hinge on the kind of life he leads,
says Dr. L. F. Barker, professor
emeritus of medicine at the Johns
Hopkins Medical School.
"Longevity is hereditary," he said
in a paper read yesterday at the New
York School of Social Work. "The
best insurance of long personal exist-
is derivation from long-lived
ence
ancestors."
Dr. Barker, 74, was too ill to read
the paper himself and a physician in
his early thirties was scheduled to
deliver it. But the substitute became
ill, too, and it was finally read by
Dr. George Lawton, 40, who kept his
fingers crossed while reading.
