WILLIAM H. JANNEY
DIES AT AGE OF 84
Retired As Secretary-Treas-
urer Of Cheltenham Insti-
tution 18 Months Ago
SERVED FOR 14 YEARS
Funeral Services To Be Held To-
morrow With Interment In
Friends' Burial Ground
William Henry Janney, secretary-
treasurer of the House of Reforma-
tion for Colored Boys at Cheltenham
until his retirement eighteen months
ago, died suddenly at his home in the
Plaza Apartments, Park avenue and
Wilson street, at noon yesterday. He
was 84 years of age.
Funeral services will be held in the
parlors at 802-4 Madison avenue at
11 A. M. tomorrow. Burial will be in
the Friends' Burial Ground.
Engaged In Banking
Mr. Janney was born in Washington,
the son of Henry and Hannah Schol-
field Janney. During the early part
of his life he was engaged in the
banking business and served as presi-
dent of the National Bank of Smyrna,
Delaware.
He came to Baltimore about twenty-
five years ago and had been connect-
ed with the Cheltenham institution
for approximately fourteen years.
His wife, who was Miss Elizabeth
Brooks, of Baltimore, died in 1919. He
was a brother of the late Dr. O. Ed-
ward Janney. His survivors are two
daughters, Miss Phida Janney, of Bal-
timore, and Mrs. F. D. Davis, of Buf-
falo, N. Y., and a son, Henry Janney,
of Baltimore.
He was a member of the Society of
Friends.
