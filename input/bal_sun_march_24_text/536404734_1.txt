Boy Confesses Stealing Letter.
James Scott, colored, 15 years old,
pleaded guilty in the United States Dis-
trict Court yesterday of the larceny of a
letter containing $6 from postal sub-sta-
tion No. 11, corner Gilmor street and Fred-
erick avenue, on February 10 last. He was
sentenced to two years in the Colored
House of Reformation at Cheltenham. The
boy was employed about the station at the
time of the larceny.
Charge Against Mr. Hanna Dismissed
The United States grand jury yesterday
dismissed the case of Robert B. Hanna,
charged with assaulting George C. Flory
on the Naval Academy grounds at Anuapo-
lis on January 4 last. Flory, it was al-
leged, was knocked down and had a leg
broken.
Rachel Brooks' Estate.
The estate of the late Rachel Brooks is
appraised at $30,304 in inventories filed in
the Orphans' Court yesterday. The total
is made up of $32,257 in real and $7,047 in
personal property.
Notes From The Courts.
Clara C. De Bols, 1112 East Monument
street, was granted an absolute divorce
yesterday from George J. De Bois and the
custody of their child, Elsle May, aged 7'
years. They were married November 16,
1894, and lived together about a month,
The divorce was granted on the ground of
abandonment. Mrs. De Bols testified that
her husband went to New York to look for
work, but he never came back.
Frank C. Rhodes, a storekeeper at Wil-
liamsport, Md., applied for the benefit of
the bankruptcy law yesterday by Lewis
D. Syester, attorney. His liabilities are
$811.07 and assets $175.
John Timmons, of Dorchester county,
Ad., was granted his discharge in bank-
ruptcy yesterday.
