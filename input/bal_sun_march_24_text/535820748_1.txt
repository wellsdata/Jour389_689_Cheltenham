Fire at Cheltenham School.
[Special Dispatch to the Baltimore Sun.
MARLBORO, MD., May 30 -At 6.30 o'clock
Saturday evening Family Building No. 4, of
the House of Reformation for Colored Youth
at Cheltenham, Prince George's county, was
consumed by fire, the contents being saved.
This was one of the six family buildings of
the institution, each about 50 bs 40 feet,
three stories high, of brick, with capacity for
fifty boys, costing about $13,000. The build-
iny was fully insured, and no boy was in-
jured. The house was used by fifty-seven
boys as a school room and dormitory, in
charge of Teacher Early. A new building
will be erected as soon as practicable.
Superintendent John W. Horn telegraphed
the facts about the fire to Mr. Frank Woods,
president of the board, in Baltimore. The
late Enoch Pratt was president of the insti-
tution for many years and on his death was
succeeded by Mr. Woods. Mr. Edward Sta-
bler, Jr., is the treasurer and Joseph J. Jan-
ney secretary and general agent.
Residence Burned.
[Special Dispatch to the Baltimore Sun.]
KENSINGTON, MD., May 30.-The fine sum-
mer residence of J. Maury Dove, on the
Georgetown pike, near Bethesda, Montgom-
ery county, was burned Saturday night with
its contents. The origin of the fire is not
known. The loss is about five thousand dol-
lure, partly insured. A valuable St. Bernard
dog perished in the house.
