Conference Planned
On Cheltenham School
Committee From Board Of Man-
agers To Meet Employment
Commissioner Monday
A committee from the board of
managers of the Cheltenham School
for Boys will confer with Harry C.
Jones, State Employment Commis-
sioner, Monday afternoon, the com-
missioner said yesterday.
It will seek to clarify "certain legal
technicalities" involved in Mr. Jones'
order that the institution agree to dis-
miss nearly half its staff as unfit for
entrance into the classified service of
the State.
The committee, appointed by Joseph
H. Blandford, president of the fifteen-
man board, will report back to the
board before any decision is reached
on whether the institution will con-
cur with the Employment Commis-
sioner's order.
State Income Tax Unit
Working On Two Shifts
Collections Thus Far Total $331,-
285, With Thousands Of
Returns Untabulated
Annapolis, March 18 (P)-Employes
of the income tax division of the State
Comptroller's office went on two shifts
today to clean up the tax payments
received before the dead line expired
on Tuesday.midnight.
One group worked from 9 A. M. to
4 P. M., and the other from 2 to 9
P. M. The fifty employes will follow
this schedule until the mail is opened
and returns tabulated.
The first tabulation in a week to-
day added $135,977.33 from 100 cor-
porations and 2,700 individuals. This
brought the total collections from the
1½ of 1 per cent. tax to $331,285.53 from
19,842 corporations, fiduciaries, non-
residents and individuals. Thousands
of tax returns remain to be tabulated.
CHICKEN THEFTS ALLEGED
Two Men Sentenced To Cut For
Eighteen Months On Charges
Thomas E. Nash, 52, of Woodensburg,
and Frank Becraft, 36, of Reisters-
town, were sentenced to eighteen
months in the House of Correction
last night by Magistrate J. E. Myers,
of Reisterstown, for the larceny of
thirteen chickens.
Nash was found guilty of stealing
six of the fowls from J. E. Pinkler,
of Woodensburg, and Becraft was con-
victed of the theft of seven from Mrs.
Alice Turnbaugh, near Reisterstown.
The men were arrested Wednesday
night.
Appeals To Tax Board
Washington, March 18 (P) - Henry
A. B. Dunning, of Baltimore, petitioned
the Board of Tax Appeals for re-
determination of an alleged 1934 in-
come-tax deficiency of $37,530. He said
the Internal Revenue Collector erred
in treating income of his wife and of
five trust estates he created as his
own taxable income.
