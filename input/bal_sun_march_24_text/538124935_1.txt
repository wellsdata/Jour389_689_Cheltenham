Sports At A Glance
Nothing in the sports world last
week could quite compete with that
dizzy World Series that went its
full seven-game limit. Skyrocket
ing to their first world champion-
ship in ten years, the Detroit Tigers
walked off with the deciding game,
9-3. The Tigers smashed five runs
across in the first inning for the
merriest scoring spree of the series
and then rode home behind the
brilliant twirling of their ace left
hander. Hal Newhouser. Hal fanned
ten batters and broke an all-time
world series mark for seven games
by running his total of strikeouts
in the series to 22 against the pre-
vious 20 held by three other fling-
ers.

In the Little World Series. things
are looking mighty bad for the
Newark team which is toppling
over fast before the superior play-
ing of Louisville. To date, three
games have been played and to
date, the Bears have lost all three,
4-2, 5-3 and 8-3.

Sunday School On Wheels
A very practical man, the Rev.
Edwards E. Elliott, is not the sort
of person to be hindered by a hous-
ing shortage when he is about the
Lord's work. When the Sunday
school at St. Andrews Presbyter-
ian Church, 3553 Elmley avenue,
began to tax the available space; he
moved the classes to a former bar-
ber shop at Potomac and Preston
streets. Now, with the crowds
spilling out of that, he has bor-
rowed a bus from the Baltimore
Transit Company's car barns near-
by, and moved it up alongside the
regular Sunday-school quarters.

Mrs. Lillian Keesecker, who al-
ternates with another teacher in
giving the children their Sunday
school lesson in the bus every Sun-
day, says that attention is good
and there are no outside attrac-
tions.
