MANY CHANGES
ADVOCATED AT
CHELTENHAM
New Official Would Raze
Several Buildings And
Expand Education
Dwight II. Ferguson, head of the
State Department of Public Wel-
fare's new division on institutions,
yesterday asserted that all except
four buildings at the Cheltenham
School for Boys should be razed
and replaced with well-constructed,
modern units.
Following a tour of the school
Saturday, Mr. Ferguson described
the administration building and six
cottages now in use as obsolete.
Earlier, J. Milton Patterson, head
of the State Department of Public
Welfare, declared the school was
"a medieval penal institution."
Would Keep Only Recent Ones
Mr. Ferguson said the only build-
ings that should be left standing
are the recently constructed gym-
nasium, hospital, boiler room and
mule barn.
The six cottages which house the
Negro juvenile delinquents, school-
rooms and workshops have been in
use since the school was built in
1872, he said, "and do not seem
worth repairing."
He also recommended that the
administration building. which
houses offices, officers' quarters.
dining room and kitchen, should be
replaced with a new structure.
Points Out Shortcomings
Vance E. Thomas, superintend-
ent of the school, pointed out to
Mr. Ferguson and Lee L. Dopkin,
a member of the board of directors
of the Welfare Department, inade-
quate toilet facilities, places where
buildings were disintegrating and
unsanitary kitchen facilities.
Inmates of the institution, it was
explained never have eaten with
any utensil but a spoon, from
cereal-type bowls. The kitchen floor
constantly is wet from steam
emitted from huge cooking pots
and food is stirred with long wood-
en sticks.
The new welfare official also as-
serted that a combination school
and workshop building should be
erected and that an athletic field
should be provided for the boys in
the institution.
Needs Are Cited
Needs cited by Mr. Thomas
were:
Installation of modern elec
trical equipment to train the
boys for vocational work such as
tailoring, shoemaking and me-
chanical lines.
Provision of adequate shower
facilities and replacement of
bathroom facilities in use for
more than thirty years.
Hiring of approximately fif-
teen staff members and teachers
in addition to the present staff of
fifty, which includes several
conscientious objectors.
