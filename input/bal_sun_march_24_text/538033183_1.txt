Way Cleared For Test
Of Cheltenham Dispute
| Court To Be Asked To Rule On
Merit Service Status Of Per-
sonnel At School
Harry C. Jones, State Employment
Commissioner, yesterday opened the
way for a court test of his right to re-
fuse to accept twenty-six employes of
the Cheltenham School, for Boys in the
State classified service.
By refusing to approve the April
pay roll of the institution, he gave the
board of managers the opportunity
of bringing mandamus proceedings
against him. It has been announced
that a friendly suit would be insti-
tuted in this manner.
Mr. Jones contends that, although
the Legislature provided that person-
nel of the institution be accepted into
the State service without examination,
many cannot qualify for the merit
system classifications provided for by
law.
To Aid Spanish Loyalists
Spanish Loyalist sympathizers in
Baltimore will hold a massmeeting
Sunday in the Lyric as part of the
emergency campaign to aid the Span-
ish Government in the current war.
The meeting will be under auspices
of the medical bureau and North
American Committee to Aid Spanish
Democracy and the American League
for Peace and Democracy. Dr. Henry
E. Sigerist will presside.
