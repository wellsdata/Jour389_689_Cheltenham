New Sites Studied
For Cheltenham
Two "very excellent" sites near
Baltimore are under consideration
for the projected new Chelten-
ham School for Boys, Thomas B.
Sprague, president of the Chelten-
ham board of managers, said yes-
terday.
He announced that a special com-
tee of the board was making a care-
ful investgation of the tracts in-
volved, and said the board had
"every reason to hope for early suc-
cess" in selecting a site.
A fund of $1,500,000 has been
set aside for construction of the
proposed new training school for
Negro youths. The present school
is situated in Prince Georges
county.
Earlier attempts by the board to
purchase property, first at Catons-
ville and later near Mount Winans,
were unsuccessful.
