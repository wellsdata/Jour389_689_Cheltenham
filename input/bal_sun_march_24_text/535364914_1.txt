[Special Dispatch to the Baltimore Sun.]
Laurel, Md., June 28.-The first spring.
meeting given by the Laurel Four-County
Fair Association began at the Laurel course'
today. The entry list promised good rác,
ing and the first day's sport proved that it
was not a false promise.
The first event was for the 2.15 class of
pacers. Six started. Every heat was a
horse race from start to finish. The first
two beats were closely contested.
Andy N. J., a horse from Reading, Pa..
proved to be the gamer and won out. He
was forced to pace the ürst mile in 2.144
by Clinton M., another Pennsylvania' en-
try. The heat pumped the contender and
he was distanced in the second heat, one,
in which Nicholas, also from Pennsylvania,
took his place as a conteuder. Nicholas
did even better. He won the third heat in
a drive from Andy N. J., but the latter
was gamer and won the deciding heat, with
Violet, another Pennsylvanian, as the con-
tender.
Trainer Dougherty, of Virginia, showed
in Pearl Wilkes, a daughter of Dr. Knox, a
mare which not only won her race in the
2.30 class trot, but an animai destined to
win in faster company. Sidney Dayton
showed speed enough to keep the winner
moving, but Mitchell, his driver, publicly
stated that he could not beat the Virginia
mare.
Much interest centered in the Four-
County free-for-all race. Bert H., from
Dickerson, Montgomery county, Md., won
off the reel. The books opened him at 4 to
1 long before post time. IHe was listed at
3 to 10. In the final heat the speculators
went to Linkwood May for place or show.
Palm Cozine was the only one able to keep
near Bert II. The route was too long.for
Linkwood May and she was beaten in the
last furlong by Gallant Mald for the show
money.
