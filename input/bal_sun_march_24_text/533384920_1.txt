Governor O'Conor yesterday an

Two Reappointed
Those reappointed were Joseph H
Blandford, who is president of the
board, and Hal B. Clagett. The Chel
tenham board elects its own presiden
so Governor O'Conor did not desig.
nate a man for that office.
Dr. H. C. Byrd, president of the
University of Maryland, was among
the old members who will. not serve
longer. He told the Governor he dic
not have time to attend to his Chelten.
ham duties and therefore did not wish
to remain on the board, although his
term did not expire until next year.
Another to leave was M. Hampton
Magruder. He also told the Governor
he did not wish to hold the post longer.
The Governor said he was sure even
further, improvement would be made
at the institution by his new board.
He said the old board already had
done much.
Lists Improvements
The Governor listed the following
improvements:
"A well qualified director of educa-
tion has been appointed. His selection
was made upon merit, and already a
promising program is under way..
"A director of recreation has been
appointed and is now functioning at
the school.
"A psychologist has been engaged
whose work will result in the proper
assignment of trainees in the various
units of the training program."
As to physical improvements in the
plant, the Governor pointed to the
reconditioning of the dining rooms,
improvements in the power plant, an
excellent gymnasium in process of
construction, new beds and new fur-
niture.
A physician is on duty, and physical
training classes are being conducted.
