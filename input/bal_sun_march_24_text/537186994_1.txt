CITY COLLEGE WINS
Baltimoreans Capture Tome's
Field And Track Meet.
DAVIS INDIVIDUAL CHAMPION
He Gets The Cup For Scoring The
Highest Number Of Points-Cen-
tral High, Washington, Second.
[Special Dispatch to the Baltimore Sun.]
Port Deposit, Md., May 12.-The fourth
annual Interscholastic field and track meet
of the Jacob Tome Institute was held here
this afternoon. Excepting for a slight
wind, the day was ideal for the events. A
large crowd of Baltimoreans came on the
steamer Susquehanna to witness the games,
which were run off without a flaw. The
Baltimore City College won the meet, and
C. P. Davis, of that college, won the indi-
vidual champlonship cup offered to the ath-
lete scoring the highest number of points.
The City College aggregate of points was
22, which does not take into consideration
the junior events. The Central High
School of Washington was second, with a
total of 13 points.
Four Tome and one State records were
broken. In the hammer throw R. McCar-
roll, of Brown Preparatory School; smashed
the previous Tome record of 125 feet 10
Inches and established a new one of 134
feet 10 inches. H. S. Gamble, of Tome In-
stitute, in the pole vault raised his own
mark of 10 feet to 10 feet 6 inches. E. MII-
ler, of Conway Hall, put the shot 43 feet
5 inches, breaking the previous record of
41 feet 0% inches, held by W. M. Hess, of
the Central High School of Philadelphia.
Garduer Ziegler, of the City College, low-
ered the State interscholastic record of
55 1-5 seconds for the quarter-mile run to
54 3-5 seconds. This race was one of the
hair-raising kind. The contestants started
off at a break-neck clip. The entire Balti-
morean contingent had their hopes cen-
tered in Ziegler. By the time the runners
turned into the homestretch he was a lit-
tle too far back of the leaders for comfort,
and the collegians began to despair, but
the City College man then showed what
was in him. He covered the last 100 yards
with great speed and crossed the line fully
three yards in front of the second man.
In the broad jump C. P. Davis, who
holds the Tome record of 21 feet 10 Inches
for this event, failed to equal it, but, never-
theless, won first place with a leap of 21
feet 1 inch. Davis won the 100-yard dash
in fine style, going the distance in 10 2-5
seconds. He also captured second place in
the high jump.
From the very start it looked as if City
College was going to have everything its
own way. The entire three heats of the
senior 100-yard dash were won by Duker,
Davis and Ziegler, respectively. The junior
100-yard dash heats, which followed, were
also captured by City College runners, but
their triumphant march was stopped by
the mile run, not one City College man win-
ning a place.
