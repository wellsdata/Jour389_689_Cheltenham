The State's plans call for a wholly new
training school for Negro boys, the site to be
in the vicinity of Catonsville. This institution
will cost approximately $1,500,000; the
money for it has already been set aside in
the special postwar building and rehabilita-
tion fund. When this school is opened, the
present inadequate Cheltenham School for
Boys will be turned over to the State Board
of Mental Hygiene for use in the care of
Negro boys who suffer from mental diseases
or are seriously retarded mentally.
Those are the plans. When they are carried
out, there should be an end to the problem
of what to do with a Negro juvenile delin-
quent though he be insane, moronic, tough
or what not. Certainly our State officials can-
not be planning to spend $1,500,000 on a new
institution plus $300,000 or so at Cheltenham
only to inform the public later that more
money is needed to provide a facility for the
care of a few unruly youngsters.
The only question remaining is, therefore,
how soon will the new school be built? The
project has been given top priority by State
officials. There has been some delay in acquir.
ing a site, but progress here is expected to be
somewhat more rapid in the next few weeks.
Then detailed planning, and actual construc-
tion, should follow promptly.
To be sure, the new school will not he
