NEW CHELTENHAM
PROTEST TONIGHT
Community Groups Lay Plans
For Mass Meeting
- Representatives of half a dozen
community and civic associations,
meeting last night at Baltimore
Highlands, completed plans for a
mass meeting at 8.30 o'clock to-
night in Lakeland Presbyterian
Church to protest location of the
new Cheltenham School for colored
boys on a 122-acre site between the
Hollins Ferry road and the Old
Annapolis road.
B. D. Hall, president of the First
Precinct Community Improvement
Association, presided at the meet-
ing at which the State administra-
tion was hotly condemned for its
part in selection of the new site.
All-Out Fight Pledged
Representatives of six improve-
ment associations with combined
membership of 1,800 pledged an
all-out fight to keep Cheltenham
out of the section.
Among those directing the fight
against Cheltenham who attended
last night's meeting were: Emmett
Batson, president, Lakeland-Dor-
chester Heights Improvement Asso-
ciation; Mrs. George Grewe, treas-
urer, Rosemont Improvement Asso-
ciation; George Dorner, of the
English Consul Progressive Asso-
ciation; C. R. Zepp, president, Thir-
teenth District Democratic Club,
of Lansdowne; Joseph Smith, Jr.,
president, Lansdowne Civic League,
and Mr. Hall, for the Baltimore
Highlands community.
Protest To Governor
Spokesmen declared telegrams
of protest had been sent Governor
O'Conor.
Mr. Batson was directed to in-
vite the following persons to at-
tend tonight's meeting: Répresent-
ative Baldwin; City Councilmen
from the Sixth district-Mrs. Ella
A. Bailey, Edward A. Freburger
and Louis W. Kotmair; State Sena-
tor George Della and represent-
atives of the Governor and of the
Board of Public Works.
