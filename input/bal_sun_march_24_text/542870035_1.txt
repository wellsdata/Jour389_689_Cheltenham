The Rev. F. R. Isaac Writes Of
Cheltenham 'Conflicts'
"Sociological conflicts" and poli-
tics, among other things, contrib
uted to the Rev. F. Reid Isaac's
decision to resign from the board
of managers of the Cheltenham
School for Boys, according to his
letter of resignation, which was
made public yesterday by Governor
O'Conor.
In the letter, dated October 24.
the Rev. Mr. Isaac wrote, in part:
"The intricacies of fiscal affairs,
sociological conflicts, aggravated
by the race question. which is
intensified by the location of the
institution, the organizational rela-
tionship of the board to the total
State welfare plan, involving un-
certainty as to function, and the
political element which is un-
avoidably present in any public
enterprise-all these things and
more, leave me with a feeling of
utter frustration.
