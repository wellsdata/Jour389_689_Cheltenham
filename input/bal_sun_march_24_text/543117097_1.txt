CROWNSVILLE
IMPROVEMENTS
COMPLETED
Four Additions To State
Hospital To Be Inspected
Wednesday
Institution To Take Many
Children From 500-Now
ン
On Waiting List
Four additions to the Crownsville
State Hospital, have been completed
and are to be inspected Wednesday
by the board of managers of the in-
stitution, it was announced yesterday.
Two of the additions are to the
building for feeble-minded children,
and will enlarge the capacity from
90 beds to 200. The other additions
are to the main building, to provide
-more space for occupational therapy
and add to the employes' dining room.
500 On Waiting List..
Dr. Gustave H. Faubert, resident
physician of the feeble-minded chil-
dren's group, said that as soon as the
buildings could be equipped-in a
month or two-the hospital would
take children from the 500 now on the
waiting list to fill the new beds.
The children will come from the
Cheltenham School for Boys and other
Negro institutions which have been
housing the children until they could
get hospital care, he explained.
Improvements Cost $140,000-
The Crownsville building program
has been undertaken with State ap-
propriations to the institution supple-
mented by a forty-five per cent. grant
from the Public Works Administra-
tion. Cost, of the four additions was
approximately $140,000. The architects
were Crisp & Edmunds, of Baltimore.
The institution is the only one in the
State for Negro insane and feeble-
minded. The first unit for feeble-
minded patients was completed in 1937.
The superintendent' is Dr. R. P.
Winterode.
