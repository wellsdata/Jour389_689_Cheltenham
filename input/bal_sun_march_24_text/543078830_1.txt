By LOUIS J. O'DONNELL
Governor O'Conor is determined to
put an end to the oft-recurring re-
ports of brutality, starvation and mis-
management emanating from the
House of Reformation for Colored
Boys at Cheltenham.
He let it be known yesterday that
he intends to make a personal investi-
gation of conditions at the Prince
George's county institution taken over
by the State two years ago.
The Governor revealed his plans!
after a visit to his Annapolis office of
a delegation-the third in as many
days-protesting against the treatment
of the Cheltenham inmates.
Silent On Remedy +
Whether he has in mind a reorgani-
zation of the institution, or a complete
shift in its personnel, the Governor
declined to say.
"I am going to find out what is nec-
essary for me to do to rectify condi-
tions that certainly must exist at
Cheltenham," he said, "and then I am
going to do it."
The Cheltenham question has been
in the news almost constantly since
the State took it over in 1937. By court
order the State was obliged to take
over the personnel that operated it
as a private institution.
Refers To Charges
From time to time inmates, investi-
gators and ex-members of the staff
have called practices there intolerable,
- citing incidents of underfeeding and
Ibeating of inmates and unsanitary and
disease-breeding conditions.
!,
The personal investigation planned
by the Governor is in line with a
new policy he inaugurated yesterday-
surveying State institutions and de-
partments and attending meetings of
various boards and commissions.
Yesterday he attended a session of
the Maryland Tuberculosis Sanatoria
Board of Managers at the office of
Dr. Warren H. Buckler, chairman.
Will Study Data
He came away with a mass of data
and statistics for further study, in-
cluding per capita cost comparisons
with other States, a report on exist-
ing facilities and needs and a break-
down of the sanatoria's fiscal setup.
He expects in the future to attend
sessions of the State Roads Commis-
sion, the State Tax Commission, the
Board of Welfare and other such com-
missions, and to make periodic calls
on the heads of various other depart-
ments.
The Penitentiary, House of Correc-
tion and Penal Farm and all the in-
stitutions for handicapped and delin-
quent children will be visited.
