The attorney, who is endeavoring

in compiling his report.

As other subsidies from the taxpayer

Calls Legislation Astounding
Speaking of legislation concerning
the company, Mr. Sherbow said the
Ac: of 1935 "is one of the most astound-
ing pieces of legislation ever put on
the books.". In that statute, he as-

purchasing the concern for a mini-
mum of. $1,200,000 in three per cent.
bonds, if and when purchase was
made.
"This," he continued, "regardless
of the fact that the State can sell its
bonds for one and one-half per cent.
in the open market."
Other legislation provides that no
competing ferries may be set up with-
in twenty miles or ten miles of the
two sites under consideration for the
proposed Chesapeake Bay bridge. In
addition, he declared, if the State does
take over the ferry, it would be bound
not to lower rates.
All such legislation could be re-
moved, and the way cleared for the
State either to purchase the company
or to found a competing transporta-
tion system, by an act of the Legis-
lature, Mr. Sherbow declared. Al-
through members of the council ex-
pressed opinions to the contrary, the
