MORE RIGID CURB
ON PARKING URGED
Grand Jury Group Favors
Complete Ban On Main
Downtown Streets
Creation Of Special Commit-
tee To Draw Traffic Rules
Also Advocated
Abolition of parking on major thor-
oughfares in the downtown area and
creation of a special committee with
the power to draw regulations govern-
ing traffic are among the recommen-
dations which have been submitted by
the traffic committee of the retiring
grand jury.
The parking situation on Charles
street, which has been discussed, pro
and con by almost everybody for the
past few years, again was given a
prominent position. The outgoing body
advocated the prohibition of parking
on that street between Centre and
Baltimore streets.
:
Other Streets Mentioned
Other streets on which a no-park-
ling law was advocated are:
Franklin street between St. Paul
and Paca streets.
Howard street between Richmond
Market, or the south end of the
Howard street extention, and
Madison street.
North avenue between Charles
street and Madison avenue.
More one-way streets with more
intersections carrying a left-hand turn
ban also were suggested, as was the
substitution of a block system of traffic-
light control instead of the present
wave system now in use on many
thoroughfares.
Market Situation Cited
As for the traffic situation around
the Lexington Market, that was de-
scribed by the grand jury committee
as a "classic traffic abuse."
With a view to studying and cor-
recting traffic conditions throughout
the city the committee, headed by
Henry C. Miller, advocated the crea-
tion of a special traffic committee to be
added to the City Planning Commis-
sion, with powers to set up regulations
f
use of the streets.
There was a divergence of opinion
in the report by the committee on
Cheltenham, the house of reformation
for Negro boys.
Report Of Majority
The majority report was signed by
David W. Bridges, committee chair-
man; Louis G. Groener and Mr. Mil-
ler. The minority report was filed by
Howard H. Murphy, Negro member of
the grand jury and one of the Chel-
tenham committee.
• ..
In the majority report, management
of the institution was called "satis-
factory and efficient
considering
the difficulties they are working under
in respect to more or less antiquated
equipment, old buildings and a limited
budget."
The minority report claimed "the
cows and hogs get much better atten-
Lion than the boys themselves" and
The barns and cowsheds are in better
condition than the dormitories.
Negro Staft Urged
Replacement of the present staff
with a Negro personnel also was sug-
gested in the minority statement.
It also was said that "Prince
George's county seems to regard Chel-
Lenham as a private political plum and
through its control of the board mem-
bership foists upon this institution
the incompetent political employes
whom Commissioner Jones (Harry C.
Jones, Employment Commissioner),
says are unfit."
