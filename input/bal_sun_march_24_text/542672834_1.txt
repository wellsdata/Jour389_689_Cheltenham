7 BOYS CONVICTED
IN 33 THEFT CASES
Loot From Stores And Shops
Exceeds $12,000
Pleading guilty to 33 charges of
stealing goods and cash totaling be-
ween $12,000 and $15,000 from
stores and shops in "Boomtown,"
civilian commercial center near
Fort George G. Meade, seven
youths have been convicted in Juve-
mile Court at Annapolis, State po-
ice at Waterloo reported yester-
day.
The convictions mark the final
cleanup of all robberies reported
in the area over a period of three
years, Sergt. Thomas M. Hignutt,
acting commander of the Waterloo
police, declared.
Sergeant lignutt gave credit to
Corporal Wilton R. May and State
Trooper Noble Collison for break-
ing the case, He said they have
been investigating for more than
a year.
Called X-9 Club
In June, 1946, nine Negro youths,
who called themselves the "X-9
Club." were convicted of thefts in
the "Boomtown" area, but a num.
ber of other robberies remained
unsolved.
The roundup of the seven youths
last week cleared up all the thefts.
This group was picked up on
Wednesday and Thursday after one
of them. a 16-year-old Negro, of
Harmons, Md., had been questioned
in connection with a robbery Tues-
day night. Sergeant Hignutt said.
