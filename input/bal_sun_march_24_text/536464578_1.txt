ST. JOHN'S COLLEGE
List Of Graduates In The Class Of
This Year,
[Special Dispatch to the Baltimore Sun.]
ANNAPOLIS, MD., June 12.-The graduat-
ing class of St. John's College this year con-
sists of 21 members, all Maryland boys, ex-
cept one, who hails from West Virginia.
The following is the standing of the class
for the four years' course:
1. W. S. Bradley, of Hurlock.
2. Louis H. Seth, of Whitman.
3. Charles S. Despard, Parkersburg, W. Va.
4. William W. Keys, Baltimore.
5. Leroy P. Baker, of Preston.
6. Edward H. Tarburton, Sudlersville.
7. W. O. La Motte, La Motte.
8. Augustus W. Bradford, Belair.
9. E. W. Putherbridge, Charlotte Hall.
10. William O. Spates, Poolesville.
11. A. A. Jackson, Jacksonsville.
12. Samuel G. Townsend, Cheltenham.
13. Alexander Randall, Annapolis.
14. Edward H. Huchins, Barstowe.
15. Julian H. Beard, Rutland.
16. Richard B. Spencer, Annapolis.
17. T. Spencer Crane, California.
18. Wade H. Goedy, Berlin.
10. William P. Ward, Gamber.
20. J. F. Linthicum, Annapolis.
21. S. W. Ahalt, Burkettsville.
The first prize in the oratorical contest-a
gold medal presented by the president, Dr.
Fell, for the best oration-was won by E.
Barton Garey. He delivered the speech of
Regulus to the Carthagenians. For second
honors Leroy T. Rohrer and Walton H.
Grant were in the lead, but the judges
could not agree, so it was not decided.
The judges were Rev. Joseph P. McCo-
mas, Surgeon T. C. Walton, United States
Navy, retired. and Prof. Charles E. Dryden.
