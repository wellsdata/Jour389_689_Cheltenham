A Nurse For Miss Clark.
Rev. John M. Giraud, S. J., assistant
pastor of Sacred Heart Catholic Church,
White Marsh, Prince George's county,
visited Loyola College, in Baltimore, yes-
terday. He is the priest who accompanied
John Berry, the young mulatto who mur-
dered Miss Amanda Clark and attempted
the life of her sister.
Father Giraud returned to Prince
George's county yesterday afternoon with
one of the Sisters of Bon Secours, who will
nurse Miss Annie Clark. He said Berry
called for him at 11.25 o'clock Saturday
night, and he was brought back to the
priesthouse by Joseph Clark, a nephew of
the victims.
The Prisoner In Jail.
Berry was more composed yesterday at
the city jail, where he was brought for
safe keeping, and did not seem to be great-
ly worried about his probable fate. Father
Boone, of Loyola College, visited him in
the morning and administered spiritual
consolation, remaining some time. He
promised to get a prayerbook for Berry,
which he asked for. One of the directors
of Cheltenham Reformatory School for
Colored Boys, while at the fall on other
business, stopped in Berry's cell and talked
with him about his statement that the in-
stitution is not a fit place for boys. Mem-
bers of the grand jury also took a look at
Berry while at the jail. Berry was very
much tickled with a picture of himself,
sent him by an artist, and which was
taken Monday, and he took pride in show-
ing it to other prisoners.
