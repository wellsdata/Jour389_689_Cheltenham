Cheltenham, Md., March 17-The
board of managers of the Cheltenham
School for Boys wants its legal posi-
tion clarified before it takes action on
the personnel ultimatum issued by
Harry C. Jones, State Employment
Commissioner, it indicated today.
After a three-hour session behind
closed doors, the board announced that
Joseph H. Blanford, president, would
appoint a committee to confer with
the State Employment Commissioner
on the "legal technicalities" involved.
Statement Unclarified
Howard C. Hill, board member who
acted as spokesman, declined to clarify
this statement in any way.
Thus it
was not made known whether the
board wants to concur in Mr. Jones'
recommendations or to oppose them.
By determining to clarify the legal
points involved, however, the board
implied that it was by no means satis-
fied that it could follow Mr. Jones'
recommendation and still meet its ob-
ligations to its employes, as outlined
in the 1937 statute making Cheltenham
a State institution.
The State Employment Commis-
sioner informed the board yesterday
that, after giving the institution's staff
2 classification tests, he had found nearly
half the fifty-seven employes un-
qualified for any classification at all.
Would Hold Up Pay Roll
Unless the board agrees to replace
these persons, the Employment Com-
missioner said he would decline to
approve the institution's April pay
roll.
The statute, however, says that
Cheltenham's employes shall be ac-
cepted into the State Merit System
"without need of special examination
as to fitness."
Today's developments strongly in-
dicated that there will be a court test.
of the Employment Commissioner's
right to decline to admit the "unfit"
employes into the State Merit System.
