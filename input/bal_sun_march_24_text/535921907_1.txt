APPROPRIATIONS SOUGHT
The Confederate Soldiers' Home Asks
$10,000-Delegations Representing
School And Charities.
[Special Dispatch to the Baltimore Sun.]
ANNAPOLIS. MD., Feb. 17.-A number of
delegations appeared before the Senate
finance and House ways and means com.
mittees today to urge appropriations to
various institutions. Gen. Bradley T.
Johnson, Col. George R. Gaither. Capt.
George W. Booth, Robert M. Elundon,
James R. Wheeler and Wm. H. Pope, su-
perintendent of the Confederate Soldiers'
Home, at Pikesville, who asked $10,000 for
the institution.
It received formerly
$7.500.
Messrs. Milton G. Urner, W. P. T. Tur-
pin, Aubrey Pearre and Benjamin Bissell,
of the Maryland Asylum and Training
School for the Feeble Minded, at Owing's
Mills, asked $20,000, or more. They desire
to erect a separate building to be used as
a manual training department of the insti-
tution.
Messrs. Thomas H. Bond, William Bur-
roughs and G. M. Thomas, the principal of
Charlotte Hall Military School, asked an
Increase to $5.000 from the $2,000 hereto
fore appropriated.
Mr. James R. Wheeler and two sisters, of
the Mission Helpers of Baltimore City,
asked $1.000. The institution has never
received an appropriation from the city or
State.
Messrs. J. J. Janney, Frank Woods, Ed-
ward Stabler, Jr., Nathan Thombpson au-l
Henry C. Reed, of the House of Reforma-
tion for Colored Boys, (Cheltenham.)
asked the usual appropriation of $10,000
and an additional appropriation to enlarge
and repair the institution.
Judge Parran Crane and Messrs. D. C.
Hammett. B. H. Camalier and G. F.
Dryer, of St. Mary's Female Seminary,
asked $6.000 instead of $2.500 since 1868.
Mr. H. M. Hebben, of the Northeast Day
Nursery, of Baltimore, asked $1.500. The
nursery has been receiving $1,000.
Messrs. P. R. Uhler. Edward Stabler,
Jr., and Professor Hammel, of the Mary-
land Academy of Sciences, asking $2,000
for the academy.
Messrs. Thomas Drennen, I. M. Elston,
Samuel Lehman, George W. Woolford,
Thomas W. Simmons, William Moore,
John G. Hurley and Dr. Brice W. Golds-
borough, of Cambridge, came to Annapolis
in the police boat Governor McLane to
urge upon the Legislature to grant an ap-
propriation for the Cambridge Hospital.
