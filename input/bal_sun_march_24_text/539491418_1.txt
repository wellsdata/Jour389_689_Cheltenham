By LOUIS J. O'DONNELL
[Annapolis Bureau of The Sun]
Annapolis, March 5-After almost
two months' labor ostensibly directed
toward effecting economies in State
expenditures, the Legislature tonight
brought forth a gross increase of $303,-
000 in the budget for the next two
years-with roughly one third of the
additional funds going to the Uni-
versity of Maryland.

Actually, the net increase in the
budget, however, will amount to only
$87,000 in the two-year period, since
Governor O'Conor, to meet pressure
of the General Assembly, cut the ap-
propriations to the Department of
Welfare for 1942-1943 by approximately
$216,000.

One Cut Is Made
The months of labor of both the
House Ways and Means Committee
and the Finance Committee of the
Senate, which received the $106,664,000
biennial appropriations bill early in
January amid declarations for
economy and reduced expenditure,
produced only one cut.

This item was $3,500 for purchase
of cash registers for Baltimore's
Traffic Court and was recommended
by Chief Magistrate Robert France.

All this was revealed tonight in the
supplemental budget submitted to both
houses by Governor O'Conor.

The supplemental budget was pre-
pared by the Governor only after the
Legislature's fiscal committees let him
know informally what action they had
taken and advised that they were
ready-when the supplemental bill
was sent in-to adopt the appropri-
ations measure.

$2,019 Extra Allowed
Conditional upon the passage of
legislation, the Governor allowed
$2,019 extra for the State Police De-
partment to be used for salaries in
the event the Legislature creates a
majorship and two captaincies in the
officered ranks of the force.

Contrary to advance reports, the
Ways and Means Committee left in the
budget appropriations of more than
$100,000 for State police teletype
system and personnel to operate it.

Two lump-sum appropriations of
$25,000 cach-or a total of $50,000-were
allotted to the University of Maryland
to be distributed by the board of re-
gents for use in the agricultural work
of the institution, particularly for con-
trol of plant and animal diseases,
and problems relating to marketing of
agricultural products.

Byrd Asked For $172,000
Requests of Dr. H. C. Byrd, presi-
dent of the university, for appro-
priations for these purposes aggregated
$172,000. Only a few days ago repre-
sentatives of farm organizations inter-
ested in the various items called on
the Governor to press for the funds
denied in the original budget.

The Board of Public Works will pass
on the allocation of this $50,000 by
the board of regents.
