as general secretary of the Henry Wat-
son Children's Aid Society and his
- acceptance of the duties of secretary
- and manager of the St. Louis Chil-
dren's Aid Society and the Provident
Association, was announced yesterday
by Philip W. Ness, president of the
local society.
Mr. Beisser's duties in St. Louis will
begin on September 1, Mr. Ness said.
The two Missouri organizations cover
the family welfare work of the city
of St. Louis and the child-welfare
work of that city and St. Louis
county.
Has Held Post Here 17 Years
.Mr. Beisser has been general secre-
tary of the local society for seventeen
years. He is a graduate of the Univer-
sity of Pennsylvania, the Wharton
School of Finance and the New York
School of Social Work.
Before coming to Baltimor he served
as secretary and legislative agent of
the Children's Division, Public Chari-
ties Association of Pennsylvania; re-
search secretary, American Association
of Social Workers; field secretary, New
York School of Social Work, and as
an economist in the Federal Bureau
of Labor and Statistics.
Made Several Surveys
In addition to his other duties he
has made community surveys in Hart-
ford, Providence, Rochester. Stam-
ford, Kansas City and Honolulu; was
active in the Home Relief Bureau of
New York city; secretary of the Gov-
ernor's Commission on Juvenile De-
linquency in 1940, and a member of
the commission to study Cheltenham
School for Colored Boys in 1939.
From 1929 to 1933 he was president
of the Maryland State Conference on
Social Welfare; was a member of the
executive committee, National Con-
ference of Social Work from 1933 to
1935, and president of the Child Wel-
fare League of America from 1938 to
1940.
His Work Lauded
James M. Hepbron, director of the
Community Fund, of which the Henry
Watson Children's Aid Society is a
member, said yesterday, "Mr. Beisser's
professional attainments are of the
highest order and he is generally rec-
ognized, not only locally, but through-
out the country as one of the leaders
in the child-welfare field.
"He came to Baltimore seventeen
years ago and has done more to raise
the general social work and welfare
standards of this city than any other
one individual.
"Successive governors and mayors
have called upon him to head com-
missions and in working out legislative
matters. Other cities in the country
have consistently sought his services
for surveys in his particular field. He
has been an outstanding asset to the
city and I greatly regret that he is
leaving us."
Wishes Beisser Success
Mr. Hess said, "I speak for our en-
tire board of managers when I say
that it is with real regret that we lose
Mr. Beisser to St. Louis, and we wish
him every success in his new position
with its enlarged opportunities and
responsibilities..
"During the last seventeen years in
which Mr. Beisser has been our gen-
eral secretary, the Henry Watson Chil-
dren's Aid Society has made vast
strides forward in the child-welfare
work in this community, and we pro-
pose to continue this much-needed
work at its present high standards
under a new executive."
Mr. Beisser is married and has two
sons, Paul, Jr., 21, and James E., 18.
He is a member of the University Club
of Baltimore.
