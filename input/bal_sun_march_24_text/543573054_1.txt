Governor Considering Paroles
For Two Negro Boys In House
Of Correction.
With tive juvenile prisoners in the
Cheltenham Reformatory, each costing
county $200 a year, St. Mary's coumy
cannot afford to send more youthful)
violators of the law to the institution,
George B. Cecil, president of the Board
of County Commissioners for St.
Mary's, said yesterday.
Instead, the juvenile offenders are
sent to the Ilouse of Correction, where
the county does not have to pay for
their support.
Paroles Under Consideration.
Mr. Cecil made this statement after
Howard C. Hill, executive secretary
of the Prisoner's Aid Society, had
charged that St. Mary's county does
not care properly for its youthful
transgressors. Mr. IMill recalled that a
14-year-old Negro boy last fall was
sent to the Rouse of Correction for n
criminal offense and that Governor
Ritchie released the boy immediately
after the case had been called to his
attention..
At the present Governor Ritchie has
recommendations of parole from Ed-
ward M. Parrish, State Parole Com-
missioner, under consideration for two
Negro boys, one 33 and the other 15,
who were sent to the House of Cor-
rection from St. Mary's county.
"About All" County Could Pay.
Mr. Cecil said that allowance for the
county prisoners in Cheltenham is
made each year when the levy for the
county is struck and that St. Mary's
county cannot afford to send more
youthful prisoners there.
He added that $1,000 a year for the
support of the five now in the reforma-
tory was "about all" the county could
pay.
