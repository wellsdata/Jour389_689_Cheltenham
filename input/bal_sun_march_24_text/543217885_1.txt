Cheltenham Dispute
Brought To City Court
Jones Given Until Friday To Show
Cause Why He Should Not
Pay 23 Employes
Legal controversy over the State's
refusal to accept twenty-three em-
ployes of the Cheltenham School for
Boys into the State merit system was
transferred to Baltimore yesterday
when mandamus proceedings were in-
stituted against Harry C. Jones, State
Employment Commissioner, in Supe-
rior Court.
The suit was filed through Enoch
Harlan and Charles C. Marbury, coun-
sel for the board of managers of the
House of Reformation. The petition
states that the plaintiff has a money
interest in the case, as well as a natural
interest in the welfare of the affected
employes.
Judge Rowland K. Adams signed
an order directing Mr. Jones to show
cause why he should not approve
pay rolls for these employes, giving
him until Friday to file his answer.
The case was set for hearing July 11.
