Grand Jury at Cheltenham.
[Reported for the Baltimore Sun.l

The members of the grand jury visited the
House of Reformation for Colored Boys at
Cheltenham, Prince George's county, yester-
day. They left in a special car furnished by
the Baltimore and Potomac Railroad. starting
from Calvert Station at 7.35 A. M. At the
station at Cheltenham carriages were await-
ing them and they were driven to the insti-
tution, about a mile, arriving at ten o'clock,
where they were received by Captain Isaiah
Waggner, the superintendent, and his assist-
ants, and conducted by the latter through
the schools, dormitories and workshops and
over the farm lands. Gen. Horn and Col.
Zimmerman, of the board of managers, ac-
companied the parry. The 251 boys were
viewed at their noon-day meal, at their
studios, work and play. They were appar-
ently contented and well cared for.
the afternoon the party partook of a
homely and substantial collation, after
which addresses were made by Gen. Horn,
Mr. T. B. Hull, Mr. W. F. C. Gerhardt, Rev.
Mr. Brown, Captain Wagener, Col. Zimmer-
man, and the foreman of the jury, Mr. Henry
C. Wagner. The present grand jury has
been harmonious and industrious, having sat
76 days, disposed of over 1,200 cases and
examined more than 3,000 witnesses. The
term will expire Saturday, May 7, proximo.
The grand jurors who visited Cheltenham
were: Henry C. Wagner, foreman, T. Bur-
ling Hull, Robert W. Hays, William Brown,
John T. Bishop, George R. Callis, Alexander
R. Barrow, George A. Reed, William J. Gray,
Benjamin F. Clark, John P. Merritt, Jacob
H. Timanus, William F. C. Gerhardt, Frank
H. Kampe, James R. Seager. Eight of the
members were absent.
