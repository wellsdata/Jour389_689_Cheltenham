Mabel Boll's Boy Friend
Tries To Kill Himself
Professional Hoofer Found On
Nice Estate Of "Queen Of
Diamonds": :
Nice, France, May 15 (AP)-A dancing
boy friend of Mabel Boll shot himself
on the lawn of her new villa here
today, but if he was trying to kill
himself, the police said, his aim was
inaccurate.
Also, if the 27-year-old professional
hoofer, Georges Charlot, was despair-
ing because the "Queen of Diamonds"
was about to throw him over, which is
the police theory, the Countess Dela
Porcerie (Mabell Boll's present name)
wouldn't confirm it.
The countess, who is divorced from
the man who gave her the title, found
Charlot in a pool of blood on the lawn
when she returned to her villa at Saint
Jean Capperrant after a shopping trip
to Nice.
A bullet was extracted from Char-
lot's side, and physicians at the Nice
Clinic said he will recover. The count-
ess, after hearing this news, shut her-
self up in the villa and refused to
talk about the affair.
89th Annual Baptist
Session Opens Today
Delegates From 18 States And 24,-
000 Churches Arrive In
:
Fort Worth
Fort Worth, Texas, May 15 (AP)-
Baptists from eighteen States and the
District of Columbia, representing
more than 24,000 churches, were here
tonight to attend the eighty-ninth an-
nual convention of the church in the
South.
Dr. M. E. Dodd, of Shreveport, La.,
president of the Southern Baptist
Convention, estimated that between
10,000 and 12,000 messengers would be
seated when the gavel falls tomorrow.
Mr. Dodd came to the convention
fresh from a tour of the South that
took him 52,000 miles.
"Men are going to church in larger
numbers than ever, taking an active
part in affairs of the church and
spending more time on their knees,"
he told preachers and laymen.
CURTIS ESTATE APPRAISED
Inventory Values Personalty At
$18,603,187
Norristown, Pa., May 15 (P)-The
value of the personal estate of Cyrus
H. K. Curtis, Philadelphia .publisher,
who died last June, was given as $18,-
603,187 in an inventory filed with the
register of wills today.
Most of the estate is represented by
stock in publishing organizations. His
yacht, Lyndonia, was placed at $150,-
000 and furnishings at his home at
Cheltenham, Pa., were listed at $50,727.
