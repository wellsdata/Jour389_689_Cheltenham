NEW CHELTENHAM
SITE ABANDONED
Title To Property Purchased
Found 'Defective'
| Because a highway to connect
with the new municipal airport is
planned to pass through the site
purchased by the State for a new
Cheltenham School for Boys, the
Cheltenham board of managers has
withdrawn its approval of the tract,
Thomas B. Sprague, president of
the board, announced last night.
The 1222-acre site lies between
the Hollins Ferry road and the Old
Annapolis road between Mount
Winans and Cherry Hill. Its pur-
chase last January for construc-
tion of a Negro training school
brought protests from residents of
the area.
Clear Title Not Obtainable
A price of $45,000 was paid for
the land to the Whitaker Iron Com-
pany, of Wheeling, W.Va.
Mr. Sprague, in a statement, said
a preliminary report and survey by
the Maryland Title Company, en-
gaged by the State to ascertain the
condition of the title to the Whit-
aker tract, showed that a clear title.
could not be obtained, "among
other things due to the fact that
there is planned a highway through
the heart of the property."
"This highway is to connect with
the projected new airport," he con-
tinued. "Although the highway had
been planned prior to the signing
of the contract to purchase the
tract, this fact was not known to
the board of managers until a title
search had been made.
Another Site To Be Sought
"It is, therefore, with regret that
the board has recinded its former
action approving the purchase of
this tract of land, as it feels that
were it not for this defect in the
title, the site would be a suitable
one.
"The board further authorized
me to as promptly as possible seek
out another location for the school.
This will be done."
