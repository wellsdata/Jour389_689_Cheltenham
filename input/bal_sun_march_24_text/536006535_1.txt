PUNISHMENT TOO SEVERE
Grand Jury Criticises Cheltenham House
Of Reformation-Management
Promises To Modify It.
The grand jury committee on the House
of Reformation for Colored Boys, at Chel-
tenham, submitted a special report yester-
day commending the management and
condition of that institution, but stating
that the punishments inflicted on the
youthful inmates have been too severe.
"We regret to say that, in our opinion,"
the report states, "the punishments have
been too severe, better suited for hard-
ened adult criminals than for incorrigible
youths.
"We are pleased to report that the man-
agement have promised to modify this
somewhat by doing away with the more
severe methods. In this connection one of
the great needs of the institution is a
strong guard building, where uncontrol-
lable inmates may be safely confined under
proper police protection. We believe that
kind treatment and moral suasion will do
more to serve the purposes of the institu-
tion than harsh and severe punishments."
On June 27 the committee visited the in-
stitution with other members of the grand
jury. The report states that they found
"the management able and efficient, and
thoroughly devoted to their work, the
buildings and grounds clean and in good
condition, the food wholesome and abund-
ant, and the dormitories clean, well lighted
and ventilated."
The report heartily Indorses the recom-
mendation of the last grand jury for an
appropriation of $17,000 to make necessary
improvements. An adequate water supply,
with fire plugs and hose to be attached to
the steam pumps to promptly extinguish
a fire, is especially urged. Appropriations
of $15,000 from the city, and $12,000 from
the State are recommended.
Messrs. Frank Nolen, Thomas W. Morse,
John R. Young, R. Jandorf and Wm. H.
Ferris are the members of the committee,
and all signed the report.
