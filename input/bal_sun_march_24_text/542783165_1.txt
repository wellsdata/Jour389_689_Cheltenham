Man, 76, Gets Two Years
For Buying Stolen Food
From Children
Described by Judge Charles E
Moylan as the operator of "a schoo
of crime for 11 and 12-year-old
boys," Fred Sheppard, 76-year-old
father of 25 children, yesterday
was sentenced to two years in the
House of Correction for contribut
ing to the delinquency of five Ne
gro boys.
Three other adults were fined
and two of them received three
months' suspended sentences on the
same charges. All the defendants
are Negroes.
Sheppard, who said he had eight
children by his first wife and seven-
teen by a second wife, was charged
with buying hams, turkeys, chick-
ens, sugar and other articles of
food stolen by the five boys in
Lexington Market recently. He
also was alleged to have bought a
stolen radio and bicycle.

Youngest Child is 14
The elderly defendant told the
Court that his youngest child is 14
years old and that his second wife
died about five years ago. On ques-
tioning by the court, he revealed
that none of the children was the
result of a multiple birth.
Before sentencing the man, Judge
Moylan said the evidence showed
that the boys made "pop's place"
their "hangout" and that they made
several trips a day to the market
to steal articles.

"Clear As A Bell"
The judge declared the case
might have presented a problem to
the court, but that he believed that
Sheppard's mind was "as clear as
a bell" and that the accused knew
what he was doing.
The five juveniles were given
hearings at the same time and all
of them were found to be delin-
quent. One of the boys already is
an inmate of the Cheltenham
School for Boys and was brought
from the school for the hearing.
Four of the boys had been in the
hands of juvenile authorities previ-
ously, according to court officials.
Judge Moylan asked for additional
reports on three of the boys before
disposing of their cases.

Boy Put On Probation
He placed on probation the fifth
boy, who, although it was testified
he had been present when articles
were taken and when they were
sold, was not said to have partic-
ipated in the thefts or to have re-
ceived any money from stolen
goods.
It was said in court that the
thefts occurred during November
and December. In most of the
cases, it was said, the boys simply

"Expensive Ham"
Sheppard's daughter-in-law,
identified as Agnes Sheppard, was
one of the three others convicted
of contributing to the boys' delin-
quency. She was fined $50 and
given a three-months' suspended
sentence.
The same penalty was imposed
on Percy McCloud, a neighbor of
the Sheppards. McCloud's mother,
who allegedly bought a stolen ham,
was fined $25 with Judge Moylan
commenting:
"That will be the most expensive
ham you ever bought."

Convicted 50 Years Ago
Two other adults who appeared
on the same charges were acquitted
by Judge Moylan.
Questioned concerning his past
record, Sheppard said he thought
he might have been convicted of
assault about 50 years ago.
The case was in the Division
for Juvenile Causes. Sheppard
lives in the 200 block of North
Pearl street.
