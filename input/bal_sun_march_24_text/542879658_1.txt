Cheltenham Site
Protests Slated
Protests against the proposed
erection of a new Cheltenham
school for Negro boys near Mount
Winans will be heard at 2 P.M.
today in the City Hall and at 10
A.M. January 30, at the offices of
the State Department of Public
Welfare, 120 West Redwood street,
it was announced yesterday.
The protests will be made by a
committee representing fourteen
organizations and about 3,500 resi-
dents of South Baltimore.
Governor O'Conor said the sec-
ond meeting, at which he will be
present, will be conducted by repre-
sentatives of the State Welfare
Board and the board of managers
of the Cheltenham School for Boys,
now in Prince Georges county.
Purchased several weeks ago by
the State for $45,000, the 122/2-acre
tract lies between the Hollins Ferry
road and the old Annapolis road
between Mount Winans and Cherry
Hill.
