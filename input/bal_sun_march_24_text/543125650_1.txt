Can't Represent Employes
He made public an opinion by Mr.
O'Conor, which had been sent to him
by Joseph H. Blandford, president of
the Cheltenham board, in which the
Attorney General said he could not
represent State employés in any dis-
pute with the Employment Com-
missioner.
Under the statute which makes the
State Law Department the sole legal
adviser of all State departments and
agencies, Mr. O'Conor explained, his
office acts as counsel to the State
Employment Commissioner.
Suggests Private Counsel
"Under this statute, this department
has consistently declined to represent
individual employes who may be in-
volved in litigation arising from their
duties, or to render legal advice to
such employes," he said.
"We have taken the view that such
employes should consult private coun-
sel. In this matter, twenty-four em-
