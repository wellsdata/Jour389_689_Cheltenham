APPROPRIATIONS APPROVED
Board of Estimates' Allowances
For Reformatory Institution.
The Board of Estimates acted finally in
approving the appropriations for the vari-
ous reformatory institutions at the meeting
yesterday morning.
The appropriation asked by the Maryland
School for Boys was $1.500 less than last
year, the reduction being on account of the
fact that less repairs will be necessary, as
the institution will leave its present home
before the year is over. The appropriation
this year will be $47,000, as against the ap-
propriation for last year of $49,400.
This appropriation, as is the case with
the other reformatory institutions, only
fixes the maximum amount which can he
expended, the actual amount being gor-
erned within the limits of the appropria-
tion by the quarterly bills rendered to the
city, according to the number of city in-
mates.
The St. Mary's Industrial School asked
for $48,000, as against an appropriation of
last year of $46.000, the authorities saying
that they expected a large Increase in the
number of inmates during the year.
No improvements will be necessary at
the Industrial Home for Colored Girls this
year, and, as a consequence, the appropria-
tion could be reduced from $8,400 last year
to $6,000 this year.
Owing to an increase in the number of
city inmates from 65 to 71, the House of
the Good Shepherd for Girls asked for an
increase, which was granted, from $4.745
last year to $5,188 this year.
The appropriation for the House of the
Good Shepherd for Colored Girls was in-
creased from $4,332 last year to $4,560 this
year the increase being due to an increase
in the per capita charge of $3, from $57
to $60.
The Female House of Refuge appropria-
tion this year will be $9,900, as against
$10.120 last year, the reduction being due
to the fact that fewer repairs will be neces-
sary.
Cheitenham, the House of Reformation
for Colored Boys, was granted $23,200, a re-
duction of $400, due to the smaller number
of boys-200, against 205 of last year.
The National Junior Republic
granted $4,200, the same amount as last
year, and the Home for Fallen Women was
given $1.080, the same as last year.
