ENOCH HARLAN DIES
WHILE ON VACATION
Baltimore Attorney Victim.
Of Pneumonia At St.
Petersburg, Fla.
Formerly Headed Board Of
House Of Reformation,
Cheltenham
Enoch Harlan, senior member of the
law firm of Harlan & Harlan and
former president of the board of the
House of Reformation at Cheltenham,
died yesterday in St. Petersburg, Fla.,
where he was on vacation.
Mr. Harlan, according to word
received from his wif who was ac-
companying him, was removed to a
hospital and placed in an oxygen tent
Thursday after developing pneumonia.
He died at 9 A. M. yesterday.
Native Of Delaware
Born at Wilmington, Del., November
15, 1867, Mr. Harlan was graduated
from the University of Maryland Law
School in 1897. In 1898 he married
Mary Susanna Benson.
He became president of the House
of Reformation Board before the in-
stitution was taken over by the State,
and remained as one of its directors
after the change.
He also was a member of the board
of the Boys' Home Society and of the
board of the Thomas Wilson Fuel
Saving Society. He was a member of
the Friends Society and of the Bar
Association of Baltimore City, the
Maryland Bar Association, and the
American Bar Association.
Formed Law Firm In 1935
In 1935, with his twin sons, Edwin
and Joseph Harlan, he founded the
law firm of Harlan & Harlan.
Edwin yesterday left by plane to
meet his mother in Florida and to
make plans for the funeral,
In addition to his wife and twin
sons, Mr. Harlan is survived by a
daughter, Mrs. Helen Harlan Bag-
galey, of Chicago; another son, Enoch
L. Harlan, two sisters, Mrs. Cora H.
Johnson, of Seattle, and Mrs. Helen
H. Marshall, of Evansville, Ind., and a
brother, J. Paul Harlan, of Oxford, Pa.
Mr. Harlan lived at 2914 Garrison
Boulevard.
