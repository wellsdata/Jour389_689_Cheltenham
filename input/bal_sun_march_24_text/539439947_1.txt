LANE PICKS 9
FOR BOARD OF
CHELTENHAM
New All-Negro Group To
Meet Friday To Hear
Governor's Ideas
Governor Lane yesterday ap-
pointed a new, all-Negro board of
managers for the Cheltenham
School for Boys.
The new board, consisting of nine
members, replaces the group of five
white and four Negro members
which resigned January 24.
Governor Lane has requested
that the new board meet in the
State House at 3 P.M. Friday for
the purpose of organization.
New Members Listed
Members of the new board are:
DR. WILLIAM E. HENRY, president
of the State Teachers College at
Bowle.
MRS. VIOLET HILL WHYTE, former
schoolteacher, who now is a Balti-
more policewoman.
MRS. BERTHA B. PROCTOR, of Balti-
more, a member of the Cheltenham
Board since June 1, 1938, who was
reappointed.
DR. JAMES ARTHUR WHITE. of Bal-
timore, a surgeon-dentist.
T. WALLACE LANSEY, of Baltimore,
co-owner and manager of a laundry.
CHARLES E. CORNISH, a member of
the city council of Cambridge and
president of the Maryland Congress
of Colored Parents and Teachers.
WILLARD A. ALLEN, of Baltimore,
president of the Southern Life In-
surance Company.
THE REV. JOHN THOMAS COLBERT,
of Baltimore, pastor of Grace Pres-
byterian Church and moderator of
the Baltimore Presbytery in 1944
and 1945.
LIEUT. COL. WILLIAM M. BRADY,
Maryland National Guard of Balti-
more, employe of the Veterans Ad-
ministration.
Governor Lane plans to appear
before the board on Friday to out-
line the objectives to which he
wishes the new members to devote
themselves.
