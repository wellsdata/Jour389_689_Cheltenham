9 BOYS HUNTED
AFTER ESCAPE
12 Flee Cheltenham School;
3 Found In Hiding
State police and St. Marys
county authorities early this morn-
ing were searching for nine in-
mates who escaped from the
Cheltenham School for Boys.
Charles Cephas, supervisor of the
school, said twelve boys fled from
a cottage last night after kicking
out a window screen. They range in
age from 13 to 15. Three of them
were found hiding on the school
grounds.
Watchman Finds Broken Screen
The escape was discovered by
Daniel Ragsdale, a roving night
watchman, who noticed that the
sereen was broken in Washington
cottage.
The cottage houses 40 boys, Mr.
Cephas said.
The watchman ghecked the oc-
cupants and discovered that twelve
were missing.
He notified State police and then
began the search of grounds which
led to the discovery of the three
boys shortly after midnight.

Academy, was given a farewell
dinner tonight before leaving for
duties with Navy Department head-
quarters in Washington.
Capt. H. B. Jones has been as-

Captain Perry.
Tonight's dinner was given by

loyés of the public-works division
of the academy and the Severn
