FIVE NEGRO BOYS-
FEAST AFTER LOOT
Tale Of Grandmammy's $180
Loss And Round-Up At
Bridge Told In Court.-
Five little negro boys sat in a row
under the Stony Run bridge and ate
and ate and ate and ate.
One of the boys, it is alleged, stole
$180.50 from his grandmother, Sarah
Perkins Morris street, on April 23
last. A week later the boys were found
under the bridge. Each is about 10
years old.
Of the stolen money $150 had been
spent for butter, bread, sausage and
fruit, of which a liberal supply re
mained. Each boy wore a khaki suit,
three had rifles and there were boxes
containing 3,000 cartridges. Only $12
of the stolen money was left.
The story was told yesterday before
Judge Robert F. Stanton, in Part 2,
Criminal Court, when an unsuccessful
effort was made to obtain the release of
one of the boys, Louis Marshall, from
the Colored House of Reformation at
Cheltenham, to which he was sentenced
in the Juvenile Court following the
round-up under the bridge.

Baltimore, felt that he should add his
felicitations to those of friends of Mag-
istrate John E. Potee, of the Southern
police district. Magistrate Potee, Tom
learned, has been mentioned for sheriff
Yesterday Tom called up the magis-
trate, saying: :
"This is Tom Murray talking. I am
coming up to see you in a minute. Wait
for me."
- The magistrate waited-5, 10 even
15 minutes. Still Tom failed to make
his appearance. Just as he had begun
to think himself hoaxed Tom made his
appearance; but such an appearance!
Escorted by Patrolman Joseph Rivers,
Tom came reluctantly into the pres-
ence of the man whom only shortly be-
fore lie had called and told he was com-
ing free and unescorted..
Tom explained volubly. He left the
telephone booth and stepped into the
street, he said, when he became in-
volved in a street brawl. Then a police-
man appeared and that was all. He
was dismissed.
