3 MEN IN CHARGE
AT CHELTENHAM
To Stay On Job Pending Choice
Of Superintendent
Cheltenham School for Boys is
being administered by a three-man'
committee pending appointment of
1
new superintendent, it was
learned yesterday.
Edward N. Wilson, secretary of
the board of managers, said the
temporary administration was
working out successfully, that
morale at the institution was good
and the buildings and grounds
were in well-kept condition.
Committee Members
Serving on the committee are
Charles Cephas, in charge of social
service; Robert Scott, business man-
ager, and George Crawford, who is
on loan from Bowie State Teachers
College to develop the educational
program at Cheltenham.
The school has been without a
superintendent for several months,
and its assistant superintendent,
H. R. Harris, resigned June 15 to
become superintendent of a boys'
training school at Hanover, Va.
Meanwhile, the board of man-
agers is having a job analysis made
of the superintendent's post in light
of recent recommendations result-
ing from a study of the institution
by the State Department of Educa-
tion.
