Elsie M.-The origin of the phrase "Man's
heart is always young" does not appear to
be traceable to an English source.
Per-
haps it is a translation from Victor Hugo's
"Hernani," act III, scene 1, where Don
Ruy Gomez says: "The heart is ever young
and ever prone to bleed:"
Le cœur est toujours jeune et peut toujours saigner.

Housekeeper. For extracting and remov-
ing grease and stains the following mixture'
has often been found reliable: Mix half a
pound each of soft soap and Fuller's earth
and form into cakes. When required for
use first moisten the stain with water, rub
one of the cakes on the spot, allow it to
dry and afterward wash off with clean
warm water.

Italla. The Garibaldi monument to be
dedicated on September 20, in Lincoln
Park, Chicago, and presented by the Ital-
ian societies of that city, is designed by
Gherardi, the Italian sculptor, of New
York. Its cost will be $10,000. The emi-
gration from Italy in 1900 rose to 153.290
persons, against 131,808 in the preceding
year. Of the former 142,933 sailed for
America, 72,663 of these to settle in the
United States.
Cobham, Va. The insect covered with
the cottony-appearing substance, found on
maple leaves, is quite a common pest on
maple trees. It is known to entomologists
as Pemphiginis Acerofolll. Kerosene emul-
sion thoroughly applied to the infected por-
tions of the trees would doubtless prove an
effective remedy, while Bordeaux mixture,
which has been tried, is only a fungicide
and will in no case kill insects.
Tenant. The Baltimore City Charter,
Section 845, provides that "If any land be
held in sald city under a lease for a month,
or any less period than a year, and the ten-
ant continues to occupy under such lease
after its expiration, he shall be deemed a
tenant for such period as the premises were
originally leased to him, and so from such
period to such period; and if his landlord
give him 30 days' notice before thetermina-
tion of any period of his tenancy it shall
terminate such tenancy."
