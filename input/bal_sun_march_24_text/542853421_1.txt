Council Adopts
School Resolution
The City Council last night |
adopted a resolution calling upon
the Board of School Commissioners
to make a survey of the O'Donnell
Heights and Graceland Park areas
to determine whether there is need
for a new school building in that
area.
The O'Donnell Heights section
has been brought to public atten-
tion recently by a strike of pupils
of School No. 288 over the lack of
public transportation from their
homes.
Other Action Refused
While adopting the public-school
resolution, the Council refused to
take immediate action on another
resolution calling upon the Mary-
land Board of Public Works "to
reconsider its action in acquiring
the property near Westport in the
Sixth legislative district for use as
a correctional institution."
..
The Board of Public Works has
designated an area in the district
as a new site for the Cheltenham
school for boys-a Negro institu-
tion.
..
•
The resolution, introduced by the
three members from the Sixth dis-
trict, Councilmen Freburger and
Kotmair and Councilwoman Bailey,
said "so much property
has
been taken for industrial uses that
very little land is left for develop.
ment for residential purposes" and
that "it is desirable that land now
zoned as residential property
should be retained for this pur-
pose."
:
Urges Immediate Attention
Councilman Freburger said the
resolution "requires our immediate
attention."
- Citing an ordinance that forbids
Federal infringement on city land
without city approval, he said
"there should be a similar ordi-
nance against State infringement."
The resolution was sent to the
Committee on Buildings and Build-
ing Regulations. The Board of Pub-
lic Works was invited to attend a
hearing at 2 P.M. Monday.
