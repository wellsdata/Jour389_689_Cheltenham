Dusty Foot Loses By Nose
In Hunt Chase At
Cheltenham
[By the Associated Press]
Cheltenham, Eng., March 2-Two
American-owned horses finished first
and second today in the Seven Springs
Handicap Steeplechase over two and
a half miles for £350.
J. H. Whitney's Thomond II, at 7
to 1, won by. four lengths from J. Am-
brose Clark's Sorley Boy, 100 to 6,
and 16 others.
Whitney's Grand National candidate,
Dusty Foot, almost made it two in a
row for the American sportsman in the
National Hunt Chase over a four-mile
course, but was nosed out by Major
Noel Furlong's Robin A Tiptoe.
Dusty Foot, the 7-to-4 favorite, ran a
game race and staged a thrilling finish
with Robin A Tiptoe. The English
horse was at odds of 100 to 6. The two
leaders were ten lengths in front of
the third horse, Steady Johnny, owned
by K. Urquhart. Ten horses of the 32
starters fell during the race.
