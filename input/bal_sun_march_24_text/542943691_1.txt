SEEKS EXPANSION
OF CHELTENHAM
O'Conor Calls Need More
Pressing Than For New Unit
The Board of Public Works may
ask the next session of the Legisla
ture to amend the State law pro
viding for construction of a new
Cheltenham School for Boys to per
mit instead early expansion and
alteration of the present institu
tion, Governor O'Conor said last
night.
• Explaining that a recent Board of
Public Works plan to spend a large
sum of money for the improvement
of Cheltenham was still under con
sideration, the Governor said the
alterations could be made during
the war to expand crowded facili-
tles, while construction of a new
school would have to wait until
after the war,
New School Authorized
The law, as passed by the Legis-
lature last year, authorizes the
board to proceed with construction
of a new training school for non-
defective Negro Juvenile delin-
quents, under the supervision of
the State Department of Public
Welfare. to replace Cheltenham
School for Boys.
Cheltenham then would be con-
verted into an institution exclu-
sively for defective Negro juvenile
delinquents, under the supervision
of the Board of Mental Hygiene.
"We are not proceeding in viola-
tion of the law," Mr. O'Conor said.
"No construction has been author-
ized on the present institution. The
project that was put out for plan-
ning was in accordance with the
plans of the Post-War Planning
Commission.
More Pressing Need
"The Board of Public Works is
considering asking the Legislature
to amend the law to permit alter-
ing the present institution instead
of building a new one. The need for
expanded facilities for normal
Negro delinquents is much more
pressing than that for facilities for
feeble-minded Negroes.
"In view of wartime construction
difficulties and the length of time
it would take to erect an entirely
new school, the board feels expan-
sion and alteration would be more
practical at the present time.
"The entire matter is before the
board. Nothing will be done of a
final nature until it is determined
if the State policy will follow the
law or ask the Legislative Council
or Legislature to introduce an
amendment changing the law."
