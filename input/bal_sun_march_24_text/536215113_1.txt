REFORMING COLORED BOYS
Managers Of The Cheltenham Insti-
tution Elected.
The annual meeting of the members and
subscribers of the House of Reformation
for Colored Bors for the election of 12
members of the board of managers was
hold yesterday afternoon at the office of
Mr. Edward Stabler, Jr., 6 South street.
The managers elected are as follows:
Goldsborough S. Griffith, Edward Stabler,
Jr., J. Erskine Carson, Joseph J. Jannes,
Frank Woods, Edward Markell. Nathaniel
G. Grasty. Edward Stinson, Baltimore; F.
Snowden Hill, ex-Congressman Charles E.
Collin and C. C. Magruder, Prince George's
county. The Governor of the State and
Mayor of Baltimore each appoint twomem-
bers of the board to look after the inter-
ests of the State and city respectively.
The Institution is located at Cheltenham,
Prince George's county. It was opened
February 4, 1873, when the first two coi-
ored boys were cornumitted to the institu-
tion. By the end of the year the inmates
had Increased to 72. Since then the his-
tory of the institution has been one of
steady growth, until now it consists of a.
farm of 750 acres and all necessary build-
Ings and modern equipment, the whole
costing about $150,000. The institution
has had a total of 2,204 boys under its eare.
The total number of Inmates at the end of
1800 was 263. The board still claims
that the appropriations made by the State
for the support of the Institution are inad-
equate.
