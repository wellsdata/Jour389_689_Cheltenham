NURSES' DANCE AT SOUTHERN
Ten. Members Of Staff From Over-
seas Are Guests.
Five hundred persons attended the
dance given last night in the Southern
Hotel' by the graduate nurses of Mercy
Hospital in honor of the pupil nurses.
Among the guests there were 25 over-
seas men, among them Lieut. Noah Wal-
ker, who was wounded at Chateau-
Thierry and Lieut. William Heathy-
both of Baltimore and ten returned
oversea nurses, all from Mercy Hospital.
One of the features of the evening was
a violin solo by Miss Woytyck, of New
York.
Dr. A. G. Rytina, chairman of the
dance committee, estimated that the
profits would be about $700. This money
is to be utilized in building a home for
the student nurses at the institution.
Mercy Hospital was one of the first to
answer the call for nurses during the
influenza epidemic at Camp Meade and
maintains one of the largest charity
wards in the city.

FIVE IN WHISKY ROUND-UP
-Two Boys Sent To Reformatories,
Two Men To Jail And One Fined.
Two boys were among four alleged
bootleggers who pleaded guilty in the
United States Court yesterday of fur-
nishing whisky to soldiers. They were
Lloyd Kirkland, who was sent to the
Reform School at Cheshire, Conn., for
11 months, and James Jefferson, colored,
who was committed to the Colored
House of Reformation at Cheltenham
for the same period.
The other two were James Casey and
Robert R. Henchey, who went to jail
for four months and two months, re-
spectively.
Robert E. Wisdom, colored, belied his
name when he undertook to take four
and a half quarts of whisky with him
upon his return to Chattanooga, Tenn.,
the other day. He was caught at the
railroad station and pleaded guilty of
attempting to take intoxicating liquor
into dry territory. He was fined $25,
and his whisky was added to the confis-
cated lot in the custody of Marshal
Stockham.
