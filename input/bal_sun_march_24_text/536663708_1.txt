BOYS ACCUSED OF BURGLARY
Three Said To Have Entered Houses
• And Carried Off Fixtures.
1
Four houses are said to have been
stripped of lead pipe and gas fixtures by
three boys who were arrested by Sergeant
Gasswein and Patrolmen Murphy and Jar-
boe, of the Western district, Wednesday.
The boys are Louis Ballock, 17 years old,
- who was committed for court by Justice
Loden; Louis Smith, 14 years old, commit-
ted for court by Judge Heuisler, and Allen
Truxton, 12 years old, colored, committed
to the Industrial School at Cheltenham by
Judge Heulsler. The two juveniles were
charged with entering 678 West Fayette
street, while Ballock is also accused of en-
tering 639, 736 and 010 West Fayette
street. The value of the articles stolen
ranged from $5 to $25, the plunder includ-
ing gas fixtures, pipe and bathtub lining.
The boys are said to have been caught in
a shed in the rear of 678 West Fayette
street Wednesday dividing their booty.
George Marks, 35 years old, 230 West
Pratt street, was released on $1,000 ball
to answer a charge of buying some of the
material from the boys, knowing it to have
been stolen
