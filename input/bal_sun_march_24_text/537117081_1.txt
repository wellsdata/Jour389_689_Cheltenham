GOOD SHOWING OF J. H. U. MEN
Baltimore Athletes Get Many Prizes
At Georgetown Meet.
[Special Dispatch to the Baltimore Sun.]
Washington, March 3.-The eighth an-
nual indoor athletic meet of the George-
town University Athletic Association, held
at Convention Hall tonight, was one of the
most successful in the organization's his-
tory.
M. W. Sheppard, the world's mile cham-
pion, won the mile run in 4 minutes and 23
seconds, a new record for the South Atlan-
tic Association. Dick Dear, of the Uni-
versity of Pennsylvania, beat Seitz, the in-
door champion, in the 50-yard invitation.
Seitz won the 50-yard handicap. The 50-
yard scholastic event was won by H. S.
Ducker, of the Baltimore City College, with
C. Stollenwerck, of the Boys' Latin School,
third.
Johns Hopkins University took all three
places in the 880-yard run. E. C. Lynch,
of Johns Hopkins, followed this by taking
first in the 50-yard novice. Indeed, Hop-
kins was a prominent factor in nearly every
event.
The relay race between Army and Navy
Preparatory and Boys' Latin School was
one of the prettiest of the evening, the for-
mer winning by a scant yard. The George-
town Preparatory and Marston's School race
furnished another close and exciting relay
event, the Preps. winning by the good work
of their last runner by 5 yards.
All the Baltimore teams showed up well
and many prizes were carried "Back, Back
to Baltimore," Johns Hopkins walked away
from George Washington in the relay race,
the first man taking a lead of 15 yards and
each successive runner increasing the dis-
tance. The University of Pennsylvania re-
lay team outran the Georgetown University
four in easy fashion in the final event of
the evening, winning by 25 yards after
Widham, the last runner, had fallen, losing
at least 25 yards.
