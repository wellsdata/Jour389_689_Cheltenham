Laxity, Poor Sanitation
Are Cited; Probers Urge
New Administrator
The September term grand jury
yesterday urged that immediate
consideration, be given to the ap.
pointment of a "competent adminis
trator" for the Cheltenham School,
where the jury said conditions were
found to be shocking.
The grand jury in a special in-
terim report to the Criminal Court
said it found conditions so bad
it requested Col. William H. Trip-
lett, a physician with extensive
training and experience in hygiene
and sanitation, to accompany the
jurors on a second visit to the
institution.
Institution Found Rundown
The jury said it found the follow-
ing:
1. The institution has been al-
lowed. to become rundown and
dilapidated.
2. Many of the staff appear to be
incompetent and unable properly
to carry on the work.
3. Dietary service is inadequate
and food served was different from
that on menus submitted.
4. The infirmary was found to
be unclean and a large part of it
used as living quarters for the
doctor, while a boy with "scabies"
was in a dormitory.
5. The kitchen was found to be
filthy and a discrepancy was found
between food served inmates and
staff.
6. The sanitary facilities in use
at the pasteurizing plant were dis-
graceful and showed lack of care
and interest by the dairyman, as
well as lack of inspection by
medical personnel' or utter in-
difference to elementary sanita-
tion by both.
7. Either insufficient money is
appropriated or the sums appropri
ated are unwisely spent or wasted.
Evidence was totally lacking of a
full utilization of means provided
for the proper management of the
school.
