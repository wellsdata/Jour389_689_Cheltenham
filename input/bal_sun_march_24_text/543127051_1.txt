Andrew T. Beckwith, suspended
cottage master at the Cheltenham re-
formatory, resigned his post yesterday
in a letter to Harry C. Jones, State
Employment Commissioner. Beckwith
denied Jones' charges that he brutally
beat and manhandled two Cheltenham
inmates.
His letter did not ask for a hearing
but criticized the employment com-
missioner for "constant interference"
in the affairs of the Southern Mary-
land Negro institution.
Beckwith was suspended by Jones
after an investigation ordered by Gov-
ernor Nice. He was given until yes-
terday to answer the complaint and
request a formal trial before Jones,
as is customary under the State civil
service law.
Incompetence Charged
Charges against him included in-
competence and inemciency, "wantonly
careless or negligent or brutal" treat-
ment of inmates; "wantonly offensive"
conduct, and a general charge of con-
duct unbecoming a State employe.
Jones said Beckwith was accused
of beating one Negro boy over the
head with a padlock, whipping him
and otherwise manhandling him, as
well as beating another inmate.
"Your charges are absolutely un-
founded," Beckwith's answer asserted.
"I have the unsolicited evidence of
five witnesses who were present that
I did not assault the boys as you
allege."
Says Boys Tried To Escape
Beckwith said the "real facts" were
that the two boys, Walter Thomas
and Elwood Waters, were attempting
to escape by propping a bed up be-
neath the window of a cell in which
they had been placed for punishment.
The cottage master said he dis-
covered the escape attempt when he
carried in the youths' supper and
ordered the bed removed.
"I merely shook Thomas and did
not beat either of them," Beckwith
asserted, adding the cell padlock re-
mained on its insp after he entered
the room. The night watchman, Beck-
with's wife and three other inmates
would testify to those facts, he con-
tinued.

the charges against me and that you
