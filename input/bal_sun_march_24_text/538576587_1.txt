Cheltenham:
The Record
Investigations of Cheltenham
and the ensuing recommenda-
tions for improvement have re-
curred with monotonous fre-
quency.
Following is a portion of the
Cheltenham record:
July 2, 1926-Grand Jurors' Association
calls Cheltenham a "reflection on the
State of Maryland."
May 10, 1930-Grand jury calls educa-
tion at Cheltenham a farce and urges
"State control of the institution.
January 12, 1935-A special committee
of the September term of the grand
jury condemns the health conditions
at Cheltenham and urges that it be
taken over by the State.
January 20, 1935-Child Welfare League
of America, Inc., censures the health
standards, diet, dormitories, clothing
and virtually all other living condi-
tions at Cheltenham and recom-
mends that the State take it over.
June 26, 1935-Members of the May
term grand jury report that condi-
tions at Cheltenham are so deplor-
able and unsanitary that Governor
Nice should close the institution im-
mediately.
December 1, 1936-In appointing n
committee to arrange plans for the
State to take control of Chelten-
ham, Governor Nice calls the insti-
tution "a blot on the good name of
the State."
January 4, 1936-Hal T. Kearns, super-
intendent of Cheltenham, reports a
mess-hall disturbance in which boys
threw chinaware, but in which no
one was injured.
July 16, 1937-A posse of Prince
George's county citizens, county and
State police are needed to quell a
riot at Cheltenham at which a hun-
dred boys escape but are later cap-
tured.
July 17, 1937-Eleven Negro youths are
placed in the Upper Marlboro jail
after the second riot at Cheltenham
in two days..
March 17, 1938-Twenty-six of the
fifty-seven members of the Chelten-
ham staff are ordered dismissed by
Harry C. Jones, State Employment
Commissioner, as unfit for present
duties.
May 2, 1938-Grand jury recommends
a probe of conditions at Chelten-
ham.
September 17, 1938-Governor Nice
orders Harry C. Jones, State Em-
ployment Commissioner, to probe
riots at Cheltenhanı.
April 18, 1939-Former cottage master
at Cheltenham charges that inmates
riot because they are half-starved
and are beaten brutally.
May 6, 1939-Grand jury urges com-
prehensive survey of conditions.
May 25, 1939-Governor O'Conor an-
nounces that he plans a personal
investigation of Cheltenham.
June 30, 1939-Governor O'Conor an-
nounces that he has appointed a
secret committee which has been
probing conditions at Cheltenham
for several weeks.
