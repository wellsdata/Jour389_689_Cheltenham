CHELTENHAM
HEAD NAMED
Calvin L. Kiah Chosen By
Board Of Managers
Calvin L. Kish, high school
principal at Cambridge, Md.. yes
terday was appointed superinten-
dent of Cheltenham School for Boys
by the institution's board of man-
agers.
The board also:
Approved a motion to ask the
Legislature to remove the Chel-
tenham superintendency from
the Merit System, applicable to
Mr. Kiah's successor.
Approved the adoption of a
handbook of rules and regula-
tions to govern the staff of the
Institution.
Mr. Kiah will assume his posi
tion around August 15. Three board
members-J. Marshall Stewart,
Howard C. Hill and Miss Bertha
Proctor-will serve as an advisory
committee to him for 30 days.
Selection Delayed
Mr. Kiah, as a veteran, held first
place on the Merit System eligi
bility list, but his selection was
delayed because he lacked previous
experience, in correctional institu
tion work.
After considerable discussion the
board agreed to give him the per
mitted six-month probationary
period and to aid him in every way
possible to "make a go" of the
position.
The group pointed out that the
heads of all correctional and penal
institutions in Maryland, except
three-one of which is Chelten-
ham-now are out of the Merit
System and may be selected solely
by their own boards of managers.
Merit System Factor:
Walter D. Owens, State employ-
ment commissioner, who supplied
the list of five names for the posi-
tion, had informed the board previ-
ously that Mr. Kiah, because of his
veteran's status, could not be
passed over in favor of any of the
other applicants on the list unless
the board could prove he was not
qualified.
The board asserted it often was
hindered in obtaining qualified su-
perintendents because of the Merit
System arrangement,
Requirements in' Handbook
The handbook will be ready for
distribution among the staff mem-
bers of the school at the end of his
week. At that time Thomas B.
Sprague, chairman of the board,
and Richard W. Emory, deputy at-
torney general, will visit the school
and explain what the staff will be
required to do.
The handbook sets forth that the
staff, must have discipline among
its members and each member
must set an example for the in-
mate by being neat in, personal
appearance and living habits and
not using profane language.
The regulations prohibit smok-
ing while supervising the boys,
prohibit alcoholic beverages or
firearms on the school grounds.
The staff cannot buy any clothing
or personal belongings of a boy or
accept any gifts from the boys or
their relatives, nor shall a staff
member wear any article of State
clothing.
No staff members shall be absent
from duty without written permis-
sion signed by the head of his de
partment and approved by the
superintendent or his assistant, nor
punish à boy by force or corporal
means unless with special permis-
sion and under personal supervi
sion of the superintendent or his
assistant.
No staff member can permit a boy
to leave the school grounds with-
out permission of the superinten-
dent or his assistant, and each mem
ber shall be held to a strict account-
ing of the boys assigned to him.
Mr. Sprague appointed a finance
committee to investigate expendi
tures of the school. Members are
Joseph P. Healy, J. Marshall Stew-
art, Hal B. Clagett, Carroll H. Hyn-
son and Joseph H. Neal.
