Efforts to have the State take over
control of the House of Reformation
at Cheltenham were renewed yester-
day with the appointment of a com-
mittee of two to effect an agreement
between the board of directors of the
institution and the Board of State Aid
and Charities.
The action was taken after Enoch
Harlan, vice-president and acting
president of the Reformatory for
Negro Boys, asked the Board of State
Aid and Charities to include in its
budget funds for certain repairs and
additions at Cheltenham.
Conditions Called Deplorable
After a discussion, in which Gov-
ernor Nice and members of the board
criticized conditions at Cheltenham
as "deplorable," Samuel E. Shanna-
han, chairman of the board, appointed
Morris A. Rome, a member of the
board, and Mr. Harlan to act as a
committee to "see what could be done
toward having the State take control"
of the institution.
In addition to Mr. Harlan, repre-
sentatives of sixteen other State-aided
institutions were given hearings yes-
terday on their requests for 1937-38
and 1938-39 allotments.
Governor Nice recalled that the
board of directors of Cheltenham two
years ago "gave him to understand"
that they favored the State taking con-
trol of the institution, but that this
stand later was reversed and as a
result the Legislature failed to take
Retion. The Governor asked Mr. Harian
whether he could explain the present
view of the board.
Race Question Raised
Mr. Harlan replied that the majority
of the board was opposed to the change
because of the possibility that the in-
stitution would be placed under Negro
management. Governor Nice replied
that he had pledged himself to main-
taining an equal balance between
Negro and white officials and attend-
ants.
"I am sure," the Governor said, "that
the Negro population of the State is
not concerned so much with who is in
control as to the improvement of con-
ditions at Cheltenham.
"There have been a series of reports
from grand juries and committees and
other investigating bodies, all criti
cizing conditions at Cheltenham. 1
have come to the conclusion that the
institution is a blot on the good name
of the State."
