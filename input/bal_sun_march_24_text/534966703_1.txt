Negro Stole Woman's Pocketbook
In Department Store,
Guy Jones, colored, convicted in the
Juvenile Court yesterday of picking the
pocket of Miss Lillian Cohen, 259 South
Bond street, last Saturday, was sen-
tenced to the Colored House of Reforma-
tion at Cheltenham by Judge Carmody.
Jones will be 15 years old on April 30
next.
The testimony showed that he picked
Miss Cohen's pocket in a department
store. A store detective saw him follow
Miss Cohen when she entered the store
and saw him take her pocketbook. When
accused of the theft, Jones dropped the
pocketbook on the floor and said he had
found it there.
Jones lived at 1030 Booth street, and
it was not his first appearance in the
Juvenile Court.

street. Soon the patiol of the North-

Automobilist Fined $10.
A.L. Katzenberg, of 1804 Linden ave-
une, who was one of the 21 automobilists
caught in the trap laid for them last
Sunday at Brooklyn, appeared last night
before Justice Fowler and was fined $10
and costs for exceeding the speed limit.
All of the automobilists had been sum-
moned to appear for a hearing tomorrow
night.
