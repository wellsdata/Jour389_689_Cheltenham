Mr. Mudd for two years, and came from the
colored House of Reformation, at Cheltenham,
about six miles from Upper Marlboro'. The
alarm was given and the whole county turned
out to hunt the criminal. The search contin-
ned all night, and on Monday morning he was
found by two colored men and brought to Mr.
Mudd. The enraged father, it is stated, drew
a knife and would have killed him, but was
prevented by his wife and a number of others.
Some of the mon present went for a constable
in the immediate neighborhood, and the boy
was taken to Port Tobacco and lodged in jail.
The colored people, it is said, would have hung
