The Governor, anticipating the
transfer of the present location of

culinary unit.
The building will fit in with the
program for defectives, the mental
hygiene board said.
'Junior Class' Of 1904
At City Meet Again
Thirty of the original 80 mem-
bers of the "junior class" of 1904
of City College, some of them from
New York and Philadelphia, last
night held their 16th annual re-
union at the Southern Hotel.
The junior class was so called
because its members were gradu-
ates of the four-year curriculum,
then an innovation, while other
graduates in the same year had at-
tended five years of school.
Among those present were Wal-
ter N. Kirkman, State Budget Di-
rector; Walter Huff, class president,
now an official of the Railway Ex-
press Agency in Philadelphia; Guy
H. Reese, class treasurer, a Balti-
more broker, and Thomas M. Bart-
lett, former football star, now at-
torney for the Maryland Casualty
Company.
