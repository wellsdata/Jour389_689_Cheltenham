Negroes, 13 And 15, Given 18

[.Annapolis Bureast of The Sun.)
Annapolis, April 10.-Recommenda
tions for the parole of two Negro boys
13 and 15 years old. who were sen
tenced to the House of Correction las
March from St. Mary's county. have
been received by Governor Ritchie
from Edward M. Parrish, State Parol
Commissioner.
The recommendation followed an in
vestigation by Mr. Parrish which Gov
Fernor Ritchie ordered last week after
be had been informed by Stuart S.
Janney, Director of the Department
of Welfare, that boys were sergin
terms in the prison for larceny and
forgery.
To Rest With Ritente.
Final disposition of the cases will
depend on the Governor. In view of
his action in starting the investiga-
tion, however, it is expected he will
favor release.
The boys-Clarence Jones and Had-
man.Holt-were sentenced to eighteen
months and one year in the House of
Correction, respectively, by Judge Wil-
liam M. Loker, of the Seventh Judicial
Circuit. Jones, 13, was convicted of
forging a check for $14 while Holt
was accused of stealing an automo
bile.
Judge Loker, when informed last
weck that the Governor had initiated
an investigation, remarked:
"I have no comment to make on the
subject. Governor Ritchie mas settle
the questions he is concerned with and
I will settle mine."
"Apt Reproduction."
Philip H. Dorsey, State's Attorney
for St. Mary's county, said the forgery
of which Jones was convicted was an
"apt reproduction" of the signature of
the man upon whom the check was
drawn.
In asking Mr. Parrish to investigate,
Governor Ritchie pointed out that in
view of the extreme, youth of the boys
he did not believe they should have
been sentenced to the House of Corree-
tion. He suggested either a parole or
their transfer to the Cheltenham Re-
formatory.
