A Move To End
Controversy At Cheltenham
The decision of the board of managers
of the Cheltenham School for Boys to
give the institution a full Negro staff is
obviously designed to end the contro-
versy occasioned by the board's earlier.
action ending racial segregation among
employés. It looks, therefore, in the
right direction.
To carry out the board's plan, however,
will take time. The filling of vacancies
in a staff of a school like Cheltenham is
difficult at best, if qualified personnel is
to be obtained. It is particularly so
now, when teachers and institution
managers find it so easy and so attrac-
tive to accept positions in private indus-
try and in the Federal Government. The
board recognizes this problem when it
says that the all-Negro staff will come
"as soon as practicable."
Those who realize that the inmates at
Cheltenham are wards of the State and
that controversy and criticism cannot
help but be detrimental to the housing,
feeding and education of those wards,
will hope that the board can carry out
its newly announced policy without un-
due delay. And they will hope that the
introduction of a full Negro staff will
remove all causes of complaint and fric-
tion and place the Negro institution on
the sound footing which the public
interest demands.
In the meantime the board's action
should end racial friction and permit the
school to push its program to the advan-
tage of those intrusted to its care.
