CHELTENHAM POST
ELIGIBLES NAMED
Former Sing Sing Official
One Of 3 Seeking Office Of
Education Supervisor
The name of a former educational
director at Sing Sing prison, New
York, was included yesterday in the
list of eligibles for appointment as
supervisor of education at the Chel-
tenham School for Boys, a State in-
stitution for Negroes.
The list was announced by Harry C.
Jones, State Employment Commis-
sioner, and was transmitted to Joseph
Blanford, Cheltenham superintendent.
Mr. Jones said he had received no in-
formation on the probable date of the
appointment.
/
Three Are Certified
Those certified were:
MELVIN H. KEMPTON, 30, of Yonkers,
N. Y., director of education at
Sing Sing until July of this year,
when the post was eliminated.
CLAUDE D. JONES, 56, of Washington,
former superintendent of the Na-
tional Training School for Boys at
Washington.
MARTIN J. BRENNAN, 39, head of the
industrial arts department at
Hampton Institute, Hampton, Va.
The position of supervisor.of educa-
tion, recently created at the suggestion
of an advisory board studying condi-
tions at the school, pays $2,400 a year
and maintenance.
400 Boys At School
Approximately 400 boys are quar-
tered at the school, and the new super-
visor will have charge of the entire
educational program, assisted by two
elementary teachers.
Two exmainations were given to
create the eligible list. Kempton, Jones
and Brennan took the first examina-
tion and passed. Seven others took the
second test, but none passed. The
State's Employment
certified the first list.
