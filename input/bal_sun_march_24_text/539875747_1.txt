Governor O'Conor announced last
night that the Board of Public Works
la, planning to withhold the sale of
$1,458,000 of construction bonds, re-
mainder of an issue approved by the
last session of the Legislature.
Approximately one third of the issue,
amounting to $859,000, was sold last
July 15 at the rate of .098 per cent.
interest. The General Assembly au-
thorized a total issue of $2,317,000.
Reason For Action
The Governor explained that the
proposed action by the boardis
prompted by the present high cost of
construction and by the scarcity of
essential materials.
Within the last few days the Office
of Production Management has ad-
vised State, county and municipal of-
ficials that they will be budgeted next
year for the purchase of iron and
steel products and other commodities
needed for national defense.
In fact, Maryland has been selected
as one of three States where OPM will..
conduct a survey to determine the pro
portionate amount of strategic mate-
rials required by each State to main-
tain hospitals, water mains, sewers and
other essential public health services.
The other test-tube States are Virginia
and New Jersey,
Equipment Affected
According to Walter N. Kirkman,
State director of budget and procure-
ment, OPM has advised State authori-
ties that the steel restrictions also will
apply to such equipment as typewrit-
ers, Aling cabinets, laundry machines,
automobiles and motorcycles.
Baltimore, together with Chester,
Pa., and Bridgeport, Conn., also will
be used by OPM to determine future
municipal requirements for essential
products.
Governor O'Conor explained last
night that money obtained last
summer now is being used for the
construction of a new hospital wing
at the Maryland Tuberculosis Sana-
torium and for several other projects.
Loan Authorization.
According to the terms of the bond
issue, as approved by the Legislature,
the State was authorized to borrow
$859,000 Inst July, $807,000 on January
15, 1942, and $651,000 on July 15, 1942. -
Should the Board of Public Works
finally decide to forego the other two
bond issues, the State will have to
defer a number of construction and
improvement plans provided for when
the bond issue was authorized.

In addition to the hospital wing,

approved the construction of a new

of a new power plant at Morgan State
College.

Several Proposals
With the additional funds the State
would have erected two chronic dis-
ease hospitals, at the total cost of
$1,500,000, one to be located on, the
Eastern Shore and the other on this
side of the bay.
The original bill proposed an appro-
priation of $250,000 for a Health De-
partment building and laboratories to
replace the present departmental
headquarters at 2400 North Charles
street.
An allotment of $225,000 had been
set aside for the addition to the Com-
missioner of Motor Vehicles' office at
Guilford avenue and Twenty-first
street.
But these projects were eliminated
at the request of the Governor when
he advised the Legislature "that the
material and labor which might be
necessary for the construction of these
buildings might be sorely needed dur-
ing the next year or two."
