TWO STRIKERS FACE
ASSAULT CHARGE
Women Arrested After Al-
leged Tussle With Rain-
coat Plant Worker
POLICE BREAK UP FIGHT
Pickets Remain In Front Of
Hanover Street Shop-Union
Complains Of Wage Cot
Two striking employes of a raincoat
company in the first block South Han-
over street, were arrested yesterday
afternoon after a brief tussle with a
woman worker leaving the plant.
The fight was broken up by police,
and the employes, who had been pick-
eting the plant with about thirty fel-
low-strikers, were charged with as-
saulting Mrs. Genevieve McDevitt,
1316 West Lombard street.
Those arrested and later released in
$26 collateral for a hearing this after-
noon, were Mrs. Louise Watts, 27, of
the 300 block North Fulton avenue,
and Miss Angela Banebace, 800 block
Hamilton Terrace. Mrs. Watts was
also charged with assaulting Miss
Mary Brock, 4308 Parkwood avenue.
Pickets Remain At Plant
Carrying signs, the pickets walked
in front of the plant for about an hour
after their companions had been taken
into custody, but no further disturb-
ances were reported.
Officials of the Garment Workers'
Union said the employes had struck
ast Thursday because the company
old them they would receive a see-
nd cut in wages and their hours would
be increased to forty-four a week.
A company official, however, said
he employes had been asked to work
orty-two and a half hours a week
and were told they would be paid on a
piece-work rate for overtime. They
were told, he said, that wages would
not be cut, but that the NRA minimum
would be maintained.
Offices Of Cheltenham
Colored School Picketed
Picketing yesterday in front of the
local offices of the House of Reforma-
tion for Colored Boys at Cheltenham,
225 East Redwood street, failed to have
any apparent effect on the office.
About half a dozen pickets were in
front of the place for an hour or two
carrying placards urging the abolition
of the school.
