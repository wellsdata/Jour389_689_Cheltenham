SPRAGUE DEFENDS
DISMISSAL OF KIAH
Says Action Of Old Chelten-
ham Board Was Unanimous
Thomas B. Sprague, president of
the former board of managers of
the Cheltenham School for Boys,
yesterday reiterated his statement
that the board was unanimous in its
action leading to dismissal of Cal-
vin L. Kiah as superintendent of
the institution.
"This action of the board was
unanimous," Mr. Sprague said. "By
that I mean it was unanimous with
regard to the.six of the nine mem-
bers present."
Mr. Sprague made his statement
after Dr. R. L. Jackson, Negro mem-
ber of the recently resigned board,
criticized the board for dismissing
Mr. Kiah.
Final Act Of Old Board
The dismissal of Mr. Kiah, after
a six-month probationary period,
was the final official act of the re-
tiring board.
In his statement Dr. Jackson
charged that, himself, J. Marshall
Stewart and J. Grayson Luttrell
were absent from the meeting and
that Carroll Hynson, of Annapolis,
another board member, asked to be
excused from voting on the dismis-
sal of Mr. Kiah.
Dr. Jackson also charged that
imeetings were held at irregular
hours, and that it was impossible
to know the time and place of board
meetings.
Lane To Meet With Board
Mr. Sprague, in commenting on
this criticism, stated that the
board meeting of February 24 was
set on January 26, and that mem-
bers of the board were notified in
plenty of time for the meeting, al-
though no time was set.
Mr. Sprague also said that due
to his work along with two other
members of the board serving
grand jury duty, it was impossible
to hold the meeting on February
24 before 3 P.M.
It was after the February 24
meeting that Mr. Kiah was notified
of his dismissal.
Governor Lane will meet with
the newly appointed nine-member
all Negro board at an organization
meeting at 3 P.M. today at the
State House.
