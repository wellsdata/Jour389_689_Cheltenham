MAGISTRATES AT CHELTENHAM
Police Justices Pleased With Negro
School Management.
Eight police magistrates went to Chel-
tenham, Prince George's county, on a tour
of inspection of the House of Refuge for
Colored Boys yesterday and, although
they had two automobiles and the magis-
trates were in a hurry to get back to their
suppers, there was no one arrested for
speeding.
1
The tourists were Justices IHenry, Say-)
ler, Dean, Golden, McFaul, Levinson, Ul-
rich and Supplee. They were greatly im
pressed with the management, location and
results of the colored school, which they
are convinced is worthy of the support of
the community. An exhibition drill was
given for the visitors and they inspected
the hospital, six dormitories and admin-
istration buildings thoroughly.
The honor system, they say, is a great
success. In spite of the fact that there
are no walls about the buildings, it is
rare for a boy to break his word and try
to escape.

Ernest Schwerdscharf Wanted.
Marshal Farnan is trying to find Ernest
Schwerdscharf, 32 years old, a gardener,
formerly of Kolomea, Europe, whose par-
ènts are ill there. He is believed to be in
Baltimore and the Marshal is anxious to
inform him of the illness of his parents
in order that he may communicate with
them.
