Edward Stabler, Jr., Dies
Following Long Illness
Founder. Of Coal- Company Which
Bore His Name Succumbs At
Age Of 88.
Edward Stabler, Jr., founder of the
coal company which for many years
bore his, name, and associated actively
with many of Baltimore's cultural in-
stitutions, died yesterday at his home,
610 Reservoir street. He had been in
failing health for several months. Mr.
Stabler was 88 years old.
Born in Sandy Spring, Montgomery
county, Mr. Stabler had lived here
nearly 70 years. He was engaged in
the retail coal trade for 50 years and
took an active part in the Baltimore
Coal Exchange.
Mr. Stabler was a lifelong member
of the Society of Friends and for years
served as secretary of their annual
meetings. He also acted as secretary
of the Maryland Academy of Sciences
and the House of Reformation (for
negroes) at Cheltenham, Md. He was
trustee of the Enoch Pratt Free Li-
brary, the Maryland Historical Society
and was active in the Baltimore Re
form League.
He is survived by a daughter, Mrs.
Emery H. Miller, of York, Pa., and two
sons, E. Alford Stabler, of Baltimore,
and Raymond Stabler, now living in
California. Funeral services will be
held at 2 P. M. tomorrow at the home.
YOUTH, 16, IS SENTENCED
John Morgan Put In Institution
Until He Becomes Of Age
John Morgan, 16 years old, who gave
his address as Lutherville, was com-
mitted to the Maryland Training School
for Boys yesterday by Magistrate
Schroeder, in the Northern Police
Court.
Morgan was, arrested: Saturday night
for being drunk and disorderly... A pint
of whisky was found on his person.
The police say he is incorrigible, and
he will remain at the school until his
twenty-first birthday...
