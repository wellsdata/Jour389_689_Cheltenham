JONES TEST SLATED HERE
Cheltenham Salary Case Referred
To Baltimore Court
Upper Marlboro, Md., June 21 (AP)-
A court test in Baltimore appeared
today to be the next step in legal
action to force Harry C. Jones, State
Employment Commissioner, to pay the
salaries of twenty-four employes of
the Cheltenham School for Boys.
The initial proceedings contesting
Jones' authority to withhold salaries
from Cheltenham employes he held
"unfit for State labor" were dismissed
yesterday by the Prince George's
County Circuit Court. The court held
State officers should be sued in the
jurisdiction in which their offices are
located.
