NEGRO SCHOOL
SITE PROPOSED
Baker Estate Suggested For
Cheltenham Location
Governor O'Conor announced
yesterday that Ingleside, the old
Bernard N. Baker estate in Catons-
ville, has been recommended as the
site for a new training school for
Negro boys.
The State Board of Public Wel
fare and the board of managers of
Cheitennam School for Boys recom
mended the 100-acre tract after
making a joint study of prospective
sites. The lot is located south of
Johnnycake road. west of Ingleside
avenue, and adjoins Douglass Park,
a Negro ridential section.
Option Obtained
A 90-day option has been ob
tained on the site, which must be
approved by the Board of Public
Works before further action is
taken. Governor O'Conor said.
W. W. Lanahan, chairman of the
State Board of Public Welfare and
F. Reid Isaac, president of the
board of managers of Cheltenham..
submitted the recommendation to
the Governor,
Has Zoning Board Okay
Their report to the Governor
also included a letter from the
Zoning Committee of Baltimore
county approving the location of
the State institution on the Catons-
ville site, which is priced at $650
per acre.
The selection of a site for the
erection of a State training school
for Negro boys is the first step in
carrying out the law enacted in
1943 providing for the separation
of normal and defective Negro
boys. Both are now cared for at the
Cheltenham School for Boys in
Prince George's county.
New Building Called For
The State legislation provided
for the construction of a new in-
Estitution for normal delinquents,
under the jurisdiction of the De
partment of Public Welfare, and
the utilization of the present build-
ings for defective juveniles.
If and when the Catonsville site
is approved by the Board of Public
Works, the Board of Public Wel-
fare and the board of managers of
Cheltenham will be advised to
hegin a study of buildings for the
new institution, Governor O'Conor
said.
