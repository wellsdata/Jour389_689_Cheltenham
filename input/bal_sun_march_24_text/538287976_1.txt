CHELTENHAM
It has long been the belief of those best
able to pass judgment on the matter that
Cheltenham should be taken over by the
State. It is a privately owned institution for
the reformation of colored boys of 10 to 21
years of age, the management of which has
come under frequent criticism. It has been
in receipt of $20,000 a year from the State,
and the city contributes to it approximately
$60,000 a year for support of boys sent there
by the city. Those sent from the counties are
charged $180 a year.
At best, wise direction of the affairs of
Cheltenham is a difficult job and under the
present arrangement dissatisfaction with the
way they are handled could not easily be
avoided. The best solution of the problem is
for the State to assume control of it. The
reported assent of the managers of the insti-
tution should eliminate the remaining obstacle |
to that procedure.
