Three Deficiencies Noted
Made at the request of Mr.
Sprague, Mr. Reidt's report listed
recent improvements at the insti-
tution and listed three outstanding
deficiencies to be corrected in the
future:
1. The eficiency of the staff at
the school is "very low." Many em-
ployés are only temporary and do
not have the listed qualifications for
the positions they are holding, al-
though the salaries pald and pre-
requisites for employment are good.
A recruiting campaign for good
personnel should be inaugurated.
2. "A careful survey should be
undertaken as to maintenance and
some better utilization of existing
facilities be made until such time
as additional facilities can be pro-
vided. No additional living quarters
should be bullt, however, that pro-
vide for unit cooking and eating by
the staff."
Habits Of Cleanliness
3. "It cannot be too strongly
Impressed upon the staff that good
eitizenship begins with habits of
cleanliness and that, unless this
fact is constantly brought home
to the boys through teaching and
inspection, they will be slovenly at
the school and upon their return
to the community. Much remains
to be done in this connection."
The report recommended that
the State Board of Welfare clearly
define the responsibilities of the
State board, the board of managers
and the superintendent of the insti-
tutions and define minimum stand.
ards of maintenance for the staffs
and a diet for the children.
Periodic Reports Called For
It also recommended that the
board of managers be given
periodic reports showing the situa-
tion of the school as viewed by the
supervisor of training schools, and
that personnel qualifications and
practices be standardized for the
four State training schools.
Since there may be many of the
present staff qualified for positions
different from those they now hold.
the report recommended a survey
to see what positions they should
hold regardless of their present
classifications. New members should
be screened carefully.
White Members' Situation
In reference to the board of man-
agers, the report stated:
"The white members of the
hoard have earnestly tried to un-
derstand and appreciate the prob-
lems and viewpoints of the staff.
"Little opportunity is afforded,
however, for confidences between
the white board members and the
staff, and, indeed, there may pos-
sibly be some resentment against a
staff member by his fellow em-
ployés who attempted to establish
such contacts.
"On the other hand, colored
board members may meet staff
members at church and social fune-
tions and establish pleasant rela-
Honships that can be most helpful.
"Helpfulness" Seen As Past
"However, there is respect on the
part of the white board members
for those staff members who are
striving to do a creditable job.
"I believe that it has been help-
ful to the school to have had white
board members in the past who
might assist in interpreting its pros
gram to the entire community, but
this advantage is no longer neces-
sary.
"A most important function of
the board at the present time is
the recruiting of a proper staff.
"I know that a large number of
colored organizations and agencies
have exhibited a new interest in
this school and its needs and that
there are available excellent col-
ored people who would be willing
willing
to work earnestly and intelligently
on this problem.
Girls School Board Cited
"After a full consideration of all
the many factors involved, it is my
opinion that the best Interests of
The institution and its charges will
be served by setting up an all-
colored board of managers.
""There are no white members
on the staff at the present time
and there is no real reason for
mixed board. The Girls School at
Glen Barnte has an all-colored
board.
"An all-colored board is likely
to hold the staff to a more strict
accountability than a mixed board,
It is my firm conviction that such
a board, wisely chosen, will not
tolerate some of the conditions
that have existed in the past at
the school."
