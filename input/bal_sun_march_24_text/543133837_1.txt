State Senator Raymond E. Kennedy
(Dem., Fifth Baltimore) yesterday
recommended to Governor Nice that
the Maryland Training School for
Boys at Loch Raven be used as a
guide for the conduct of the Chelten-
ham Reform School for Negro Boys.
The Cheltenham institution recently
came in for criticism, when Harry C.
Jones, State Employment Commission-
er, said a number of the employes were
not fitted to hold their jobs, and de-
plored the practice of allowing mem-
bers of the board of managers to be
paid employes of the school as well.
