Urges State Run
Delinquent Home
- Board Will Ask Assembly To Take
Over Negro Boys'
Institution
Efforts to have the State take over
control of the Cheltenham Colored
House for Reformation will be re-
newed at the next session of the legis-
lature, Samuel E. Shannahan, chair-
man of the Board of State Aid and
Charities, said yesterday.
The matter came up for discussion
yesterday at a meeting of the Chil-
dren's Committee of the Board with
about forty representatives of various
civic, religious and social organiza-
tions headed by the Citizen's Com-
mittee for Delinquent Colored Boys.
Mr. Shannahan said he had been in-
formed that the proposed legislation
already had been drawn up and would
be supported by the various organiza-
tions at the meeting. A similar bill
was defeated in the Assembly last
year.
Church Cornerstone
To Be Laid Tomorrow
Signatures Of Members And
Friends To Be Placed in Block
At Salem Lutheran
Signatures of members and friends
of the congregation of Salem Evangeli-
cal Lutheran Church will be placed
in the church cornerstone when it is
laid at 3 P. M. tomorrow.
The new building, which will in-
clude a church school and will cost
$40,000. will be at Battery avenue and
Randall street. It is expected to be
completed and dedicated May 16.
The Rev. H. V. Krug, pastor, will
officiate at the cornerstone laying. The
Rev. Dr. Foster U. Gift, pastor of the
Lutheran Deaconess Home, will de-
liver the address.
