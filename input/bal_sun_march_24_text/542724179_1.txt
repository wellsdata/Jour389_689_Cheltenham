CHELTENHAM
BARN BURNS;
BOY BLAMED
12-Year-Old Inmate Ac-
cused Of Starting
$10,000 Blaze
Fire, blamed on a 12-year-old
inmate, early yesterday razed a
storage barn and destroyed farm
equipment for a total loss of more
than $10,000 at the Cheltenham
School for Boys, Prince Georges
county.
It was the last storage barn left
standing at the school, three others
having been destroyed by fire in
the past eighteen months, accord
ing to Thomas B. Sprague, presi
dent of the school's board of man
agers.
Cause Not Determined
Cause of the three previous fires
has not been determined.
Mr. Sprague and Wallace Reidt,
executive secretary of the board,
who together personally investi
gated the blaze, said the boy
admitted under questioning by
State police that he had set the
two-story frame building afire.
School officials said the barn.
which is about 2 city blocks from
the main buildings of the institu-
tion, seemed to burst into flame
throughout the entire structure.
Blaze Spreads Quickly
They attributed the quick ad-
vance of the blaze to fire spreading
to 20 tons of hay, stored in the
building.
Also destroyed were pieces of
field machinery, including harvest-
ing equipment and wheat-binders,
and about 100 iron beds which
were stored in the building.
Called immediately to the scene
the Upper Marlboro Fire Depart-
ment. located 7 miles away, arrived
to find the building completely
enveloped in flames.
Firemen worked to isolate the
fire to prevent its spread through
dry grass to neighboring boring buildings.
Naval Unit Sends Help
Fire equipment from the naval
air station near Upper Marlboro
also responded, Mr. Sprague said.
So rapidly was the building con-
sumed that it was impossible to
form school inmates into fire fight-
ing crews. Mr. Sprague said he was
told by officials at the school.
He said he was first informed of
the fire, which then had been burn-
ing for about 20 minutes, when he
made a telephone call from Balti-
more at 11 A.M. on other school
business.
