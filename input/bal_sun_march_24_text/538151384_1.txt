51 PERSONS SEEK
CHELTENHAM POST
Tones Names Special Ex-
aminers To Hold Test To
Fill Superintendency
Employment Commissioner
Hopes To Have Eligible
List This Month
Harry C. Jones, state employment
commissioner, announced he had ap-
pointed a special board of examiners
Lo review applications and prepare and
conduct a test for the superintendency
of the Cheltenham School for Boys.
The examination is necessary to fill
vacancy created by the resignation
of B. B. Sunderland. Mr. Sunderland,
when a new superintendent is named,
will return to his former post as chief
parole officer of the institution.
Advertised Position
Commissioner Jones said the position
had been advertised freely over a
sixty-day period and that institutions
similar to the Southern Maryland
Negro reformatory had been canvassed
for available applicants.
Fifty-one persons subsequently filed
applications.
The examining board which will
handle the reviewing and testing will
consist of Mr. Jones, Dr. Thomas J.
Pullen, assistant State superintendent
of education, and Paul T. Beisser,
executive secretary of the Henry Wat-
son Children's Aid Society and a mem-
ber of the Governor's commission on
juvenile delinquency.
Mr. Jones said he hoped to complete
the examination and compile an eligi-
ble list before the end of the month,
