Youth Sentenced To
Cheltenham For Dam-
aging Synagogue
After he was convicted of being
delinquent because he participated
in acts of vandalism in the Ohr
Knesseth Israel Synagogue last
October 27, a 13-year-old boy
yesterday was sentenced to four
years in the Cheltenham School by
Judge Charles E. Moylan, of the
Division for Juvenile Causes..
Meanwhile, Adolph Foster, 16-
year-old Negro, was held in $2,000
bail pending grand-jury action on a
charge of being 'the other youth
who took part in the acts of van.
dalism, which resulted in damage
estimated at $5,000.
Held For Court Action
Foster was held for court action
by Magistrate James H. Ellis, Jr.,
in Northwestern Police Court, on
charges of twice burglarizing the
synagogue, the first time on Sep-
tember 25 when $45 were stolen
from a poor box.
:
The older youth pleaded guilty
to the charges.
The younger defendant, also a
Negro, lives near the West Frank-
lin street synagogue, Judge Moylan
was told. Foster gave an address in
the 300 block West Biddle street.
Locks Had Been Forced
Although the second unlawful
entry of the synagogue was attrib
uted by the younger defendant to
Halloween pranks, police contend.
ed that a quest for money was the
real motive, pointing out that
locks on drawers and windows had
been broken or forced.
According to Lieut. Louis Vinup.
and Sergt. Joseph Koenig, the
vandals destroyed many sacred reli-
gious papers and desecrated the in-
terior of the building by spreading
scrolls and other religious articles
on the floor and then walking on
them.
Whisky and beer bottles were
found strewn over the building
and some of the liquid contents had
been poured over the religious em-
blems, the police testified before
Judge Moylan.
They also asserted that the van-
dals had broken a sacred pointer,
had moved and overturned the pul
pit, broke and crushed sacred
candles, spread religious robes and
crowns on the floor and crushed
them and poured salt water into
pipes.
