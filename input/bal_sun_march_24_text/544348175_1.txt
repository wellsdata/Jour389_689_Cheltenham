Thomas H. Bowles Dies
After Four-Year Illness
i
First President Of Baltimore Trust
Company Succumbs At
White Plains,
Thomas H. Bowles, first president of
the Baltimore Trust Company and also
first president of the Baltimore Dry
Docks and Shipbuilding Company, died
Tuesday night at White Plains, N. Y.
Four years ago Mr. Bowles' ill health
caused him to resign the posts he then
held in the financial field in Baltimore,
and leave the city in an effort to check
the maladies from which he suffered.
He sold his country home in the
Green Spring Valley, but for several
years spent his winters at his city
home at St. Paul and Monument streets.
He spent last winter in his home on
Somerset road, Roland Park.
Two months ago Mr. Bowles and his
wife left Baltimore for their summer
home at Manchester, Vt., but as Mr.
Bowles' condition grew worse they went
to White Plains.
Born in Fluvanna county, Virginia,
Mr. Bowles, after completing his edu-
cation, selected life insurance as the
field for his endeavor. In 1887 he be-
came Louisiana, and Mississippi man-
ager of the Mutual Life Insurance Com-
pany of New York. He then went to
Milwaukee, whence he came to Balti-
more. He was a member of the Mary-
land Club.
Surviving is a widow, Mrs. Louise
Cunningham Bowles. Burial will be in
Savannah, Ga.
