Verdict Of $779,812 Upheld On Appeal Receivers For Davison
Realty Company Win Suit Against C. Wilbur Miller Attorneys
for the receivers of the Davison Realty Company yesterday
were informed by the rederal Cir- cuit Court of Appeals, at
Charlotte, N. C., that a verdict of $779,812 ob- tained a
year ago against C. Wilbur Miller, former president of the
com- pany, had been affirmed. The sum represents, with
interest, amounts which Mr. Miller is said to have borrowed
from the company on eight promissory notes. The original
verdict, returned in November, 1934, by a jury in the
United States District Court here, was appealed by Mr.
Miller, who con- tended the loans were not personal
obligations, but were for the benefit of the Davison
Chemical Company, of which he then was head. Mr. Miller was
represented by Isaac Lobe Straus and J. Wallace Bryan,
while the re- ceivers' case was presented by Hilary W.

Police-Plans Safety Campaign Major Enoch Barton Garey,
super- intendent of the Maryland State po- lice, disclosed
plans for a highway safety campaign yesterday with a charge
