The course is focusing on the history of the House of Reformation for Colored 
Children at Cheltenham in Prince George’s County.

# Remembering Victims of Cheltenham

Capital News Service is examining the history of the House of Reformation for 
Colored Children at Cheltenham in Prince George’s County where some 230 boys 
died while in state custody up until 1940. This is a case study of a national 
tragedy: the use of the juvenile justice system to extend the mechanisms of 
slavery and Jim Crow well into the 20th century. The articles below represent a 
mix of investigations and ongoing news coverage by Capital News Service 
reporters of the Cheltenham episode and the state of Maryland’s response. We 
plan to release data and documents from our published work as well.

Associate Professor Rob Wells is overseeing this project, and please contact him 
with any questions at robwells@umd.edu.
